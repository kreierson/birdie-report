The **adidas Tour360 24** is the better golf shoe if you actually need spikes.

The **FootJoy Pro/SLX** is the smarter buy only if the recent sale pricing is still real and you want a firmer premium spikeless shoe instead of another soft athletic imitation.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **adidas Tour360 24** if you want the cleaner answer and you already know spikes are the point.

Buy the **FootJoy Pro/SLX** if:

- you want a firmer support-first spikeless shoe
- you do **not** actually need spikes
- and the recent sale price still leaves a meaningful gap between it and the adidas

For most golfers who truly need help in soft or wet conditions, I would buy the **Tour360 24**.

For golfers who keep talking themselves into spikes when what they really want is a more planted spikeless shoe at a saner sale number, I would buy the **Pro/SLX**.

## Why the Tour360 24 Is the Better Pure Golf Answer

The **Tour360 24** solves the problem more honestly.

If you are shopping because:

- you play wet morning golf
- your courses have sidehill lies that make you a little twitchy
- you hate feeling loose underfoot
- you swing hard enough to notice when a shoe loses the plot

then the adidas is the cleaner buy.

The current adidas product page still puts the **Tour360 24** at **$200**, calls the fit **true to size**, and keeps selling the same basic story Birdie Report's review already landed on:

- real spikes
- modern support
- Boost cushioning
- premium bad-weather credibility

That is a coherent reason to spend the money.

If your actual complaint is traction, the answer should probably be the shoe with spikes.

## Why the Pro/SLX Only Make Sense When the Price Is Right

The **Pro/SLX** are interesting because they become much more convincing when FootJoy push them into previous-season sale pricing.

That is the whole twist here.

At full-premium money, the Pro/SLX have to survive a much nastier question:

Why am I paying almost spike money for a shoe that still stops short of being a spike?

At recent sale pricing, the answer gets better:

- firmer support than many soft premium spikeless shoes
- strong waterproofing story
- more planted golf-shoe feel than the looser athletic crowd
- better value if you wanted structure, not hardware

That is why the [FootJoy Pro/SLX review](/blog/footjoy-pro-slx-review/) ended up where it did. The shoe itself is good. The **price is what turns it from niche to smart**.

## Traction: adidas Win Without Needing a Debate

This one is simple.

The **Tour360 24** are the better traction shoe.

Not because the Pro/SLX are bad.

Because spikes are still spikes.

The adidas case is much easier if you regularly play:

- soft turf
- dew-heavy mornings
- spring and fall golf
- uneven lies that make you think about your feet before you swing

The **Pro/SLX** can still be the right buy for a golfer who wants a firmer spikeless platform. They just are not the shoe I would choose if the whole shopping mission started with "I want more grip."

**Edge: adidas Tour360 24**

## Walking and Everyday Use: FootJoy Are Easier to Live With

This is where the **Pro/SLX** get back into the fight.

They are still the easier shoe to own if you:

- walk a lot
- do not want to hear your shoe click on hard surfaces
- prefer premium spikeless versatility
- want support without full spike structure

The **Tour360 24** are more walkable than older spikes because the Boost setup is legit. But they are still the more committed shoe. Heavier. More structured. More golf-only.

If you want the one-pair premium-shoe case, the FootJoy path is easier to defend, especially if you are comparing it mentally against softer spikeless alternatives like [Nike Air Zoom Infinity Tour NEXT%](/blog/footjoy-pro-slx-vs-nike-air-zoom-infinity-tour-next/) or the broader [FootJoy Pro/SL 2026](/blog/footjoy-pro-slx-vs-footjoy-pro-sl-2026/) lane.

**Edge: FootJoy Pro/SLX**

## Value: This Page Turns on the Price Gap

That is the real buyer question.

If the **Pro/SLX** are still sitting far enough below **$200**, they are a compelling answer for the golfer who wants firmer support without paying full current-premium money.

If the gap narrows too much, the adidas start looking much cleaner because:

- the traction story is stronger
- the product identity is clearer
- the compromise is smaller

So the value read is:

- **Pro/SLX** if the sale number is the reason you clicked
- **Tour360 24** if the price gap is small and spikes are genuinely useful to you

That is more helpful than pretending one shoe universally destroys the other.
