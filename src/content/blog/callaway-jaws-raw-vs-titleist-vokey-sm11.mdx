## Quick Verdict

This is not a fair fight if you only compare them as wedges.

It gets interesting when you compare them as **purchases**.

Buy the **Callaway Jaws Raw** if:

- you want the cheaper older-premium value lane
- you are comfortable shopping specific loft and grind inventory
- you care more about getting strong traditional-wedge performance at a discount than buying the newest thing

Buy the **Titleist Vokey SM11** if:

- you want the cleanest premium buying path
- you value current custom-order support and the deeper fitting matrix
- you already know your wedge preferences well enough to use what the Vokey ecosystem offers

For most golfers spending their own money, the **Jaws Raw** is the sharper **value** buy.

For the golfer who wants the safest **premium** answer and does not care about paying more, the **SM11** is still the better overall wedge family.

## Why This Buyer Fork Exists

The **Jaws Raw** and **SM11** do not show up in the same shortlist for the obvious reason.

The Jaws Raw is now an **older-model clearance-style** wedge decision.

The SM11 is the **current premium benchmark**.

So the real question is not "which one is newer?" You already know that.

The real question is:

**How much do you value a cheaper traditional wedge with real spin tech versus a full-price premium wedge with the deepest mainstream fitting story?**

That is a real buying decision.

This is where the Jaws Raw becomes dangerous in a good way.

- **Callaway Jaws Raw Face Chrome** on Callaway Golf Pre-Owned: **$53.66 to $133.88**

That is not a small gap.

That is the difference between:

- replacing one wedge

If you need multiple wedges, price is not a side note. It is the entire story.

## The Vokey SM11 Is the Better Current Wedge Family

Let us be fair to Titleist.

The **SM11** is still the more complete current premium package.

Titleist's official product page currently gives you:

- **27 configurations**
- **six grinds**
- **44 to 60 degree** loft coverage in the current lineup
- the **Vokey Spin System**
- unified CG placement across grinds within a loft
- the cleanest mainstream premium wedge ecosystem in golf

If you already know what you want from turf interaction, launch window, and sole shape, the Vokey matrix is genuinely useful.

That is why the full [Titleist Vokey SM11 review](/blog/titleist-vokey-sm11-review/) still ends with SM11 as the premium benchmark.

## The Jaws Raw Is the More Interesting Value Wedge

The **Jaws Raw** does not beat the **SM11** by being newer or more refined.

It beats it by making you ask a brutally practical question:

**Do I really need the extra Vokey ecosystem badly enough to pay that much more per wedge?**

Because the Jaws Raw still brings plenty:

- raw face
- Groove-in-Groove texture on scoring lofts
- tungsten in sand and lob wedges
- variable-length hosels
- **48 to 62 degree** loft range
- **C, S, W, X, and Z grinds**

That is not compromised junk. It is just an older premium wedge family with a much friendlier entry price now.

## Which One Is Easier to Buy Correctly?

This depends on what kind of difficulty annoys you more.

### The SM11 is easier if:

- you want to buy **new**
- you want current Titleist support
- you are comfortable inside a bigger fitting matrix
- you like the security of knowing the exact finish and configuration are built around the current product line

### The Jaws Raw is easier if:

- you know the loft and grind you want already
- you care more about price than model-year freshness
- you are happy to let inventory availability steer the final choice a little

That is the key distinction.

The Vokey is the easier **premium** buy.

The Jaws Raw is the easier **value** buy.
