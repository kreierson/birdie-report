## Setup and Use-Case Fit

This is the most useful nuance in the whole comparison.

### Choose the SC4 Pro if you care about:

- quick setup
- more self-contained range use
- less app dependence outdoors
- a product that feels practical instead of ceremonial

Voice Caddie's current guidance is simple: keep the unit level with the hitting surface, place it **about 5 feet behind the ball**, and if you are hitting into a net or screen, allow about **10 feet** from ball to net.

That is refreshingly normal.

### Choose the KIT if you care about:

- premium radar specifically
- a cleaner no-unlock ownership feel
- integrated HD video
- using one premium unit across a more intentionally premium indoor-outdoor setup

Full Swing's current space guidance is still more demanding. Outdoors, it wants about **10 feet behind the ball**. Indoors, it wants **8-10 feet behind the ball** and **8-10 feet of ball flight**.

That does not make the KIT bad.

It makes it a more serious-room product.
