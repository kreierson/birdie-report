It is a debate about **which watch is better for golf buyers**.

Because the **Apple Watch Ultra 2** is the better everyday smartwatch.

The **Garmin Approach S50** is the better golf smartwatch for most people actually shopping because of golf.

That is the real **Garmin Approach S50 vs Apple Watch Ultra 2** split.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Garmin Approach S50** if you are buying a watch **because of golf**.

Buy the **Apple Watch Ultra 2** if you already want the best Apple smartwatch and golf is just one more thing you need it to do.

For most golfers making this exact search before buying, I would recommend the **S50** first.

For people who already live deep in Apple's ecosystem and care more about an all-day smartwatch than a golf-first device, I would recommend the **Apple Watch Ultra 2**.

## Why the S50 Is the Better Golf Purchase

The **S50** wins this page because the golf part is already built in.

You are not adding golf later.

You are not choosing between app subscriptions.

You are not asking your smartwatch to cosplay as a golf watch.

The **S50** already gives you:

- **43,000-plus** courses
- **hazard view**
- **green view**
- **PlaysLike distance**
- golf-first menus and screen flow
- a battery setup designed around being a golf watch on purpose

That matters.

It is the same reason Garmin's dedicated watches keep making more sense than app-dependent paths in the site's GPS coverage, even when Apple stays the nicer general watch.

## Where Apple Watch Ultra 2 Still Has a Real Case

Apple's argument is not subtle.

The **Ultra 2** is the better watch for basically everything that is not golf-specific.

That includes:

- the broader app ecosystem
- better iPhone integration
- stronger everyday smartwatch value
- a better "one premium watch for normal life" story

If your buying logic starts with:

"I already want an Apple Watch Ultra 2, and I also golf,"

then this comparison gets tighter immediately.

Because then you are not really buying a golf watch.

You are buying a premium smartwatch and checking whether the golf side is good enough.

## The Golf-App Layer Is the Whole Apple Problem

This is where the **Ultra 2** loses ground for golf buyers.

Apple does not solve the golf job natively the way Garmin does.

It solves the golf job through apps.

That can be fine.

It can even be pretty good.

But it still means:

- more setup decisions
- more reliance on a third-party app you may or may not love
- possible subscription cost on top of an already expensive watch
- a less golf-specific workflow than a dedicated Garmin

That is why I would not recommend the **Ultra 2** first to a golfer who is shopping mainly for golf.

It is a good workaround.

The **S50** is the cleaner product fit.

## Everyday Wearability: Apple Wins, Obviously

The **Apple Watch Ultra 2** is the better everyday watch.

That is not controversial.

The **S50** is a good middle-tier golf smartwatch that also does a respectable job off the course. But it is not trying to beat Apple at being Apple.

So the clean tradeoff is:

- **S50** for the better golf watch
- **Ultra 2** for the better everything-else watch

If you need one watch to anchor your whole phone-notification, workout, payment, and general-life routine, Apple keeps the stronger case.

## The Ownership Math Favors Garmin for Golfers

This part matters more than the spec sheet.

The **S50** costs about **$399.99**.

The **Apple Watch Ultra 2** sits around **$799** before you even get into the golf-app side of the equation.

So if golf is the main reason you are shopping, Apple is asking you to pay roughly **double** for the watch that still needs extra help to be the golf solution.

That is not impossible to justify.

It is just a much narrower buyer case.

The **Apple Watch Ultra 2** is the better smartwatch.

If you are buying specifically for golf and want the cleaner recommendation, buy the **S50**.
