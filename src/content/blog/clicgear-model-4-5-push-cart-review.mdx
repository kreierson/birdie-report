The **Clicgear Model 4.5** is the push cart for golfers who hate buying the same thing twice.

It is not trying to be the lightest cart. It is not trying to win the cutest parking-lot demo. It is built around a much simpler pitch: spend real money once, get years of walking confidence, and stop wondering whether you should have bought the sturdier one.

This review is research-based and built from Clicgear's current official product page, current pricing as of **April 20, 2026**, and the broader pattern of golfer feedback around Clicgear's durability-first reputation. No fake "I pushed it for 54 holes this weekend" nonsense.

## Quick Verdict

The **Clicgear Model 4.5** is one of the safest premium push-cart buys in golf if your priorities are:

- long-term durability
- a stronger accessory ecosystem
- better built-in storage than the old 4.0 era
- a hand-brake setup instead of the usual foot-brake compromise

If your main goal is the easiest daily setup, the [Bag Boy Nitron review](/blog/bag-boy-nitron-push-cart-review/) still makes a strong case. If you want the wider shopping context first, start with [Best Golf Push Carts 2026](/blog/best-golf-push-carts-2026/) and the newer [Best Premium Golf Push Carts 2026](/blog/best-premium-golf-push-carts-2026/).

## What Changed on the 4.5

Clicgear did not reinvent the product. That is a good thing.

The company kept the core identity that made the older 4.0 family popular, then tightened the parts that actually needed work. On the official 4.5 page, the upgrades are pretty direct:

- redesigned command-center console with a phone holder, charging-cable access, magnetic and hook-and-loop storage pads, and a three-ball holder
- new storage bag and mesh basket
- upgraded adjustable drink holder
- internally routed brake cable

That is the correct kind of update.

None of it is fake innovation. It is just Clicgear admitting that premium push carts need to feel good every round, not just durable in theory.

## Durability Is Still the Real Selling Point

This is why people buy Clicgear.

The **Model 4.5** still leans hard into a heavy-duty frame, stable rolling feel, and the general aura of "this thing is going to outlive your current driver." Clicgear even positions it as the flagship cart built to go the distance for hundreds of rounds.

That matters because premium push carts are not impulse junk. At this price, golfers are buying for years, not months.

The **Model 4.5** makes the strongest case for golfers who think like this:

- I walk a lot
- I do not want wobble, flex, or cheap-feeling plastic
- I would rather accept more weight than more doubt

That is not every buyer. It is a very real one.

## Storage and Console: Better Than Older Clicgear Generations

Older Clicgear carts had the reputation everyone remembers:

- rock solid
- feature-rich once accessorized
- a little less slick out of the box

The **4.5** is clearly trying to smooth that third part out.

The command-center redesign matters because golfers use the console every hole. A cart can be durable as hell and still annoy you if the daily touchpoints feel clumsy. The new phone holder, charging access, storage pads, and basket upgrades make the cart feel more current without turning it into a gimmick cart.

That closes the usability gap a bit with convenience-forward options like the [Bag Boy Nitron](/blog/bag-boy-nitron-push-cart-review/).

## Brake and Walking Confidence

The **front-wheel hand brake** is still one of the better reasons to pay Clicgear money.

Handle-based braking is just nicer to live with than stomping at a rear-wheel foot brake every time the course gets hilly or you stop next to a green. The new internally routed brake cable also cleans up one of the more obvious wear-and-aesthetics issues from older designs.

This is the kind of detail golfers dismiss until they own the thing for two seasons.

Then they care a lot.

## Weight and Portability: The Tradeoff You Cannot Ignore

Here is the part where Clicgear stops pretending to be for everyone.

The **Model 4.5** is listed at **21 pounds** and folds down to roughly **13" x 15" x 24"**. Compact enough? Yes. Light? Not really.

That means the cart feels more serious every time you:

- lift it out of the trunk
- load it back in after the round
- carry it around a garage or storage room

Once it is rolling, the weight feels more like stability than punishment. But parking-lot life still counts. Golfers who are older, smaller, or just less interested in lifting a premium tank should take that seriously.

That is where the **Nitron** or even lighter value carts start looking smarter.

## Accessory Ecosystem: Still a Big Reason to Buy

This remains one of Clicgear's best edges.

If you are the type of walker who eventually wants:

- a seat
- extra storage
- cooler add-ons
- travel cover options
- a cart setup that feels more like a platform than a one-off purchase

the **Model 4.5** has a cleaner story than most competitors.

Clicgear does note that some legacy accessories designed around the older umbrella-holder thread are not compatible with the new 4.5 thread. That is annoying, but it is not a dealbreaker unless you are already deeply invested in older add-ons.

For new buyers, the bigger point still wins: the Clicgear ecosystem is deep, mature, and easy to believe in.

## Where the 4.5 Can Lose You

### It is expensive in a very adult way

At **$339.00**, this is not a fun little golf impulse.

You are paying for durability, platform depth, and long-term confidence. If you walk twice a year, that pitch is ridiculous. If you walk twice a week, it starts making a lot more sense.

### It does not feel effortless

The **Model 4.5** feels solid. That is good on the course and less fun in the trunk. Golfers who want a cart that feels easier, faster, and a little more modern day to day may prefer the Nitron.

### It is more "buy the right tool" than "enjoy the trick"

This cart does not have a party trick. It has a resume.

Some golfers love that. Some golfers want the more immediately satisfying ownership experience. That difference is basically the whole [Bag Boy Nitron vs Clicgear Model 4.5](/blog/bag-boy-nitron-vs-clicgear-model-4-5/) debate.

## Who Should Buy the Clicgear Model 4.5

Buy it if:

- you walk often and want one premium cart for the long haul
- you care more about durability than setup theatrics
- you want a better accessory ecosystem and a stronger buy-once story
- you prefer a hand brake and a more planted overall feel

Skip it if:

- you want the easiest setup in the category
- trunk weight bothers you more than long-term confidence
- you are not walking enough to justify a $339 cart
- you want the lightest premium option instead of the sturdiest-feeling one

## Final Verdict

The **Clicgear Model 4.5** is a very good push cart for a very specific buyer.

That buyer is not chasing novelty. That buyer wants the cart that still feels like the smart decision three years later.

If that sounds like you, the **Model 4.5** is one of the better premium purchases in walking golf.
