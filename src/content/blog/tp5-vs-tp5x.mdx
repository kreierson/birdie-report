# TP5 vs TP5x: Which TaylorMade Ball Should You Actually Play?

*Image: TaylorMade Golf*

This is one of those golf-shop questions that sounds simple until you realize the two boxes cost the same and promise slightly different magic.

The clean answer is this:

- **Buy TP5** if you care more about softer feel, a little more wedge spin, and a ball that feels easier to live with round after round.
- **Buy TP5x** if you want the firmer, faster, lower-spinning option off the top of the bag and you don't mind giving up a bit of that softer short-game personality.

Most golfers do not need a 40-minute existential crisis in front of the golf ball rack. They need the right lane.

## The Fast Version

TaylorMade's own current comparison is refreshingly direct:

| | **TP5** | **TP5x** |
|---|---|---|
| **Construction** | 5-layer tour ball | 5-layer tour ball |
| **Driver spin** | Low | Lower |
| **Driver flight** | Mid | Mid |
| **Iron spin** | Mid | Low |
| **Wedge spin** | Highest | High |
| **Feel** | Soft | Solid |
| **Price** | $57.99/dozen | $57.99/dozen |

That tells you almost everything you need to know. TP5 leans toward feel and short-game spin. TP5x leans toward speed and lower spin.

## What Actually Changes on the Course

The easiest way to think about this matchup is not "better" versus "worse." It's **softer control ball** versus **firmer speed ball**.

TaylorMade says the 2026 **TP5** is its softest five-layer tour ball, and the company also says the updated dimple pattern is built for a lower, more penetrating flight. The **TP5x**, meanwhile, is positioned as the family's lowest-spinning and fastest ball.

In normal-person golf terms:

- TP5 is the one I'd hand to the golfer who likes to feel the ball on chips, pitches, and putts.
- TP5x is the one I'd hand to the golfer who wants a firmer response and cares more about speed off driver and long irons.

## Off the Tee

If your priority is driver speed and a firmer, more stable feel at impact, **TP5x is the obvious fit**.

TaylorMade's own language on TP5x is not subtle. It calls it the fastest and lowest-spinning ball in this family. That usually means it makes more sense for golfers with enough speed to take advantage of it, or for golfers who simply hate seeing too much spin off the tee.

TP5 is hardly some floaty, spinny marshmallow. It is still a premium five-layer tour ball. But if you're shopping this exact comparison because you want the more "athletic" full-swing option, you're probably already leaning TP5x for a reason.

## Into the Green

This is where **TP5 makes its best case**.

TaylorMade's comparison says TP5 has **mid iron spin and the highest wedge spin**, while TP5x is **low on iron spin and high on wedge spin**. That's not a tiny wording change. That's the whole personality split.

If you like:

- a softer sensation on partial wedges
- a little more grab around the green
- a ball that feels more cooperative than aggressive

then TP5 is the smarter buy.

If you prefer a firmer click and don't mind slightly less of that soft-spin identity, TP5x still gives you plenty of scoring-club performance. It just gets there with a different feel profile.

## Feel Is the Real Tiebreaker

For a lot of golfers, this comparison gets decided on the putting green in about six strokes.

**TP5 feels soft. TP5x feels solid.**

That sounds minor until you realize your golf ball is the only piece of equipment you use on every shot. If you hate the feel off the putter or on little bump-and-runs, the "better" ball on paper becomes the worse ball in your bag.

This is why I keep telling golfers to stop copying whatever ball the best player in their group uses. Your game and your preferences matter more than the label.

## Who Should Buy TP5

Buy **TP5** if:

- you want the softer-feeling TaylorMade tour ball
- you care about wedge spin and touch more than top-end driver speed
- you already launch the ball fine and don't need to chase every last little bit of firmness
- you want the more forgiving recommendation for a broad range of decent golfers

TP5 is the easier ball to recommend to the "good but not insane" player.

## Who Should Buy TP5x

Buy **TP5x** if:

- you want the firmer TaylorMade feel
- you like the idea of lower spin and more speed off the top of the bag
- you hit the ball hard enough to prefer a ball that feels more stable than soft
- you already know you dislike softer premium balls

TP5x is the more specialized recommendation, but it is absolutely the right one for the golfer who wants that profile.

## My Verdict

If I had to recommend one ball to the most golfers without watching them hit a shot, I'd pick **TP5**. Softer feel plus more short-game spin is just easier for more players to enjoy.

If I knew the golfer had real speed, liked firmer feedback, and wanted the faster/lower-spinning side of TaylorMade's lineup, I'd send them straight to **TP5x** and not apologize for it.

So the short answer:

- **TP5 is the default.**
- **TP5x is the power-user version.**
