## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Callaway Jaws Raw** if your real goal is simple:

- get a still-legit traditional wedge
- pay way less than current-premium money
- and let available loft, grind, and condition steer the exact spec

Buy the **Titleist Vokey SM10** if you want the safer older-premium purchase:

- cleaner new-club shopping
- stronger Vokey shape-and-feel comfort
- and a premium wedge path that does not depend on pre-owned inventory luck

For most golfers who already know they want a players-style wedge, I would lean **Vokey SM10** as the safer recommendation.

For golfers trying to win the budget battle without dropping into junk-club compromise land, I would lean **Jaws Raw**.

## Why This Matchup Exists

This is not really a **Callaway versus Titleist** chest-thumping contest.

It is a more useful question:

**Do you buy the older premium wedge that got weirdly cheap, or do you buy the older premium wedge that still feels like the safer known thing?**

That is a real buying fork in 2026 because both clubs can now show up below current-model flagship pricing, but they get there in very different ways.

- **Jaws Raw** gets there through aging inventory and pre-owned markdowns
- **SM10** gets there through the usual "new one came out, old premium got friendlier" cycle

Those are not the same ownership stories.

## Price: Jaws Raw Hits Harder

This is the whole reason the **Jaws Raw** still matters.

Birdie Report's current July 15 inventory work had **Jaws Raw Face Chrome** starting at **$53.66** and topping out at **$133.88** on Callaway Golf Pre-Owned.

That is a serious number spread.

It means the Jaws Raw can be:

- dramatically cheaper than a new **SM10**
- cheap enough to make a two-wedge or three-wedge refresh feel sane
- tempting even for golfers who would not normally chase older models

The downside is obvious too.

You are shopping a moving target:

- condition changes
- lofts sell out
- grinds disappear
- one good bargain at breakfast can be gone by lunch

If you hate that kind of shopping friction, the value advantage starts shrinking emotionally even if the number still looks great.

## Buying Simplicity: SM10 Wins

The **SM10** wins the cleaner-ownership category.

That matters more than golfers like to admit.

The Vokey premium lane still gives you:

- the familiar shape
- the known-at-address look
- the deeper fitting logic
- and a cleaner "pick the right loft and grind, buy the club" experience

You are not wondering whether the face condition will be exactly what you hoped. You are not chasing a disappearing pre-owned spec. You are just deciding whether the older premium Vokey price is good enough to justify the purchase.

That is a much calmer purchase.

## Shape, Feel, and Greenside Confidence: SM10 Still Has the Cleaner Premium Case

The **Jaws Raw** still has a legit performance case.

The **SM10** still has the more natural premium-comfort case.

That difference matters most on:

- partial wedges
- little flighted knockdowns
- touch shots where you care how the club sits and releases

Golfers who already like Vokey usually are not chasing it for raw spin headlines alone. They are chasing the total package:

- shape
- sole options
- feel
- visual trust

That is why the **SM10** becomes attractive once it stops costing full flagship money.

If you only care about math, **Jaws Raw** can beat it.

If you care about whether the club looks like the answer before you even swing it, **SM10** still has more pull.

## Grind Depth and Fit Flexibility: Jaws Raw Is Deeper Than People Expect

One thing the **Jaws Raw** does not deserve is the lazy "cheap old wedge" dismissal.

The underlying setup is still serious:

- **48 through 62 degrees**
- **C, S, W, X, and Z grinds**
- tungsten in sand and lob wedges
- Groove-in-Groove texture in the scoring lofts

That is real wedge inventory.

It also means the **Jaws Raw** is best for golfers who are comfortable buying by loft and sole.

If that sounds like a hassle, it probably is. If that sounds normal, the Jaws Raw becomes a lot more attractive.

## Forgiveness: Neither One Is the Smart Help-First Answer

This is the part worth saying out loud:

neither the **Jaws Raw** nor the **SM10** is the wedge I would recommend to golfers who mostly need help.

They are both traditional-shape wedges.

They are both better fits for golfers who:

- strike wedges reasonably well
- want more control and shotmaking room
- know they are intentionally choosing a players-style shape

## Who Should Buy Each

### Buy the Callaway Jaws Raw if:

- you want the cheapest path into serious traditional-wedge performance
- you are comfortable shopping older inventory by loft, grind, and condition
- you want to stretch the budget across multiple wedges
- you care more about bargain value than brand comfort

### Buy the Titleist Vokey SM10 if:

- you already know you like the Vokey look and setup logic
- you want the safer older-premium purchase
- you care about premium half-shot feel and familiar shape
- you do not want the friction of pre-owned bargain hunting

## Final Verdict

The **Titleist Vokey SM10** is the better recommendation for most golfers shopping this exact fork because the buying path is cleaner, the premium fit story is easier to trust, and the older-Vokey discount is now good enough to make sense without turning the whole purchase into a scavenger hunt.

The **Callaway Jaws Raw** is the better value play if you are willing to shop opportunistically and let the available loft, grind, and condition do some of the deciding.

That is the split.

Smarter default: **SM10**.

Smarter bargain: **Jaws Raw**.
