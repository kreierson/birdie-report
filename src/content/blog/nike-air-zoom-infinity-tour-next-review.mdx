The **Nike Air Zoom Infinity Tour NEXT%** are the golf-shoe version of buying the loudest answer in the room and then hoping the loud answer also happens to be right.

Sometimes it is.

Sometimes it is just prettier.

That is why this shoe needs a real review instead of the usual "looks fast, therefore must be elite" nonsense.

This review is **research-based** and built from Nike's current product positioning, listed pricing context, and recurring golfer feedback patterns reflected across our existing shoe coverage in **June 2026**. No fake "I sprinted the back nine in these and achieved enlightenment" storytelling.

## Quick Verdict

The **Nike Air Zoom Infinity Tour NEXT%** are worth it for golfers who care about athletic feel, bolder style, and a more energized underfoot ride than the typical golf shoe delivers.

They are not the smartest all-around premium shoe. That lane still belongs more comfortably to the [FootJoy Pro/SL 2026 review](/blog/footjoy-pro-sl-2026-review/) and some of the more balanced picks in [Best Golf Shoes 2026](/blog/best-golf-shoes-2026/).

But if you hate conservative golf footwear and want the shoe that feels most like Nike dragging its running DNA onto the course, the **Infinity Tour NEXT%** absolutely have a case.

If your real decision is Nike's athletic energy versus FootJoy's broader one-shoe practicality, go directly to [Nike Air Zoom Infinity Tour NEXT% vs FootJoy Pro/SL 2026](/blog/nike-air-zoom-infinity-tour-next-vs-footjoy-pro-sl-2026/).

## What Nike Are Selling Here

The **Infinity Tour NEXT%** pitch is not subtle.

Nike want this shoe to feel:

- faster
- more responsive
- more athletic
- less like traditional golf footwear

That matters because the whole product is aimed at golfers who want their shoes to feel closer to modern training footwear than to classic golf leather.

If that sounds great, good.

If that already sounds annoying, you are probably not the target buyer.

## The Best Part: It Actually Feels Like a Nike Shoe

This sounds obvious. It is not.

A lot of brand-extension golf shoes feel like a compromise version of the parent brand's real identity. The **Infinity Tour NEXT%** do not have that problem.

The shoe's whole appeal is that it leans into:

- springier underfoot feel
- more aggressive athletic styling
- a faster visual profile
- a less buttoned-up ownership experience

That is why golfers who find a lot of golf footwear too stiff, too safe, or too country-club-clean keep ending up back in Nike's lane.

## Comfort and Ride: Livelier Than the Conservative Crowd

The **Infinity Tour NEXT%** are not trying to disappear on your feet. They are trying to feel active.

That is a real distinction.

Golfers who like the ride usually like that the shoe feels:

- more energized
- more athletic through the stride
- less old-man-comfort focused

That does not automatically make it better for everybody. Some golfers would rather have the steadier, lower-drama comfort of the [FootJoy Pro/SL 2026 review](/blog/footjoy-pro-sl-2026-review/) or the broader walking logic in [Best Golf Shoes for Walking 2026](/blog/best-golf-shoes-walking-2026/).

But if your ideal golf shoe feels closer to a modern performance trainer than to a polished golf staple, Nike are playing the right song.

## Traction: Good Enough for Most Normal Golf, Not the Main Event

The **Infinity Tour NEXT%** have a real traction story, but it is not the shoe's best selling point.

They make sense for:

- dry and mostly normal course conditions
- golfers who want spikeless convenience
- players who care about movement and feel more than maximum ground-lock

They make less sense if your whole shopping logic starts with:

- dewy sunrise rounds
- frequent wet turf
- hilly courses where slipping annoys you
- wanting the most planted answer available

That is where something like [Nike Air Zoom Infinity Tour NEXT% vs FootJoy Pro/SL 2026](/blog/nike-air-zoom-infinity-tour-next-vs-footjoy-pro-sl-2026/) or even the more traction-driven [FootJoy Pro/SL 2026 vs adidas Tour360 24](/blog/footjoy-pro-sl-2026-vs-adidas-tour360-24/) comparison becomes more useful than pure hype.

## Fit: More Selective Than FootJoy's Broad-Market Approach

This is one of the bigger reasons the shoe will work brilliantly for some golfers and feel like work for others.

Nike's fit lane tends to make more sense if you prefer:

- a more athletic shape
- less traditional roominess
- a shoe that feels more tuned than generic

That is great if it matches your feet.

It is less great if you already know you prefer the more accommodating feel of FootJoy's mainstream fit logic. That is part of why the [FootJoy Pro/SL 2026 review](/blog/footjoy-pro-sl-2026-review/) stays the safer recommendation for the average buyer.

## Style: This Is Why Many Golfers Want It

Let's stop pretending style is an afterthought here.

The **Infinity Tour NEXT%** have the strongest "I actually want to wear these" case in this lane.

That matters.

Golfers routinely overbuy "sensible" gear and underuse it because it never excites them. Nike's big advantage is that the shoes do not feel like a chore to choose.

If you care about:

- athletic styling
- a more current silhouette
- a shoe that looks less country-club safe

this is one of the better-looking options in the category.

## Where the Infinity Tour NEXT% Lose Ground

### The all-around value case is weaker

At roughly **$179**, the Nike are not cheap enough to be forgiven automatically.

The buyer has to actually value the athletic feel and style story, because the broader one-pair pragmatism is simply stronger on [FootJoy Pro/SL 2026](/blog/footjoy-pro-sl-2026-review/).

### Wet-weather trust is not the main selling point

There are better shoes if waterproof confidence and year-round versatility are the first things on your list.

This is not the premium spikeless answer I would push first for ugly conditions.

### Fit can be a filter

The more athletic shape is a feature for some golfers and a blocker for others. That is not a small detail when you are spending premium-shoe money.

## Who Should Buy the Nike Air Zoom Infinity Tour NEXT%

Buy them if:

- you want the most athletic-feeling premium spikeless shoe in this cluster
- style matters to you and you are tired of conservative golf shoes
- you mostly play normal conditions and do not need maximum wet-weather reassurance
- Nike's more tuned fit shape usually works for you

Skip them if:

- you want the safest all-around value buy
- you prioritize waterproof trust and broad daily versatility
- you prefer a roomier, more forgiving fit lane
- you are shopping golf shoes as a pure practicality play

## Final Verdict

The **Nike Air Zoom Infinity Tour NEXT%** are a very real answer for golfers who want energy, style, and a more athletic feel than most golf shoes offer.

They are not the smartest shoe for most golfers.

They are the most tempting shoe for a specific kind of golfer, and that is not the same thing.

If I were recommending blind to the broadest audience, I would still point to [FootJoy Pro/SL 2026](/blog/footjoy-pro-sl-2026-review/).

If I knew the golfer wanted the more athletic, less conservative, more Nike-feeling choice, I would feel perfectly good pointing here.
