The **Women's Scottish Open** did not open with fake-summer-soft conditions and a bunch of polite 68s.

It opened with **wind**, **firm turf**, and two players who handled it better than everybody else.

According to the **LPGA's official round-one recap published Thursday, July 23, 2026**, checked on **Friday, July 24**, **Jenny Shin** and **Lauren Coughlin** share the lead at **6-under par** after matching **66s** at **Dundonald Links**. The same LPGA report says **A Lim Kim** sits alone in third at **5-under 67**, one shot back.

That is a much better start than a sleepy "major warm-up" script.

This piece is based on the **LPGA's official July 23, 2026 round-one recap**, checked on **Friday, July 24, 2026**. No pretending I was out there charting every bounce in the Scottish wind with a notebook getting soaked through by lunchtime.

## Shin Owned the Morning Wave

The LPGA said **Shin** posted the best score from the morning side with a **66** that started on the **10th hole** and built quickly.

Per the tour recap, she made birdies on:

- **10**
- **12**
- **14**
- **18**
- **3**
- **7**
- **9**

Her only dropped shot came at the **4th**, which is a pretty clean card on a day the LPGA itself described as windy.

The more useful detail was Shin's explanation of how the course played. The LPGA quoted her saying tee shots were crucial, the course had dried out enough to play shorter than in past years, and the par 5s were there to attack if you got the ball in play first.

That tracks. Firm links golf always looks fun from the couch until the ground starts making its own decisions.

## Coughlin Looked Like Somebody Who Has Seen This Place Before

**Coughlin** got there from the afternoon wave, and the LPGA's round-one story made the shape of the round pretty clear:

- **eight birdies**
- **two bogeys**
- another **66**

That is not a plodding survival round. That is a player finding enough control to still make birdies when the course is acting weird.

The LPGA also noted that Coughlin is the **2024 champion** at Dundonald, which matters because this did not look like somebody trying to rediscover the place. It looked like somebody who already understood where the score could still be found when the course got dry and awkward off the tee.

Her quotes in the LPGA recap were basically a links-golf survival guide: hit as many fairways and greens as possible, miss in the right spots, and trust your feel for the wind.

That is a lot more useful than pretending this place can be bullied.

## A Lim Kim Is One Back, and the Board Has Enough Depth Already

The LPGA said **A Lim Kim** is alone in third after a **67** that included **three straight birdies on holes 10 through 12**.

Behind her, the report says **Lisa Pettersson** is fourth at **3-under**, and four players share fifth at **2-under**:

- **Esther Henseleit**
- **Anna Nordqvist**
- **Erika Hara**
- **Miyu Yamashita**

That is already a useful leaderboard because it has both form players and actual links-golf competence on it. It does not feel like one of those first-round boards you can politely ignore until Saturday.

## The Conditions Are the Story Too

The most interesting part of the LPGA recap might be the course description more than the number itself.

Both **Shin** and **Coughlin** talked about how firm Dundonald was playing, especially compared with previous years. That matters because it gives the week exactly the kind of identity this tournament deserves. We already argued in [our pre-tournament opinion piece](/blog/womens-scottish-open-not-a-warm-up-opinion-2026/) that this event should be treated like more than a holding pattern before **Royal Lytham**.

Rounds like this help make the point.

Firm links turf, real wind, and a leaderboard that already feels earned is a lot better than another week where everybody pretends the only thing that matters is what happens seven days later.

## Bottom Line

The **LPGA's official July 23 round-one recap**, checked on **Friday, July 24, 2026**, says **Jenny Shin** and **Lauren Coughlin** share the **Women's Scottish Open** lead at **6-under 66**, with **A Lim Kim** one back at **5-under 67**.

More importantly, Dundonald already looks exactly how this tournament should want to look: firm, windy, slightly annoying, and worth paying attention to on its own.
