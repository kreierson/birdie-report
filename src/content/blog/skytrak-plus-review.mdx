The **SkyTrak+** is still one of the more interesting launch monitors in golf because it solves a real problem: a lot of golfers want simulator-friendly accuracy without jumping all the way into Bushnell Launch Pro money.

The problem is that the shopping story got a lot uglier.

This review is **research-based** and built from the current official SkyTrak product page and the surrounding Birdie Report launch-monitor coverage as checked on **May 11, 2026**. No fake "I had this in my simulator cave all winter" nonsense.

## Quick Verdict

The **SkyTrak+** still makes sense for a pretty specific golfer:

- you care a lot about indoor simulator use
- you want a smaller-footprint setup than behind-the-ball radar usually asks for
- you find legitimate inventory, certified pre-owned stock, or a real discount that changes the math

If you want the cleanest new-buy answer in launch monitors, this is not it anymore.

The official product page currently shows a **$1,995** sale price and also says the **launch monitor is no longer available**. That means the SkyTrak+ is no longer a simple "buy this one" recommendation. It is now more of a targeted recommendation for golfers who know exactly why they want it.

If you want the cleaner alternatives first, read [FlightScope Mevo+ Review](/blog/flightscope-mevo-plus-review/), [Bushnell Launch Pro Review](/blog/bushnell-launch-pro-review/), [SkyTrak+ vs FlightScope Mevo+](/blog/skytrak-plus-vs-flightscope-mevo-plus/), and [SkyTrak+ vs Rapsodo MLM2PRO](/blog/skytrak-plus-vs-rapsodo-mlm2pro/).

## What SkyTrak+ Is Actually Selling

SkyTrak's current pitch is still coherent even if the availability situation is annoying.

The official page highlights:

- **dual doppler radar plus a photometric camera**
- indoor and outdoor use
- a **4-hour** rechargeable battery
- a **2 x 2 hitting zone**
- access to practice and simulator features through the app ecosystem

That is the real draw.

The SkyTrak+ is built for golfers who think of a launch monitor as a **simulator anchor first** and a casual range toy second. If that is your mindset, the product still has a legitimate lane.

## Why the Smaller-Setup Story Still Matters

This is the best reason to care about the SkyTrak+ in 2026.

A lot of radar-first launch monitors are good until you try to make them live in a garage, basement, or spare room that was not designed around golf tech. Then space behind the ball becomes a whole extra problem.

SkyTrak pushes the ST+ as a cleaner fit for tighter simulator spaces, and that still matters. If your setup room is compromised, the smaller hitting footprint is not some tiny spec-sheet footnote. It is the difference between a launch monitor feeling usable and feeling like a constant workaround.

That is also why the SkyTrak+ stays interesting next to products like the [FlightScope Mevo+](/blog/flightscope-mevo-plus-review/) and the [Rapsodo MLM2PRO](/blog/skytrak-plus-vs-rapsodo-mlm2pro/). Those products make a lot of sense, but they solve the ownership problem in a different way.

## Indoor-First Value Is Still the Real Appeal

The SkyTrak+ is easiest to defend if your golf-tech budget is really a simulator budget.

If you mostly want:

- better indoor practice
- tighter simulator feedback
- cleaner ball-data trust than cheap radar usually gives you

then the SkyTrak+ has a better case than it does for the golfer who just wants fun range data.

That is the dividing line.

The ST+ does not make the most sense as a portable range companion. It makes the most sense as the golfer's attempt to buy a more serious indoor experience without going all the way up the premium ladder.

If your buying path is more outdoor or mixed-use, the [Garmin R10 vs Rapsodo MLM2PRO](/blog/garmin-r10-vs-rapsodo-mlm2pro/) and [Best Golf Launch Monitors Under $1,500 in 2026](/blog/best-golf-launch-monitors-under-1500-2026/) pages are probably the smarter place to start.

## The Shopping Story Is the Problem

This is the part worth saying clearly.

The official SkyTrak page is not presenting a clean "add to cart and relax" story right now.

What it currently communicates is:

- the device is framed at a **$1,995** sale price
- you get a **14-day trial**
- memberships still matter for deeper app functionality
- and the page also says the **launch monitor is no longer available**

That is a messy combination.

If you find a legitimate retailer listing, certified pre-owned unit, or discounted inventory, the product may still make sense. But if you are comparing fresh, clean new-buy options, the SkyTrak+ immediately loses ground to simpler answers like the **Mevo+** and **MLM2PRO**.

## Membership Friction Is Not Zero

Older SkyTrak discourse still tends to talk like the whole appeal is "buy once and never think about it again."

That is not the full current story.

The official page now makes the app ecosystem much more visible, with paid membership tiers tied to broader feature access after the included trial. That does not automatically make the SkyTrak+ a bad buy. It just means the ownership story is not as magically friction-free as people sometimes pretend.

That matters because launch-monitor shopping gets stupid fast when:

- the hardware is expensive
- the shopping path is messy
- and the best version of the ownership experience still wants more software commitment

- your space makes behind-the-ball radar less attractive
- you find real stock at a compelling price
- you understand the product is now a more conditional buy, not a default one

## Final Verdict

The **SkyTrak+** is still interesting because the core product idea is still good.

Compact simulator-first setups are real. Indoor practice matters. And there is still a group of golfers who would absolutely prefer this type of launch monitor to a behind-the-ball radar unit.

But the recommendation is not clean anymore.

The official page's **$1,995** sale pricing, **14-day** trial-to-membership structure, and **no longer available** note all push the ST+ out of the easy-buy category.

So the honest recommendation is this:

- **Buy the SkyTrak+ only if you know exactly why you want it and can actually source it well**
- **Buy something else if you want the straightforward answer**
