This is one of the cleanest upgrade questions in golf right now.

Not because the two wedges are wildly different.

Because they are **close enough** that the price gap actually matters.

The **Vokey SM11** is the newer, smarter, more complete premium answer.

The **Vokey SM10** is the older premium answer that still looks dangerously sensible once the receipt shows up.

This comparison is **research-based** and built from Titleist's official **SM11** and **SM10** product pages plus Titleist's **January 19, 2026** SM11 launch release, checked on **June 5, 2026**. Those current official pages show **SM11** starting at **$199** and **SM10** starting at **$159**. Titleist's SM11 release also details the line's new **Vokey Spin System**, **5% larger groove volume**, **identical CG placement across grinds within each loft**, new **44.10F** option, and new **.06K** low-bounce lob-wedge option. No fake "I played both for three months and discovered my true self" nonsense.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Vokey SM10** if you want the smarter value and you are not chasing every last update.

Buy the **Vokey SM11** if you are already in the premium wedge lane on purpose and want the cleaner spin, fit, and durability story.

My honest split:

- **SM10** is the better buy for most golfers as long as the price gap stays around **$40**
- **SM11** is the better buy for golfers who care enough about wedge fit to actually use what changed

## Price: This Is Why the Comparison Exists

If the current official Titleist pricing were basically identical, this conversation would be shorter.

It is not.

| | Titleist Vokey SM11 | Titleist Vokey SM10 |
|---|---|---|
| **Current official starting price** | $199 | $159 |
| **Core pitch** | newer spin, cleaner fit logic, more complete lineup | still-premium Vokey performance at the friendlier number |
| **Best fit** | golfers who want the latest premium-control package | golfers who want Vokey quality without full new-model tax |

That **$40 gap per wedge** gets real fast if you are building a two- or three-wedge setup.

That is why the **SM11** has to do more than "be new."

It has to be meaningfully more useful.

## What the SM11 Does Better

### 1. The fit story is cleaner

The smartest official SM11 change is the **identical CG placement across grinds within each loft**.

That means if you move between grinds to fit turf interaction better, you are not also unintentionally changing the flight profile more than necessary.

That is subtle.

It is also exactly the kind of subtle thing serious wedge buyers should care about.

### 2. The spin package is more complete

Titleist says SM11 adds:

- **three shot-specific groove shapes**
- **5% larger groove volume**
- a new **directional face texture**
- **heat-treated groove edges**

That is a stronger "premium because it helps" story than a lot of yearly wedge refreshes manage.

The point is not just more spin in a launch-monitor fantasy. The point is more consistent control when the lie is messy and the strike is less than perfect.

### 3. The lineup got more useful

SM11 adds:

- **44.10F** for stronger-loft wedge gapping
- **58.06K** and **60.06K** for a new low-bounce K-grind lob-wedge option
- a new **Jet Black** finish alongside Tour Chrome, Nickel, and Raw

That makes the newer line more complete at both ends of the matrix.

## What the SM10 Still Does Really Well

This is the part golfers need to stop pretending away.

The **SM10** is not suddenly a bad wedge because **SM11** exists.

It still gives you:

- Vokey feel
- six grinds
- broad loft coverage
- the same premium wedge family identity that made Vokey the default answer in the first place

At **$159**, the SM10 starts looking like the practical Vokey buy.

That matters even more if you are buying multiple wedges and care more about getting the whole setup right than about owning the latest generation.

## Final Verdict

The **Titleist Vokey SM11** is the better wedge family.

The **Titleist Vokey SM10** may still be the smarter purchase for more golfers.

That is not a contradiction.

It just means the improvements in **SM11** are real, but not so dramatic that every golfer should automatically pay for them.

If you are a wedge nerd, a fitter, or the kind of golfer who actually notices and values the new CG and spin details, buy the **SM11**.

If you mostly want premium Vokey quality and the price gap still matters, buy the **SM10** and spend the saved money on the rest of the setup instead of on ego maintenance.
