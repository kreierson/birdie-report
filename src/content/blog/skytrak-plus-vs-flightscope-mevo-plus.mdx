## Quick Verdict

Buy the **FlightScope Mevo+** if you want the cleaner recommendation in real life.

Buy the **SkyTrak+** only if you specifically want its camera-plus-radar approach and you find legitimate inventory or certified pre-owned pricing that makes the shopping friction worth it.

That may sound harsh, but the official SkyTrak product page literally says the **ST+ is no longer available**, while FlightScope still has the **Mevo+ live at $1,099** with included simulator and practice software.

So the comparison is not just about launch-monitor capability anymore.

It is about whether you want to buy a product or start a side quest.

## The Shopping Reality Is the Whole Story

FlightScope's current Mevo+ page lists:

- **$1,099** sale pricing
- **20 data parameters**
- **12 E6 Connect courses**
- **Fusion Tracking**
- free practice, skills combines, and PC software

SkyTrak's current ST+ page lists:

- **$1,995** sale pricing
- dual doppler radar plus photometric camera
- indoor and outdoor use
- a **4-hour** rechargeable battery
- then, right in the middle of the page, the note that the launch monitor **is no longer available**

That changes the argument immediately.

If your question is, "Which launch monitor should I confidently buy new right now?" the answer is **Mevo+**.

If your question is, "Which one is more interesting if I find discounted or certified pre-owned SkyTrak stock?" then the matchup gets tighter.

## Where SkyTrak+ Still Makes a Real Case

The SkyTrak+ is not interesting because it is easy.

It is interesting because it still offers a feature mix plenty of golfers want:

- **dual doppler radar** for club data
- an **advanced photometric camera system** for better ball tracking
- indoor and outdoor use
- club path, face angle, smash factor, and other useful club data

That is a good pitch. A genuinely good one.

It is especially appealing if you think of your launch monitor as an **indoor simulator anchor first** and a portable range tool second.

The official page also claims accuracy within **2 yards** of leading pro-level launch monitors based on third-party GolfBays testing. I would treat that as brand positioning, not as a universal promise, but it does tell you how SkyTrak wants this device understood: as a serious sim/practice product, not a toy.

## Where Mevo+ Has the Cleaner Real-World Advantage

The Mevo+ wins the adult-shopping part of this comparison.

FlightScope's official page is clearer and simpler:

- the product is available
- the price is aggressive
- the included software value is obvious
- the support story is still active

That matters more than golfers want to admit.

A launch monitor is already a complicated purchase. When one option adds product-status weirdness on top of the usual simulator-software and setup questions, the cleaner option becomes more attractive fast.

That is what the Mevo+ is right now.

## Indoor Setup Reality

Neither of these is a friction-free device.

The **Mevo+** asks for real setup discipline. FlightScope's official requirements say:

- **8 feet** from sensor to tee
- **8 to 13 feet** of limited-flight ball space indoors
- aluminum stickers for some limited-flight use

That is not impossible. It is just not casual.

The **SkyTrak+** has a smaller hitting-zone footprint on the page and is clearly positioned as an indoor-and-outdoor hybrid. If your space is tighter, the SkyTrak form factor and camera-first positioning may still feel more natural.

So if your simulator room is compromised or awkward, do not pretend that point is secondary. It is not.

## Software and Ownership Annoyance

This is where the products diverge in personality.

The **Mevo+** includes a lot right away:

- practice software
- skills combines
- PC software
- **12 E6 Connect courses**

The **SkyTrak+** includes a **14-day trial membership**, then pushes you toward paid membership plans to unlock the fuller app experience.

That does not automatically make SkyTrak a bad deal. But it does make the Mevo+ feel more complete right out of the box.

If you already hate the idea of buying hardware and immediately negotiating with software tiers, Mevo+ is the safer lane.
