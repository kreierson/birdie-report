If you already know you want one of Garmin's newer mid-price golf watches, this is the comparison that matters.

Not **S70 versus everything**.

Not **Garmin versus Apple Watch**.

The current buying fork is **Approach S50 versus Approach S44**, because both watches give you the same new-generation Garmin look, the same **1.2-inch AMOLED display**, and the same **43,000-plus course** ecosystem.

The difference is whether you should pay the extra **$100** for the **S50**.

This is a research-based comparison built from Garmin's official launch details and current official product positioning checked on **May 28, 2026**. No invented testing, no fake "I wore both for three months and slept in the data" routine.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Garmin Approach S44** if you want the smarter golf-first purchase.

Buy the **Garmin Approach S50** if you specifically want:

- **PlaysLike distance** built in
- wrist-based **heart rate**
- **health and wellness** tracking
- **activity profiles**
- better everyday-watch justification beyond the golf course

For most golfers who mainly want a clean Garmin golf watch, I would recommend the **S44**.

For golfers who want one watch to carry more of normal life and training too, I would recommend the **S50**.

## Why the S44 Gets the Recommendation for Most Golfers

The **S44** already covers the main golf-watch use case cleanly:

- **front, middle, and back distances**
- **hazard view**
- **green view**
- **43,000-plus preloaded courses**
- **AMOLED display**
- **smart notifications**

That is enough for a lot of golfers.

More importantly, it is enough without asking you to spend four hundred bucks on a watch that might mostly live in golf mode anyway.

The S44 is easier to recommend because it stays focused. It is a golf watch first. It does not need a long explanation to justify itself.

## What the Extra $100 Buys on the S50

This is where the article gets real.

Garmin's official launch stack says the **S50** adds:

- **PlaysLike distance**
- wrist-based **heart rate**
- **sleep**, **stress**, and **Body Battery** features
- **activity profiles** for strength, yoga, cardio, and more
- **Morning Report**
- **Garmin Pay**
- music and podcast controls with supported services
- a **ComfortFit nylon strap**

That is not fake differentiation.

It is just not automatically golf-only differentiation.

The S50 is better if you want the watch to matter outside the course. That is the real value proposition.

## This Is Really a One-Watch Versus Golf-Watch Decision

The easiest way to think about this:

- **S44** if you want a golf watch
- **S50** if you want a golf watch that can credibly do more of your everyday wearable job

That sounds like a subtle difference, but it is the whole buying decision.

If your golf watch goes on only when you head to the course, the **S44** makes far more sense.

If you actually want to wear the thing to the gym, on walks, during the workday, and maybe let it pretend it has some say in your sleep quality, the **S50** becomes easier to justify.

## Golf Features: Closer Than the Price Suggests

This is why the comparison is good.

Both watches already share:

- the same core display size
- the same course library
- the same main Garmin golf ecosystem
- the same slim, lightweight general concept

That means the **S50** is not winning because it transforms the golf experience. It is winning because it layers extra value on top of a very similar golf base.

The one direct golf differentiator that matters most is **PlaysLike distance**.

If you care about slope-adjusted yardage on the watch itself, that matters.

If you do not, the gap between the two watches gets smaller very fast.

## Why the S50 Still Has a Legit Case

I do not want to undersell the **S50**.

For the right buyer, it is the better purchase.

That buyer is probably someone who:

- wants **PlaysLike** on the watch without messing around
- values the **health and wellness** tools
- prefers spending once on a more complete wearable
- knows they will wear the thing well beyond golf

That is also the buyer who might otherwise start drifting toward more expensive options like the [Garmin Approach S70 review](/blog/garmin-approach-s70-review/).

In that context, the S50 looks pretty sane.

## Where the S50 Can Still Be the Wrong Buy

The S50 becomes a mistake when golfers buy it for features they will never use.

That happens all the time in golf tech.

People talk themselves into:

- better spec sheet
- more features
- better future-proofing

and end up using the watch exactly the same way they would have used the cheaper one.

If your real behavior is going to be:

- check yardage
- glance at hazards
- maybe move the pin
- finish round

then the **S44** is the sharper adult decision.
