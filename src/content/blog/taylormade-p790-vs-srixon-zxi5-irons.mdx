The **TaylorMade P790** has basically become the default answer whenever a golfer says, "I want sexy irons that still help me a little."

The **Srixon ZXi5** is the iron gear nerds bring up right after that, usually with the tone of someone who thinks they found the smarter answer.

Honestly, they might have.

This is one of the most legit players-distance comparisons in golf right now because both irons are aimed at the same golfer, but they get there differently. The P790 sells speed, style, and that TaylorMade glow. The ZXi5 sells balance, turf interaction, and the kind of all-around competence that makes good players nod like they know a secret.

## The Quick Answer

Buy the **TaylorMade P790** if you want the more explosive, more famous, more flex-worthy players-distance iron.

Buy the **Srixon ZXi5** if you want the more complete iron for the money.

That sounds simple because it is simple.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

| | TaylorMade P790 | Srixon ZXi5 |
|---|---|---|
| **Price** | $1,399 / 7-piece set | $1,299 / 7-piece set |
| **Category** | Players distance | Players distance |
| **Best trait** | Ball speed and modern distance | Balance, feel, and turf interaction |

| **Forgiveness** | Very good | Very good |
| **Best for** | Golfers who want speed and sleek looks | Golfers who want the most complete package |

This is not a good-versus-bad matchup. It is a good-versus-annoyingly-good matchup.

## Distance: P790 Has the Bigger Reputation

If you're shopping this category, you already know the P790's whole thing.

It is built to look like a players iron while still giving you real help. The face tech, hollow-body construction, and distance-first reputation are why this club never leaves the conversation. Golfers want something that looks clean in the bag and still gives them a little bailout when the strike drifts away from perfect. The P790 absolutely scratches that itch.

That is also why it keeps showing up in conversations next to the [Titleist T200](/taylormade-p790-vs-titleist-t200-irons), the [Mizuno JPX925 Forged](/mizuno-jpx925-forged-vs-taylormade-p790-irons), and basically every "what should I buy if I'm getting better?" thread on earth.

The ZXi5 is not short. Let's kill that idea right away. Based on early reviews and the broader consensus around the head design, it still brings plenty of speed. It just doesn't scream distance in the same way the P790 does. TaylorMade leans harder into that identity, and golfers feel it.

So if your first priority is, "which one gives me the hotter modern-iron vibe?" the answer is still **P790**.

## Feel and Turf Interaction: ZXi5 Has the Better Golf Sicko Case

This is where the **Srixon ZXi5** starts feeling like the smarter pick.

The biggest recurring compliment around Srixon irons is not hype. It is consistency. Players keep praising the turf interaction, and that matters a lot more than marketing copy does. The **Tour V.T. sole** has earned a real reputation for helping the club move through the turf cleanly instead of feeling grabby or weird.

That sounds like a small detail until you play on inconsistent turf or tend to be a little steep. Then it becomes a very big detail.

The ZXi5 also carries the same general strength that came through in our full [Srixon ZXi5 irons review](/srixon-zxi5-irons-review): it does a lot of things well without feeling like it had to sacrifice its soul to get there.

The P790 feels good. The ZXi5 feels more complete.

## Forgiveness: Close, But Slight Edge to ZXi5 for Real-World Use

Both of these irons live in the sweet spot where good golfers do not feel insulted and improving golfers do not feel punished.

That is why players-distance irons are such a dangerous category for your wallet.

The P790 gives you strong help across the set, especially in the longer irons. The ZXi5 also gives you real forgiveness, but it tends to get framed as the more balanced, easier-to-trust iron when the strike is not perfect and the turf is doing weird stuff.

If you want the iron that feels a touch more stable and grown-up, I lean **ZXi5**.

If you want the one that feels more electric, I lean **P790**.

## Looks: P790 Wins the Vanity Contest

Look, this matters. Golfers pretend it doesn't, then spend $1,400 based on topline vibes.

The **P790** is still one of the best-looking irons in this category. TaylorMade knows exactly what this club is supposed to make you feel when you set it down, and they are very good at that game.

The **ZXi5** looks excellent too, but in a calmer way. It is less "main character iron" and more "serious golfer who did the reading."

If you want the prettier iron, buy the **P790**.

If you want the iron that may age a little better in your brain after the purchase high wears off, buy the **ZXi5**.

## Value: ZXi5 Is the Smarter Buy

This is where I stop dancing around it.

The **Srixon ZXi5** is the better value.

You're spending a little less, getting a ton of the same category appeal, and probably ending up with the more rounded iron. That does not mean the P790 is overpriced junk. It means the P790 charges a little extra for being the P790.

TaylorMade can do that because the club is popular as hell.

Srixon is where the value lives if you care more about actual fit than logo gravity. That is why the ZXi5 belongs right in the middle of the [best irons for mid handicappers in 2026](/best-irons-mid-handicappers-2026) conversation.

## Who Should Buy the TaylorMade P790

Buy the **P790** if:

- you want the more explosive distance-first personality
- you care a lot about compact looks and brand confidence
- you want a players-distance iron with maximum mainstream validation
- you are willing to pay extra for the hotter name in the category

## Who Should Buy the Srixon ZXi5

Buy the **ZXi5** if:

- you want the more complete iron top to bottom
- turf interaction matters a lot to you
- you want premium performance without paying full hype tax
- you like gear that golfers rave about for actual reasons, not just marketing reasons

## Final Verdict

The **TaylorMade P790** is the cooler iron.

The **Srixon ZXi5** is the smarter iron.

For some golfers, the cooler iron is the right answer. If you love the P790 look, want that faster feel, and have always had your eye on it, I'm not going to talk you out of it.

But if you want my actual recommendation for the average good player shopping this category, I would buy the **Srixon ZXi5**.

It feels like the more balanced choice, the better value, and the one you're less likely to second-guess three months later.
