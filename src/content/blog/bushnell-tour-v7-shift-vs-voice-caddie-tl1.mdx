- **Voice Caddie** feels more feature-forward

## Pricing Is What Keeps the TL1 Alive

This is where the article actually gets useful.

At full **$349.99 MSRP**, the TL1 is a legitimate product but a tougher sell.

At a real promo price below that, the TL1 becomes much more dangerous.

That is the whole point.

If the actual out-the-door choice is:

- **Bushnell at $399.99**
- **TL1 much closer to the high-$200s or low-$300s**

then Voice Caddie has a real argument.

If the pricing gap stays tight and the TL1 is basically just "slightly cheaper premium," the **Bushnell Tour V7 Shift** becomes the much easier recommendation.

So the recommendation is not fake-equal:

- **promo active:** TL1 gets interesting fast
- **promo gone:** V7 Shift is the clearer buy

## Warranty and Ownership Still Favor Bushnell

This is a sneaky but important edge.

Bushnell gives you a **two-year warranty**.

Voice Caddie gives you **one year**.

That does not automatically mean the TL1 is fragile. It does mean the expensive-gadget comfort level is lower.

And that matters because premium rangefinders live in carts, bags, light rain, hot trunks, and the occasional dumb moment where a golfer treats his gear like it owes him money.

Bushnell's extra warranty coverage is part of why the ownership story stays cleaner.
