## Cameron Young Just Won the Biggest Tournament of His Career — And He Did It the Hard Way

Look, if you told me at the start of Sunday that Cameron Young — starting four shots back of Ludvig Åberg — was going to win The Players Championship, I'd have asked what you were drinking and where I could get some.

But that's exactly what happened. And honestly? It was one of the most entertaining final rounds at TPC Sawgrass in years.

## The Åberg Meltdown

Let's address the elephant in the room. Ludvig Åberg came into Sunday with a comfortable lead and proceeded to shoot himself out of the tournament alongside playing partner Michael Thorbjornsen. The two combined for **9 over par** on the day. Nine. Over. At the fifth major.

I'm not going to pile on Åberg here — the dude is 26 and will win plenty of big ones. But man, TPC Sawgrass on a Sunday with the lead... that place just eats people alive. The Stadium Course doesn't care about your world ranking.

## Young's Near-Flawless 68

While the leaders were making a mess, Cam Young was quietly going about his business from the penultimate group. A **4-under 68** with just one bogey on the card. Five birdies. Bogey-free on the back nine when it mattered most.

The two shots that won it:

**The 17th hole birdie.** Young hit a 57-degree wedge from 130 yards to 10 feet on the Island Green — arguably the most pressure-packed shot in golf — and buried the putt. He called it "a great number" and said a full swing was actually less nerve-wracking than a touchy, feely one. Cold. Blooded.

**The 375-yard bomb on 18.** This is genuinely insane. The longest drive *ever* recorded on the 18th hole at TPC Sawgrass in the ShotLink era. Young himself called it "the best shot of my life." He had 98 yards left. Ninety-eight. On a par 4. With the tournament on the line.

## Fitzpatrick Falls Just Short

Matt Fitzpatrick played a great round too — also a 68 — and actually held the lead briefly after birdies on 12 and 13. But he found the pine straw right off the tee on 18 and missed an 8-foot par putt that would have forced a playoff.

You hate to see it decided by a miss, but that's TPC Sawgrass. There's nowhere to hide on that closing stretch.

## What This Means for Young

This is only Cameron Young's **second PGA Tour victory**, but it's a massive one. Remember, this is the guy who racked up a tour-record-tying *seven* runner-up finishes before finally breaking through at the Wyndham Championship last summer.

The Players win vaults him to **No. 4 in the world** — his highest ranking ever. At 29 years old, the narrative has officially shifted from "most talented guy who can't close" to "big-game player."

The Valspar Championship is up next this week at Innisbrook. It'll be interesting to see if Young rides this momentum or takes a well-deserved breather.
