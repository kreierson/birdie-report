This is the golf-watch version of a question golfers ask in a lot of categories:

Do you buy the **nicer product**, or do you buy the one that actually gives you the most useful golf value?

Because the **Garmin Approach S50** and **Shot Scope V5** are not really trying to win the same way.

The **S50** is trying to be the golf smartwatch you will gladly wear beyond the course.

The **V5** is trying to help you shoot lower scores without quietly billing you again later.

This is a **research-based comparison** built from Garmin's official Approach S50 launch materials and Shot Scope's current V5 product page checked on **May 29, 2026**. No fake "I tested these across 40 rounds and became a different man" nonsense.

![Garmin Approach S50 golf smartwatch](https://res.garmin.com/en/products/010-03010-00/v/cf-lg.jpg)
*Image: Garmin*

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Shot Scope V5** if you want the smarter golf purchase for most golfers.

Buy the **Garmin Approach S50** if you specifically want:

- a better **AMOLED smartwatch**
- built-in **PlaysLike distance**
- **health and wellness** tracking
- **activity profiles**
- a device you will actually wear when you are not golfing

For golfers who mostly care about improving and keeping the ownership math sane, I would recommend the **Shot Scope V5** first.

For golfers who want the better overall wearable and do not mind paying for it, I would recommend the **S50**.

## Why the Shot Scope V5 Gets the Recommendation for Most Golfers

The **V5** is one of the cleanest value stories in golf tech because Shot Scope does not make you assemble the useful part later.

The current V5 package still gives you:

- **16 tracking tags included**
- **100-plus stats**
- **36,000+ preloaded courses**
- **full hole maps**
- **green view and pin placement**
- **no subscription fees**
- a **two-year warranty**

That is a strong package at **$209.99 sale / $249.99 regular**.

More importantly, it is a package aimed directly at the golfer who wants the watch to help answer a real question:

**where am I actually losing shots?**

That is a much more useful question than "does my golf watch also track yoga?"

## What the Extra Money Buys on the S50

Garmin does have a real answer here.

The official S50 launch stack adds:

- **PlaysLike distance**
- wrist-based **heart rate**
- **sleep**, **stress**, and **Body Battery**
- **activity profiles**
- **Morning Report**
- **Garmin Pay**
- music controls with supported services
- a more premium **AMOLED** experience

That is why the S50 exists.

It is not just a golf watch. It is a golf watch that wants a much bigger role in your life.

That can absolutely be worth the money if you will use it that way.

It just is not automatically the better golf buy.

## This Is Really Smartwatch Value Versus Improvement Value

The easiest way to frame it:

- **S50** if you want a better watch
- **V5** if you want better golf value

The Shot Scope is not trying to win on luxury. It is trying to win on usefulness.

The Garmin is not trying to win on raw game-improvement value. It is trying to win on polish, display quality, and all-day wearability.

That makes this one of the cleanest buyer-intent pages in the whole GPS cluster.

## Where the V5 Has the More Useful Golf Story

The **V5** matters because it gives you GPS plus tracking plus post-round learning in one purchase.

That means:

- no club-tag add-on decision
- no recurring subscription
- no "I will buy the analysis part later" lie you tell yourself in March and forget by June

If you actually want to learn from your rounds, the **V5** is the smarter golf-specific tool.

That is the same reason it keeps making sense against the [Garmin Approach S44](/blog/garmin-approach-s44-vs-shot-scope-v5/), the [Garmin Approach S12](/blog/shot-scope-v5-vs-garmin-approach-s12/), and subscription-heavy shot-tracking paths like [Arccos vs Shot Scope](/blog/arccos-vs-shot-scope-shot-tracking/).

## Where the S50 Can Still Be the Better Purchase

The **S50** is the better choice if your watch needs to matter off the course too.

That golfer probably:

- wants a much nicer display
- cares about **PlaysLike**
- prefers **Garmin's** broader ecosystem
- wants health and activity tracking
- does not want to wear a golf-only watch

If that is you, the Shot Scope can still look like good value and still be the wrong purchase.

Value is only value if it lines up with how you actually use the thing.

## The Two Biggest Questions to Ask Yourself

### 1. Will you actually use the post-round data?

If the answer is **yes**, the **V5** gets very hard to beat.

If the answer is **no**, a huge chunk of the V5's advantage disappears.

### 2. Do you want a golf watch or a smartwatch that happens to be very good at golf?

If you want the second one, that is the **S50**.

If you want the first one, the V5 is probably the more rational answer.
