So small improvements can matter:

- clearer number presentation
- nicer overall viewfinder feel
- more obviously premium confirmation and display behavior

## What Did Not Change Enough to Make the V6 Irrelevant

The **V6 Shift** still does the actual golf job really well.

It still gives you:

- a great slope switch
- excellent lock confidence
- trustworthy premium-brand familiarity
- a very normal ownership experience for golfers who just want the safer known answer

Also, both products still lean on **CR2 batteries**, so the newer model is not suddenly some ultra-modern ownership revolution.

That matters because it keeps the comparison grounded.

You are mostly paying for a better premium feel, not escaping some huge V6 ownership flaw.
