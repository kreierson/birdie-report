**Ryan Fox** did not back into this thing.

He walked onto the hardest hole at **Royal Birkdale**, tied for the lead, and decided to win **The Open Championship** there instead of asking for a playoff.

According to **Associated Press reporting published Sunday, July 19, 2026**, and **The Open's official final-round coverage and leaderboard**, checked on **Monday, July 20, 2026**, Fox closed in **2-under 68** and finished **10-under par** to win **The 154th Open** by **one shot** over **Cameron Young**. The Open's official report says Fox birdied **four of his last six holes**, including a **12-foot birdie on the 18th**, the toughest hole on the course for the week.

That is not a tidy little major win.

That is someone grabbing the trophy by the throat.

This piece is based on **AP's July 19, 2026 winner report**, plus **The Open's official July 19 day-four recap, late-bloomer feature, and leaderboard**, all checked on **Monday, July 20, 2026**. No pretending I was in Southport with a scorer's badge and a dramatic voice memo from behind the 18th green.

## Fox Won It on the Only Hole That Felt Cruel Enough

The best detail from Sunday is also the simplest one.

Fox did not sneak home with a par while everybody else melted.
He made the winning birdie on **18**.

The Open's official final-round report says **Royal Birkdale's 18th** played at **161-over par** for the championship and had yielded only **four birdies** before Fox arrived in the final group. AP reported Fox told caddie **Dean Smith** on the tee that they were going to take the hole on and "try to win the tournament."

That call mattered because **Cameron Young** had already posted **9-under** and sat on the range for more than two hours waiting to see whether anyone could catch him. Fox got to the last tied at **9-under**, hit the fairway, stuffed a **9-iron from 175 yards** to about **12 feet**, and rolled the putt in anyway.

That is a proper Claret Jug finish.

## Cameron Young Did Almost Everything Right, Then Got Foxed Anyway

This is the part that makes the Sunday board sting a little.

**Young** was brilliant.
He just happened to run into the one finish better than his own.

The Open's official report says Young shot **64**, made the turn in **29**, and got to **10-under** with birdies on both par-5s on the back nine before a closing bogey dropped him to **9-under**. AP added that he spent the last stretch waiting on the range, then heard the roar from **18** and knew the playoff had vanished.

It is a brutal way to lose.

It is also the second time Young has finished runner-up at **The Open**, after **St Andrews in 2022**, according to both AP and The Open's official coverage. That makes the result feel less like a random hard-luck week and more like another serious close call he will have to carry for a while.

## Sam Burns Had the Lead, Then the Front Nine Bit Him

The other obvious Sunday story is the one that never quite got its clean ending.

**Sam Burns** started the day at **10-under**, two clear of the field, after going **62-65** over rounds two and three. But AP reported he opened the final round with **three bogeys in his first four holes**, and The Open's official report says a **72** dropped him to **8-under** and solo third.

That does not erase the week.

Burns still nearly won a major he had not even expected to play until after the early arrival of his daughter. We already covered that turn in [our Sunday preview piece](/blog/sam-burns-open-final-round-birkdale-2026/). But on the actual championship day, he never got the calm front-nine start a leader wants, and from there the board kept getting more crowded, not less.

## Fox's Route Was Weird, Late, and Way More Fun Than the Usual Script

The part I like most is how non-corporate this victory feels.

The Open's official late-bloomer feature says Fox had **never finished inside the top 10 of a major** before this week, turned professional at **25**, was a **DP World Tour rookie at 30**, and a **PGA TOUR rookie at 37**. The same story says he was close to giving the game up early in his career before a week with his father caddying helped steady everything.

Then this week looked half-normal until it did not.

The Open's official report says Fox was only **level par through 36 holes**, just **two inside the cut line**, before a Saturday **62** launched him into the final group. It also says the jump from **T52 on Friday night** to winner is the **largest 36-hole comeback by position in men's major history**.

Golf does not usually hand you a cleaner reminder than that:

- make the cut
- catch fire late
- stare down the hardest hole on the course
- and hit the shot anyway

That is how a great Sunday is supposed to feel.

## Scheffler and Fleetwood Were There, But Not Quite There Enough

The final leaderboard was crowded enough to stay interesting all day.

The Open's official report says **Scottie Scheffler** briefly got to **8-under** before late bogeys and a final-hole mistake left him tied for **fourth** with **Tommy Fleetwood** at **7-under**. Fleetwood gave the local crowd another run, and The Open says he got within **one shot** after birdies on **1, 5, and 8**, only for the middle of the round to wobble.

That is why Fox's finish landed so hard.

The heavyweights were around.
The hometown pressure was loud.
Young had already posted.
Burns started the day in front.

And Fox still took the ugliest, most decisive route available.

## Bottom Line

**Ryan Fox** won **The 154th Open at Royal Birkdale on Sunday, July 19, 2026**, with a closing **68** and a birdie on the brutal **18th hole**, according to **AP's winner report** and **The Open's official day-four coverage** checked on **Monday, July 20**.

He beat **Cameron Young** by one shot, finished at **10-under**, and turned a week that looked ordinary at halfway into the first major title of his career.

That is a big-boy major win, not a paperwork victory.
