If you already know you want a Garmin golf watch, this is the question that matters.

Not Garmin versus Bushnell.

Not Garmin versus Apple.

**Garmin Approach S70 vs Garmin Approach S44** is the real fork in the road, because both watches give you the Garmin look, Garmin course coverage, and Garmin ecosystem without asking you to leave the brand. The problem is that one costs premium-watch money and the other costs merely annoying money.

This is a research-based comparison built from current Garmin feature sets, listed pricing, and how both watches are positioned in the broader GPS-watch market. No fake "I wore both for four months and became one with the satellites" nonsense.

![Garmin Approach S70 golf watch](https://res.garmin.com/en/products/010-02746-02/g/cf-lg.jpg)
*Image: Garmin*

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Garmin Approach S44** if you want the smarter purchase. It gives you the nicer Garmin screen-and-ecosystem experience without making you spend driver money on a wrist gadget.

Buy the **Garmin Approach S70** if you play enough to actually use the premium stuff. If **Virtual Caddie**, **PlaysLike**, better course interaction, and stronger daily-watch utility sound like things you will genuinely use, the S70 is still the best Garmin golf watch.

For most golfers, I would recommend the **S44**.

For golfers who play a lot, love data, and want the fullest Garmin experience, I would recommend the **S70**.

## Price: This Is Why the Decision Exists

| | Garmin Approach S70 | Garmin Approach S44 |
|---|---|---|
| **Current price** | $649.99-$699.99 | $299.99 |
| **Display** | AMOLED | AMOLED |
| **Course coverage** | 43,000+ courses | 43,000+ courses |
| **Headline feature** | Virtual Caddie and premium all-in-one experience | cleaner mid-price Garmin value |
| **Best for** | frequent players who want the flagship | golfers who want Garmin without the financial self-harm |

That is a massive gap.

The S70 is more than double the money, and that means the S44 does not need to be better. It just needs to be good enough that most golfers stop caring about the extra stuff.

That is exactly why this is such a good comparison.

## On-Course Experience: S70 Is Better, S44 Is Good Enough

The **Garmin S70** is still the better golf watch.

It gives you:

- the richer on-wrist mapping experience
- more premium interaction during the round
- **PlaysLike** distance
- **Virtual Caddie**
- a broader sense that the watch is doing more thinking with you

That last part is what you are paying for. The S70 is not just a GPS watch. It is Garmin trying to make a luxury golf computer you can wear.

The **S44** is much more straightforward. You still get the clean Garmin interface, the AMOLED display, the big course library, and the core yardage tools most golfers actually use. What you do not get is the extra layer of guidance and premium golf intelligence that makes the S70 feel special.

So yes, the S70 wins this category.

The real question is whether it wins by **three hundred and fifty to four hundred dollars** for the way you actually play.

## The Premium Features: This Is the S70's Entire Case

The strongest argument for the **S70** is that it gives you features the S44 simply does not.

The biggest ones are:

- **Virtual Caddie**
- **PlaysLike** yardages
- better overall off-course smartwatch and fitness utility
- a more complete flagship feel

If you are the kind of golfer who plays enough for the watch to learn your distances, trusts data-driven club selection, and wants the watch to live on your wrist beyond the course, that matters.

If you are mostly using your watch to get yardages, check hazards, and keep moving, a lot of the S70 story turns into really expensive optional drama.

That is why the S44 exists, and why it makes sense.

## Everyday Wearability: S70 Wins More Clearly

This is one area where the S70 separates itself pretty hard.

The **S70** has the stronger case as a real everyday watch. Better fitness utility, better premium feel, better "I might actually wear this when I am not trying to hit a 7-iron" energy.

The **S44** is still a nicer wearable than a lot of golf-first devices, and that is part of why it is appealing. But it still feels closer to a golf watch that happens to be pleasant than a flagship wearable that also happens to be excellent for golf.

If you want one watch to cover golf, workouts, and normal life, the **S70** earns more of its price here than anywhere else.

## Value: S44 Keeps Punching This Thing in the Face

This is where the **S44** becomes hard to ignore.

At **$299.99**, it keeps the most obvious Garmin wins:

- AMOLED display
- Garmin course ecosystem
- large course coverage
- polished app and product experience

That is enough for a lot of buyers.

The S70 is the better product. But once a golf watch gets into the $650-$700 range, it has to do more than be better. It has to be better in a way you will actually notice often enough to care.

For the average golfer, I do not think that bar gets cleared.

## Who Should Buy the Garmin Approach S70

Buy the **S70** if:

- you play enough to benefit from Virtual Caddie and premium course tools
- you want the best Garmin golf-watch experience, not the best deal
- you also care about fitness tracking and daily smartwatch use
- you are comfortable paying flagship money for flagship polish

It also makes sense if you are already cross-shopping premium alternatives like the [Garmin S70 vs Shot Scope V5 comparison](/blog/garmin-approach-s70-vs-shot-scope-v5/) and know you want the nicer watch.

## Who Should Buy the Garmin Approach S44

Buy the **S44** if:

- you want Garmin without spending an absurd amount
- you care about a good display and clean software more than advanced caddie features
- you mainly want dependable GPS help, not a golf-tech flex
- you would rather keep three hundred extra dollars in play for rounds, balls, or a [rangefinder](/blog/best-rangefinders-2026/)

It is also the better stepping stone if you were already considering mid-price options like the [Bushnell iON Elite vs Garmin Approach S44 comparison](/blog/bushnell-ion-elite-vs-garmin-approach-s44/).

## Final Verdict

The **Garmin Approach S70** is the better watch.

The **Garmin Approach S44** is the better buy for most golfers.

That is the whole answer.

If you are a frequent player who wants the full premium Garmin experience and will actually use the smarter course tools, buy the **S70**.

If you want the clean Garmin experience without making yourself explain a $700 golf watch purchase, buy the **S44**.

My pick for the average golfer is the **Garmin Approach S44** because it keeps enough of Garmin's appeal without wandering into luxury-golf-watch nonsense.
