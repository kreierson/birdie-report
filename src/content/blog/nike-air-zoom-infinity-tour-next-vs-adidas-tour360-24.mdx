This is one of those premium-shoe decisions where the last **$21** matters less than the kind of golfer you actually are.

The **adidas Tour360 24** are the smarter buy for most direct shoppers because the on-course case is stronger, the weather case is stronger, and the price gap versus Nike is tiny.

The **Nike Air Zoom Infinity Tour NEXT%** only become the better answer if you know you want the lighter-feeling athletic-spikeless lane and do not want a more committed spike-based shoe in your life.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **adidas Tour360 24** if you want the smarter premium golf-shoe recommendation in this exact matchup.

Buy the **Nike Air Zoom Infinity Tour NEXT%** if you care more about athletic feel, lighter spikeless comfort, and style than you do about wet-condition traction and a stronger support-first platform.

My lean is **adidas Tour360 24**.

That is not because Nike are not good. It is because the adidas solve more golf problems, and they do it for almost the same money.

If the Tour360 were dramatically more expensive, the Nike value case would get louder.

If the Nike were dramatically cheaper, the "just buy the athletic spikeless shoe" case would be easier.

Instead, the prices sit close enough that this turns into a question of purpose.

## Why the Tour360 24 Get the Recommendation

The **Tour360 24** win because the reasons to buy them are easier to defend.

From the existing [adidas Tour360 24 review](/blog/adidas-tour360-24-review/), the product story is pretty coherent:

- stronger traction
- more planted support
- waterproof leather
- spike-first confidence without feeling like an old brick

That matters because golfers spending close to **$200** usually want the shoe to solve a real on-course problem.

The Tour360 solve more of those.

If you:

- play in dew
- deal with soft turf
- like feeling locked in through the swing
- or simply want the cleaner performance answer

the adidas are the better use of the money.

## Why Nike Still Have a Real Case

The **Infinity Tour NEXT%** are still the better answer for one very specific golfer:

the golfer who hates conservative golf shoes and wants something that feels closer to a performance trainer.

Nike still win on:

- athletic look
- livelier ride
- lighter-feeling spikeless ownership
- the whole "I actually want to wear these" factor

That is not trivial.

A lot of golfers talk themselves into the technically correct shoe and then keep reaching for the pair that feels more fun.

If you already know that is you, the Nike case gets much more legitimate.

## Walking Comfort and Day-to-Day Use: Nike Win

This is the category where the **Infinity Tour NEXT%** hit back hardest.

The shoe's whole value is that it feels more:

- active
- trainer-like
- easygoing
- and less like a fully committed golf platform

That makes it the easier answer for golfers who mostly play:

- normal-condition public golf
- walking rounds
- range sessions
- casual golf trips where one more spike-only pair feels like too much baggage

The [Nike review](/blog/nike-air-zoom-infinity-tour-next-review/) already makes clear that this is not the broad safest shoe in the market. But it is still the more relaxed, more athletic, more fun ownership experience in this exact comparison.

**Edge: Nike Air Zoom Infinity Tour NEXT%**

## Traction and Conditions Fit: Tour360 Win Clearly

This part is not especially close.

The **Tour360 24** use:

- screw-in spikes
- a stronger support chassis
- waterproof leather
- a more locked-in feel under pressure

The **Nike** are absolutely playable in normal golf conditions. But they are not the shoe I would pick first for:

- wet mornings
- shoulder-season golf
- hilly lies
- or the golfer whose main shopping word is "traction"

That is why the Tour360 keep looking like the adult purchase here.

**Edge: adidas Tour360 24**

## Versatility Versus Specialization

This is the part most buyers need to be honest about.

The **Nike** are more versatile in the casual sense:

- easier walking feel
- less golf-shoe hardware
- more crossover athletic personality

The **adidas** are more versatile in the golf sense:

- broader weather use
- stronger footing
- more trustworthy support
- less need to second-guess the shoe when conditions get weird

So the question is not:

Which shoe is more versatile?

The question is:

Versatile for what?

- you mostly play in normal conditions and do not need spikes

## Final Verdict

The **Nike Air Zoom Infinity Tour NEXT%** are the more exciting shoe.

The **adidas Tour360 24** are the smarter shoe for most golfers comparing these two directly.

That is the real answer.

If you want the sharper athletic-spikeless personality and mostly play in normal conditions, buy the **Nike**.

If you want the stronger performance case, better traction, and a premium shoe that solves more actual golf problems for almost the same money, buy the **Tour360 24**.
