TaylorMade looked at a putter line that was already beating everybody up and said, yeah, let's make more of those.

New **TaylorMade Spider prototypes** were spotted at the **RBC Heritage** this week, which is exactly what you'd expect after the Spider family turned into the hottest flatstick ecosystem on Tour. This isn't a random equipment-nerd side quest anymore. This is the putter story of the season.

According to **Golf Monthly**, TaylorMade's Spider line had been used in **five of the first six PGA Tour wins** entering Heritage week. **Golf.com** pushed that even further after Rory McIlroy's Masters win, noting that the company had piled up **six wins by five different players** already in 2026. Either way, the point is obvious: the damn things are everywhere.

## Why This Matters More Than Normal Tour-Van Gossip

Usually, "prototype spotted" stories are fun but mostly for gear sickos who enjoy zooming in on grainy photos and pretending they can identify hosel geometry from 40 yards away.

This one matters because the Spider line already has actual results behind it.

Rory McIlroy just won another Masters with a **Spider Tour X**. Scottie Scheffler has already proven a Spider switch can reshape the ceiling of an elite player. Collin Morikawa and Brooks Koepka have both been mentioned as recent Spider adopters or testers. Tommy Fleetwood's move into the family helped kick off the best summer of his career.

When that many world-class players keep circling the same putter family, it stops being a trend and starts looking like a market takeover.

## What Got Spotted at Harbour Town

Golf Monthly reported that TaylorMade's proto lineup at Harbour Town includes fresh Spider shapes beyond the established Tour and Tour X heads, with **Spider Tour F** and **Spider Tour V** among the models drawing attention. The finish also appears to lean into that bronze-style look that has already become closely associated with Rory's current gamer.

That matters for one simple reason: TaylorMade is not treating Spider like one product. They're treating it like a full-on platform.

And honestly, that's the smart play.

Golfers don't all want the same look at address. Some want a cleaner compact mallet. Some want more fang shape. Some want a little more visual help without feeling like they're putting with a spaceship. If Spider is going to keep steamrolling the market, it needs to give players different flavors of the same basic promise: stability, alignment help, and enough confidence to stop looking like a hostage over four-footers.

## Rory's Masters Win Poured Gas on the Fire

The timing here is not subtle.

Golf.com reported that Rory's putter rep, James Holley, was already in the TaylorMade truck at RBC Heritage watching McIlroy finish off his second straight Masters win. That same story said Spider putters had won **all but two Masters since 2020** and were the winningest putter family on Tour last season.

So if you're TaylorMade, why would you slow down now?

You wouldn't. You'd show up at a Signature Event with more Spider stuff and let every curious Tour pro wander over like moths to a bronze-colored flame.

## The Big Picture

This is the gear version of when one offense in the NFL starts cooking and then suddenly half the league copies the same concepts by October.

The Spider line is no longer just Rory's putter, or Scottie's putter, or that mallet your buddy swears he putts better with. It's becoming the default answer when elite players want more forgiveness without fully giving up shape preferences.

And that's a problem for basically everyone else in the putter market.

Scotty Cameron still has the aura. Odyssey still has depth. L.A.B. still owns the weird-smart corner of the internet. But TaylorMade has the thing every company wants and almost nobody gets: a product family that feels both proven and trendy at the exact same time.

That's a nasty combo.
