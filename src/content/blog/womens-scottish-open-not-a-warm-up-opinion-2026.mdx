The **Women's Scottish Open** is one of those golf weeks people keep underselling out of habit.

You can already hear the framing:

nice field, nice venue, nice little tune-up before the **AIG Women's Open**.

No. Screw that.

According to the **LPGA's official July 20, 2026 field announcement**, **field breakdown**, **watch guide**, and the tour's current **tournaments page**, all checked on **Tuesday, July 21, 2026**, this week's **ISPS HANDA Women's Scottish Open** at **Dundonald Links** has **seven of the Rolex top 10**, sits as the **LPGA's only Scotland stop of the season**, and hands out **500 Race to CME Globe points** before next week's **AIG Women's Open** at **Royal Lytham & St Annes**, which the same tournaments page lists at **$10 million** and **650 points**.

That is not a courtesy lap.

That is the front half of the best two-week summer runway the LPGA has right now.

## The Warm-Up Label Gets the Direction Wrong

I get why people do it.

The **AIG Women's Open** is next.
It is the bigger purse.
It is the major.
It is **Royal Lytham & St Annes**.

So the temptation is to flatten the Scottish Open into "the week before the real week."

But that is stupid calendar thinking.

The better way to read this stretch is not:

- one week that matters
- preceded by one week that mostly does not

It is:

- one meaningful tournament at **Dundonald**
- followed immediately by one even bigger tournament at **Royal Lytham**
- which together give the LPGA a rare chance to hold the same part of the sports conversation for more than 72 hours

That is what good sports calendars are supposed to do.

## Seven of the Top 10 Should End the "Placeholder Week" Talk by Itself

The LPGA's official **field breakdown** says this week's field includes:

- **Nelly Korda**
- **Hyo Joo Kim**
- **Lottie Woad**
- **Miyu Yamashita**
- **Charley Hull**
- **Hannah Green**
- **Sei Young Kim**

That is **seven of the world top 10**.

You do not get to call that a filler week unless you are determined to sound like somebody who only checks a calendar after the trophy ceremony.

And it is not just ranking vanity either. The same LPGA field breakdown says the field also includes **nine 2026 LPGA winners** and **13 2026 LET winners**. That means the week is full of players who already know what closing looks like this season.

That should be enough competitive credibility for anyone.

## Scotland Should Matter as More Than a Scenic Bridge

This part annoys me most.

The LPGA's official field story says this is the tour's **only event in Scotland** in **2026**. It also says last year's week drew **more than 11,000 spectators** and that this is the **fifth straight year** the event has been held at **Dundonald Links**.

So why does golf still talk about it like a lobby before the ballroom?

This event has:

- a stable venue identity
- a real local hook
- a repeatable crowd
- a strong field
- and a direct line into the final women's major of the year

That is a feature, not a flaw.

The week should feel connected to the AIG.
It just should not feel subordinate.

## This Is Also the Right Way to Sell the LPGA's Summer Story

The LPGA is at its best when it stops atomizing itself into isolated little media moments.

We have already written versions of that argument all year:

- [Nelly Korda's Hall of Fame chase](/blog/nelly-korda-hall-of-fame-chase-opinion-2026/) gave the season a real through-line
- [Lottie Woad's second LPGA win](/blog/lottie-woad-second-lpga-win-opinion-2026/) demanded more aggressive star framing
- [our structural-growth piece](/blog/lpga-growth-finally-looks-structural-2026/) argued that women's golf works better when the institutions stop acting small

This is the same conversation.

If the LPGA wants a bigger footprint, the answer is not always to invent a new concept. Sometimes the answer is just to recognize the good sequence already sitting on the schedule and push it like it matters.

Scottish Open into AIG is one of those sequences.

## The AIG Week Gets Better If Scotland Feels Important First

This is the other upside people miss.

Treating the Scottish Open seriously does not steal oxygen from the AIG.
It builds oxygen for the AIG.

If viewers spend four days watching **Korda**, **Woad**, **Hull**, **Hannah Green**, **Hyo Joo Kim**, and the rest trade punches at **Dundonald**, then next week's major does not start from zero. It starts with active tension. It starts with carryover. It starts with players already feeling like current characters instead of names reintroduced by a graphics package.

That is how sports retain attention.

The LPGA's current tournament page makes the staging opportunity really obvious:

- **July 23-26, 2026**: **Women's Scottish Open**, **$2 million**, **500 points**
- **July 30-August 2, 2026**: **AIG Women's Open**, **$10 million**, **650 points**

That is not a dead week and a live week.

That is a built-in two-step.

## Golf Still Has a Bad Habit of Acting Like Women's Events Need One Designated "Main Week"

This might be the dumbest old reflex in the whole coverage ecosystem.

Men's golf gets treated like a long-running ecosystem where momentum can travel from one week into the next.

Women's golf still too often gets presented like it must wait for one approved spotlight window, and everything around that window is just context.

That is backwards.

If anything, women's golf needs *more* connected event framing, not less. It needs more weeks where fans are told, correctly, that the current tournament matters because it is good *and* because it affects what comes next.

This Scottish-AIG pairing is exactly that.

## Bottom Line

The **Women's Scottish Open** should not be treated like a warm-up week people politely endure before the **AIG Women's Open** arrives.

The **LPGA's official July 20 field and schedule updates**, checked on **Tuesday, July 21, 2026**, show a Dundonald week with **seven of the world top 10**, **nine 2026 LPGA winners**, and the tour's **only stop in Scotland**, immediately followed by a **$10 million AIG Women's Open** at **Royal Lytham & St Annes**.

That is not preamble.

That is a real two-week stage, and golf should sell it like one.
