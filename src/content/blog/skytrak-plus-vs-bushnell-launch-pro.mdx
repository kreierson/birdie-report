# SkyTrak+ vs Bushnell Launch Pro: Which Premium Launch Monitor Is the Smarter Buy?

*Image: SKYTRAK Golf*

This is a research-based comparison built from current official SKYTRAK and Bushnell product pages plus the surrounding Birdie Report launch-monitor coverage as of **April 26, 2026**. No fake "I had both in my garage all winter" garbage.

## Quick Verdict

If you are buying **brand-new today** and want the easiest premium recommendation, buy the **Bushnell Launch Pro**.

If you find a **SkyTrak+** at a real discount and you care more about lowering the buy-in than having the cleanest premium ownership path, the SkyTrak+ still has a case.

The **better product** is the Launch Pro.

The **better bargain, when it is actually available**, can be the SkyTrak+.

That availability caveat is not a footnote. It is the whole damn comparison.

## The First Problem: One of These Is Easier to Buy Than the Other

Bushnell's current Launch Pro page lists the device at **$2,499.99** and presents it like a live, supported, premium product with clear software tiers and current stock.

SKYTRAK's current ST+ page shows a **$1,995 sale price** but also says the launch monitor is **no longer available**.

That means this is not just a spec-sheet comparison. It is a **shopping reality** comparison.

If your question is "which one should I buy new right now without turning this into an eBay side quest?" the answer is the **Launch Pro**.

If your question is "which one is more interesting if I find discounted inventory or a strong used-market price?" the answer gets more interesting, because the SkyTrak+ becomes a more legitimate value play.

## What Each Product Is Actually Selling

The **Bushnell Launch Pro** sells confidence.

Bushnell's current page leans hard into:

- camera-based measurement
- an auto-calibrating **three-camera** system with infrared
- stronger trust in launch, carry, and spin data
- premium indoor and outdoor performance

The **SkyTrak+** sells balance.

SKYTRAK's current ST+ page positions it around:

- **dual doppler radar**
- an improved **photometric camera system**
- indoor and outdoor use
- useful club and ball data
- a simulator-and-improvement platform aimed below full premium pricing

So this is not really "which one is better?"

It is "do you want the cleaner premium answer or the more tempting lower-cost premium-ish answer?"

## Why the Launch Pro Still Feels More Serious

The Launch Pro has a cleaner case because Bushnell is more explicit about what you are paying for.

You are paying for:

- stronger data confidence
- a more premium simulator foundation
- a product that feels designed for golfers who will actually build around it

That is why it makes so much sense for:

- serious home simulator setups
- gapping work
- fitting-style use
- golfers who will absolutely obsess over whether the cheaper option is close enough

For the full Bushnell case, read the dedicated [Bushnell Launch Pro review](/blog/bushnell-launch-pro-review/).

## Why the SkyTrak+ Still Has a Real Case

The SkyTrak+ is interesting because it lives in the annoying zone where the product may be harder to buy cleanly, but the proposition is still appealing.

SKYTRAK says the ST+ combines radar and photometric tracking, supports indoor and outdoor use, and provides a broader improvement environment with game-improvement features and membership options.

That matters because if you find one at the right price, the SkyTrak+ is not some random budget compromise. It is still a serious launch-monitor product.

The problem is that "still a serious product" is not the same as "easy premium recommendation in late April 2026."

## Software and Ongoing Cost Are Both Annoying

Neither of these is a pure one-time-purchase fairy tale.

Bushnell's current Launch Pro page shows:

- **Silver** at **$199/year**
- **Gold** at **$499/year**
- club data access tied to subscription paths

SKYTRAK's current ST+ page says the basic product comes with limited included access, while advanced game-improvement and course-play tools depend on paid membership plans.

So if your dream scenario is "I spend a pile of money once and never think about software again," neither company is doing cartwheels to help you.

Bushnell's software path is more expensive but cleaner.

SKYTRAK's software path is less terrifying at first glance, but the product's availability situation muddies the recommendation.

## Indoor Simulator Buyer vs. Deal Hunter Buyer

This is the split that matters most.

### Buy the Launch Pro if you are the indoor simulator buyer

The Launch Pro is the stronger answer if your launch monitor is the centerpiece of the setup.

That is especially true if you want:

- premium ball-data confidence
- a more straightforward premium ecosystem
- the cleaner buy-new-now recommendation

If you are still deciding whether you should spend this much at all, read [Garmin R10 vs Bushnell Launch Pro](/blog/garmin-r10-vs-bushnell-launch-pro/) and [Best Golf Launch Monitors 2026](/blog/best-golf-launch-monitors-2026/) before swiping the card.

### Buy the SkyTrak+ if you are the deal hunter buyer

The SkyTrak+ makes more sense if:

- you find available inventory at a real discount
- you want a lower entry point into this tier
- you are comfortable living with more shopping friction to save money

That is a narrower lane, but it is a real lane.

## Who Should Buy the SkyTrak+

Buy the **SkyTrak+** if:

- you can actually find one
- the price is meaningfully lower than a Launch Pro
- you want premium-adjacent launch-monitor performance without paying Bushnell money
- you are comfortable accepting a messier product-status picture in exchange for value

## Who Should Buy the Launch Pro

Buy the **Launch Pro** if:

- you want the straightforward premium recommendation
- you care a lot about trust-the-number confidence
- you are building a serious simulator or gapping setup
- you would rather pay more for the cleaner answer than spend weeks chasing a deal

## Final Verdict

The **Bushnell Launch Pro** is the right answer for most golfers shopping this comparison in **April 2026**, because it is still clearly available, clearly supported, and clearly positioned as the more serious premium product.

The **SkyTrak+** is the more interesting answer only if you can actually buy one at a compelling number and know exactly why you are accepting the tradeoffs.

So the shortest honest version is this:

- **Launch Pro if you want the clean premium answer**
- **SkyTrak+ if you find a legit bargain and want to save money without dropping all the way down a tier**

That may sound less romantic than some giant spec table, but it is the kind of answer that keeps you from making an expensive dumb decision.
