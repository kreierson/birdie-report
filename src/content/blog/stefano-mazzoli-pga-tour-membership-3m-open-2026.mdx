The **3M Open** just picked up one more useful reason to pay attention.

According to the **PGA TOUR's official announcement published Wednesday, July 22, 2026**, checked on **Thursday, July 23**, **Stefano Mazzoli** has officially accepted **PGA TOUR membership** and will make his first start as a member at this week's **3M Open** in **Blaine, Minnesota**. The same TOUR announcement said **South Africa's Casey Jarvis** accepted **PGA TOUR Special Temporary Membership**.

That is the kind of mid-summer membership churn that matters more than people think, especially when the TOUR says this week's stop at **TPC Twin Cities** is the **32nd of 34 events** in the **FedExCup Regular Season**.

This piece is based on the **PGA TOUR's official July 22, 2026 membership announcement**, plus the TOUR's current late-season storyline framing and the **3M Open's official tournament information**, all checked on **Thursday, July 23, 2026**. No pretending I was in Ponte Vedra processing paperwork by hand.

## Mazzoli's Timing Is the Story

Mazzoli did not accept membership in some sleepy corner of the calendar.

He did it the day before a tournament week that now sits right on top of playoff math, job-security math, and category-positioning math.

The TOUR's announcement ties his first member start directly to the **3M Open**, and that matters because this is not just a ceremonial badge swap. This is a player stepping into the current TOUR pressure cooker right as the regular season is nearly gone.

That gives the story a lot more bite than the usual "player accepts status" headline people skim and forget.

## Casey Jarvis Makes the Same General Point From a Different Lane

Jarvis taking **Special Temporary Membership** is not identical to Mazzoli accepting full membership, but it points in the same broader direction:

- the late-season TOUR map is still moving
- upward mobility is still live
- and the current structure is forcing players to act quickly when a real opening appears

That is why this update lands as more than admin.

It is another sign that the PGA TOUR's global and developmental pathways are not abstract talking points right now. They are actively changing who gets access, who gets starts, and who gets to turn a good season elsewhere into meaningful opportunity on this circuit.

We just wrote about the larger system piece in [the Asian Tour / PGA TOUR / DP World Tour alliance story](/blog/asian-tour-pga-dp-world-alliance-2029/). This Mazzoli-Jarvis update is the player-level version of the same basic truth: golf's pathway language matters a lot more when the season gets short.

## Why the 3M Open Is a Better Debut Spot Than It Looks

The **3M Open's official tournament page**, checked on **July 23**, lists the event running **July 20-26, 2026** at **TPC Twin Cities** and describes it as an official **PGA TOUR** stop that is part of the **FedEx Cup series**.

That sounds obvious, but it is worth restating because post-major weeks get treated like dead air way too often.

They are not dead air.
They are sorting mechanisms.

That is especially true this week, because the TOUR's own current late-season package says only **three Regular Season events** remain before the **FedExCup Playoffs**. So Mazzoli is not just arriving on TOUR. He is arriving when the whole board gets tighter.

## This Is Also a Small Reminder That the PGA TOUR Season Still Has Churn

The easy lazy storyline after **The Open** is that the real season is sort of over until Memphis.

That is nonsense.

These membership decisions are exactly the kind of thing that proves the opposite. The weeks after the majors are where players still:

- grab access
- lose access
- change categories
- and try to convert one breakthrough into something durable

That is not filler.
That is professional golf doing its actual labor.

We saw a different version of this earlier in [our July 1 piece on Jackson Koivun's pro debut at the John Deere Classic](/blog/john-deere-classic-field-jackson-koivun-pro-debut-2026/). Koivun's story was about first-step intrigue. Mazzoli and Jarvis feel more like compression. There is less runway now, which makes every move heavier.

## Bottom Line

**Stefano Mazzoli** officially accepted **PGA TOUR membership** on **Wednesday, July 22, 2026**, and the TOUR said his first start as a member will come immediately at this week's **3M Open**. The same announcement said **Casey Jarvis** accepted **Special Temporary Membership**.

With the **3M Open** serving as the **32nd of 34 FedExCup Regular Season events**, this is not just housekeeping. It is a timely reminder that the late PGA TOUR summer is still where careers keep moving, even after the majors are done hogging the light.
