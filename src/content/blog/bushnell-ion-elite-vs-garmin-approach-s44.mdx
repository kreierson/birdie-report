The **Bushnell iON Elite** and **Garmin Approach S44** are a good example of why golf tech shopping gets stupid fast.

One watch says, "here's the golf stuff that actually matters, including slope, and we're not going to overcharge you for it."

The other says, "yeah, but what if your watch felt a lot nicer to own?"

That is the real debate.

This comparison is based on current pricing, listed specs, feature sets, and the broader reputation both brands have with golfers. No fake range-session fairy tales, no pretending I wore both for six months and achieved enlightenment.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Bushnell iON Elite** if you want the better pure golf value.

Buy the **Garmin Approach S44** if you want the nicer watch and do not mind paying for Garmin's cleaner ecosystem.

For most golfers, I still think the **Bushnell iON Elite** is the smarter buy because slope plus hole-mapping at roughly two hundred bucks is hard to argue with.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

| | Bushnell iON Elite | Garmin Approach S44 |
|---|---|---|
| **Price** | $199.99 | $299.99 |
| **Display** | Color touchscreen | Color display |
| **Slope-adjusted distances** | ✅ | ❌ |
| **Hole and green views** | ✅ | ✅ |
| **Best trait** | pure golf value | better overall polish |
| **Best for** | golfers who want max golf help per dollar | golfers who want a more premium Garmin experience |

This is not a same-price matchup like the [Bushnell iON Elite vs Garmin Approach S12](/bushnell-ion-elite-vs-garmin-approach-s12) debate.

This one is more annoying because Garmin asks you to spend meaningfully more money.

## Golf Features: Bushnell Wins the Value Fight

The **iON Elite** still has one huge card to play, **slope-adjusted distances**.

That matters.

At this price, slope is not some throwaway marketing bullet. It is one of the few features that can actually change club selection for normal golfers. Add in **HoleView**, **GreenView**, and movable pin placement, and the Bushnell makes a very strong argument that it is doing the golf-specific part of the job better per dollar.

That is why it has stayed relevant in our [Bushnell iON Elite review](/bushnell-ion-elite-gps-watch-review) and our guide to the [best golf GPS watches for seniors in 2026](/best-golf-gps-watches-seniors-2026).

If your main question is, "which watch helps me more during the round for less money?" the answer is still **Bushnell iON Elite**.

## Screen, Interface, and Ecosystem: Garmin Wins

This is where **Garmin** usually gets people.

The **Approach S44** feels like the more modern, more polished product. Garmin's whole ecosystem tends to feel cleaner, more mature, and more thought through. The display experience is nicer, the software reputation is better, and the watch itself feels more like something you would actually want on your wrist instead of something you tolerate for golf.

That does not mean the Bushnell is bad.

It means the Bushnell feels more like a tool, and the Garmin feels more like a product.

That distinction matters if you are the kind of buyer who notices interface quality and generally gets irritated by clunky gear.

## Everyday Wearability: Garmin by a Comfortable Margin

A lot of golf watches are basically course appliances.

The **Bushnell iON Elite** kind of lives in that lane. It is useful, but it is not pretending to be some elegant all-day wrist companion.

The **Garmin Approach S44** has the stronger case if you want something you would actually wear beyond the tee sheet. It still is not a full smartwatch replacement, but it has more of that broader Garmin appeal where the watch does not feel trapped inside one use case.

If you want a golf-first tool, Bushnell is fine.

If you want the one that feels better as an actual wearable, **Garmin** wins.

## Battery and Simplicity

Bushnell's battery life is good enough. Garmin's reputation in this category is usually steadier, and Garmin generally does a better job making its devices feel less fussy over time.

I would not buy either of these strictly on battery alone unless you are incredibly weird about charging habits.

The bigger difference is that the **Bushnell** is trying to maximize golf features while the **Garmin** is trying to feel like a better product.

One wins the spec argument. One wins the ownership argument.

## Value: Bushnell Keeps This From Being a Blowout

At **$199.99**, the **Bushnell iON Elite** feels honest.

At **$299.99**, the **Garmin Approach S44** feels like you need to care about Garmin specifically.

That is not a knock. Garmin has earned that brand trust. But this is where the buying decision gets really simple.

If you just want reliable golf help, yardages, slope, and decent visuals, the Bushnell is hard to beat.

If you want the nicer watch, cleaner app ecosystem, and more polished day-to-day feel, the extra hundred bucks for Garmin starts to make more sense.

For golfers who purely care about scoring help, I still think the **Bushnell** has the stronger value case.

## Who Should Buy the Bushnell iON Elite

Buy the **Bushnell iON Elite** if:

- you want the most golf-specific help for the money
- slope matters to you
- you like hole maps and movable pin placement
- you do not care much about wearing the watch off the course
- you want to save a hundred bucks and still get the important golf stuff

## Who Should Buy the Garmin Approach S44

Buy the **Garmin Approach S44** if:

- you want the more polished watch overall
- you trust Garmin's ecosystem more than Bushnell's golf-first approach
- you care about cleaner interface quality and better wearability
- you are okay paying more for a nicer overall ownership experience

## Final Verdict

The **Garmin Approach S44** is the nicer watch.

The **Bushnell iON Elite** is the smarter golf buy.

That is where I land.

If somebody handed me both at the same price, I would probably take the **Garmin S44** because it feels like the cleaner product.

If I am spending my own money, I would buy the **Bushnell iON Elite** and keep the extra hundred bucks for greens fees, golf balls, or literally anything more fun than overpaying for marginal polish.
