This is one of the cleanest golf-tech decisions on the site because both watches are trying to solve the same problem from opposite ends of the Garmin universe.

The **Garmin Approach S70** says: buy the flagship and let your golf watch do almost everything short of reading the putt for you.

The **Garmin Approach S12** says: or stop spending silly smartwatch money and buy the Garmin that just gives you the damn number.

That is a real buyer fork.

This comparison is **research-based** and built from Garmin's current Approach S70 and Approach S12 product positioning, Birdie Report's existing Garmin review cluster, and current market context checked on **June 10, 2026**. Garmin's current S70 product pages still frame the watch around a superbright AMOLED display, **43,000-plus** preloaded courses, Virtual Caddie, and broader smartwatch tools. Garmin's current S12 product pages still frame that watch around **42,000-plus** courses, button-control simplicity, and up-to-**30-hour** GPS battery life. ## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Garmin Approach S12** if you want the smarter Garmin buy for most golfers.

Buy the **Garmin Approach S70** if you play a lot, want one premium watch for golf and everyday wear, and will actually use the extra tools.

My honest split:

- **S12** for most golfers who mainly want fast yardages, huge battery life, and less device drama
- **S70** for golfers who already know they want richer maps, Virtual Caddie, PlaysLike help, and a premium daily smartwatch

For a lot of golfers, the answer will be no.

## Why the S70 Has a Real Case

The **S70** is still the best golf-watch flex in Garmin's lineup because it combines three things the cheaper watches do not:

- premium **AMOLED** readability
- richer hole maps and on-wrist context
- smarter club-selection help through **Virtual Caddie** and **PlaysLike**

That combination matters if you play often and want the watch to do more than confirm basic yardages.

The S70 is not just prettier. It is more complete.

It is also easier to justify if:

- you wear one watch every day
- you care about fitness and health tracking outside golf
- you like using maps and course visuals instead of only front-middle-back numbers
- you want a watch that feels flagship every time you touch it

If that sounds like you, the S70 starts making sense fast.

## Why the S12 Is Still the Smarter Buy

The **S12** remains one of the better golf-tech recommendations on the site because it understands something expensive wearables often forget:

**less can be more useful**

Garmin gives you:

- front, middle, and back distances
- hazard and dogleg yardages
- **Big Numbers** mode
- button controls
- up to **30 hours** of GPS battery life

That is enough for a huge percentage of golfers.

It is also why the **S12** keeps surviving comparisons against products with richer feature sets, including [Shot Scope V5 vs Garmin Approach S12](/blog/shot-scope-v5-vs-garmin-approach-s12/) and [Bushnell Phantom 3 Slope vs Garmin Approach S12](/blog/bushnell-phantom-3-slope-vs-garmin-approach-s12/).

The watch does the golf job cleanly and then gets out of your way.

That is a very strong product pitch.

## Maps and Club Help Versus Simplicity and Battery

This is the whole argument.

Choose the **S70** if you care most about:

- richer course visuals
- premium screen quality
- **Virtual Caddie**
- **PlaysLike** help
- broader smartwatch utility

Choose the **S12** if you care most about:

- simpler controls
- less charging anxiety
- lower spend
- fast yardages without extra menus
- a golf watch that never feels like homework

The **S70** is better if you want more information.

The **S12** is better if you want less friction.

## Which One Helps More Golfers?

If I am recommending blind, it is still the **S12**.

That is not because the **S70** is overrated.

It is because the S70's premium features only matter if you actually want them, trust them, and use them. Plenty of golfers will buy the flagship watch and then use it like a very expensive distance screen.

That is where the S12 gets scary good.

It costs far less, lives longer on a charge, and covers the basic golf job incredibly well.

The S70 is the better watch.

The S12 is the better default recommendation.
