## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Bridgestone Tour B RX** if you swing **under 105 mph** and want the premium ball that is more openly built around that reality.

Buy the **Callaway Chrome Tour** if you want the broader **medium-feel flagship** with a firmer, more neutral premium personality.

For most everyday amateur golfers making this exact comparison, I would lean **Tour B RX** first.

That is because a huge chunk of the golfers shopping this fork are not trying to optimize a mini-tour launch window. They are trying to buy a premium ball that actually fits a normal-person speed profile.

## Why Tour B RX Gets the Early Nod

The **Tour B RX** gets the first recommendation here because it has the clearer fit story for the average amateur.

Bridgestone is not being subtle about what this ball is for:

- golfers under **105 mph**
- premium feel
- premium-ball behavior
- a product that does not pretend every good player swings like a maniac

That is useful.

A lot of premium-ball marketing still talks like the only golfer worth fitting is the one who can hit driver through a back fence.

The RX cuts through that nonsense.

## Feel: This Is the Real Separation

Both balls live in serious-premium territory.

The difference is that the **Chrome Tour** feels like a premium ball built to be a flagship, while the **Tour B RX** feels like a premium ball built to fit a more specific golfer.

That changes the buying logic.

Choose **Chrome Tour** if you want:

- medium feel
- a more neutral premium response
- the cleaner "all-around flagship" identity

Choose **Tour B RX** if you want:

- soft premium feel
- the under-105 fit story
- a ball that makes more sense for moderate swing speeds

That is a much more useful split than pretending golfers only choose premium balls by logo.

## Flight and Long-Game Fit: This Is Where Chrome Tour Pushes Back

The **Chrome Tour** is the better choice if you want the ball that is easier to treat like a normal premium-tour-ball answer.

It is less about one specific speed threshold and more about wanting:

- stable long-game behavior
- lower spin than a soft-premium default
- a firmer, more awake response

That is where Chrome Tour earns its spot.

If you are a stronger player or just know you hate soft-feel premium balls, Chrome Tour is the better fit and this comparison becomes less complicated.

But if you are sitting in the more common amateur lane and asking which premium ball is easier to justify for sub-105 speed, the **Tour B RX** keeps dragging the conversation back toward itself.

## Which Ball Fits Which Golfer

### Buy Chrome Tour if:

- you want the cleaner premium flagship
- medium feel sounds better to you than soft premium feel
- you are not specifically shopping the under-105 lane
- you want the firmer-feeling Callaway answer instead of Chrome Soft

### Buy Tour B RX if:

- you swing under 105 mph and actually want the purchase to reflect that
- you want premium-ball performance without forcing yourself into the broad-flagship lane

## Final Verdict

The **Bridgestone Tour B RX** is the smarter buy for most golfers making this exact comparison.

The **Callaway Chrome Tour** is the better buy for golfers who want the broader flagship profile and already know they do not want the softer moderate-speed route.

So the honest recommendation is simple:

- buy **Tour B RX** if you are the normal-speed premium buyer
- buy **Chrome Tour** if you are the firmer-feel flagship buyer

That is a much better use of your money than blindly grabbing whichever premium box looks more expensive and pretending that counts as fitting.
