## Quick Verdict

For most golfers making this search before buying, I would recommend the **Garmin Approach S44** first.

That is because the **S44**:

- is built around golf first
- costs way less
- gives you Garmin's course ecosystem without smartwatch bloat
- asks you to make fewer compromises on the course

The **Samsung Galaxy Watch Ultra** is the better **Android smartwatch**.

The **Garmin Approach S44** is the better **golf buy**.

That is the useful answer.

## Why the S44 Gets the Recommendation

The **Approach S44** is easier to recommend because it is much easier to explain.

Garmin's current product page positions it as a golf smartwatch with:

- **43,000-plus preloaded courses**
- a **touchscreen display**
- **front, middle, and back** yardages
- and up to **15 hours** of battery life in GPS mode

That is a clear golf product.

It is not pretending to be your expedition computer, health guru, dive companion, and workday notification machine all at once.

If you want a watch that helps on the course without turning the buying decision into lifestyle theater, the **S44** is the better answer.

## Why Samsung Still Has a Real Case

Because some golfers do not actually want a golf watch.

They want a **premium Android smartwatch** that also covers golf.

Samsung's current pages are selling the **Galaxy Watch Ultra** on:

- **titanium** construction
- **10 ATM / IP68** durability
- up to **100 hours** in **Power Saving Mode**
- up to **48 hours** in **Exercise Power Saving Mode**
- a broader outdoors, health, and smartwatch story

That is a good pitch if you already live in Samsung's ecosystem.

It is just not a golf-first pitch.

## The Golf-App Problem Is the Whole Problem

This is the deciding issue.

The **Galaxy Watch Ultra** is not a dedicated golf watch with a built-in golf identity that defines the product.

Golf still arrives through **third-party apps**.

That means:

- extra setup
- extra friction
- less golf-specific workflow clarity
- more chances to realize you paid a lot for something that is still adapting itself to the golf job

If that sounds fine to you because you already wanted Samsung's flagship watch, great.

If you are buying because of golf, the case gets weaker fast.

## Price Makes This Even Easier

This comparison would already lean Garmin even if both watches cost the same.

They do not.

The **Galaxy Watch Ultra** starts at **$649.99 before trade-in**.

The **Garmin Approach S44** sits at **$299.99**.

So Samsung is asking you to spend more than double for the watch that is less directly built around golf.

That does not kill Samsung's case.

It just makes Samsung's case much narrower:

- you want the broader Android smartwatch life
- you care less about a dedicated golf workflow
- and you are comfortable paying the premium for that trade
