The **TaylorMade P790** is the iron golfers buy when they want the premium answer.

The **Callaway Elyte** is the iron golfers buy when they want the honest answer.

That sounds harsher than it is.

Both sets make sense.

This is a **research-based comparison** built from Birdie Report's current iron cluster, the new [TaylorMade P790 irons review](/blog/taylormade-p790-irons-review/), the existing [Callaway Elyte irons review](/blog/callaway-elyte-irons-review/), [Best Irons 2026](/blog/best-irons-2026/), and [Best Irons for Mid Handicappers 2026](/blog/best-irons-mid-handicappers-2026/). No fake "I found inner peace inside a launch bay and the monitor named a winner" nonsense.

If you want the short answer before we get lost in premium-iron self-therapy:

- buy **P790** if you want the broader premium players-distance recommendation
- buy **Elyte** if you want the easier-forgiveness answer that probably lowers scores faster

## Quick Verdict

For **most 10-to-18 handicaps**, I would buy the **Callaway Elyte** first.

For **the 8-to-14 handicap who specifically wants a premium players-distance set and is already striking it well enough to use one honestly**, I would buy the **TaylorMade P790**.

That is the whole split.

The P790 is not automatically "better" because it costs more and looks cooler in a group chat.

The Elyte is not automatically "lesser" because it helps more.

The question is whether you need:

- a more aspirational premium shape with stronger long-iron help
- or a cleaner game-improvement set that covers more normal-human contact

## Why This Matchup Is So Common

The **P790** and **Elyte** attract the same buyer even though they live in slightly different lanes.

That buyer usually wants:

- distance that feels modern
- a shape that does not look embarrassing
- forgiveness that does not turn every miss into a punishment ritual

The difference is how each set gets there.

The **P790** says:

"You can buy the premium players-distance icon and still get usable help."

The **Elyte** says:

"You can stop overcomplicating this and buy the set that helps more right now."

That is why this fork matters.

## Forgiveness and Launch: Elyte Covers More of Your Nonsense

This is the clearest practical difference.

The **Callaway Elyte** is the easier club to hit for the average golfer. It launches higher, covers a wider range of contact quality, and makes less of a moral issue out of imperfect strikes.

That matters more than a lot of golfers want to admit.

If your current iron misses include:

- low-face strikes
- toe misses
- random long-iron panic
- "I swear that felt better than the result looked" contact

the **Elyte** is the safer play.

That is also why the Elyte keeps showing up near the top of the broader forgiving-iron conversation in [Best Irons 2026](/blog/best-irons-2026/) and why it already has cleaner buyer forks against [Ping G440](/blog/callaway-elyte-vs-ping-g440-irons/) and [Srixon ZXi5](/blog/callaway-elyte-vs-srixon-zxi5-irons/).

If your goal is to lower scores, not impress strangers with topline minimalism, the Elyte starts strong here.

## Feel and Set Identity: P790 Feels More Like the Premium Purchase

This is where the **P790** earns its reputation.

The **P790** has the more aspirational shape, the more obvious premium-buy energy, and the better "I finally bought my real irons" pitch.

It also gives you a little more of the modern players-distance mix that golfers actually chase:

- compact visuals
- easy ball speed
- enough help in the longer irons
- a set identity that can stick around as your game gets better

If you care about the psychological side of irons, and everyone does whether they admit it or not, the **P790** is easier to want.

If you want the product-level case first, read the full [TaylorMade P790 irons review](/blog/taylormade-p790-irons-review/).

## Distance and Control: Depends Which Problem You Are Solving

The **P790** is the more energetic, hotter-feeling iron.

The **Elyte** is the more openly helpful iron.

Those are not the same thing.

If your main question is:

"Which one gives me the most premium distance pop without jumping to a full GI look?"

the answer is **P790**.

If your main question is:

"Which one keeps the round together when I am not hitting the middle all day?"

the answer is **Elyte**.

The mistake golfers make here is assuming the more premium-looking set must be the smarter scoring set.

Sometimes it is.

Sometimes it is just the more expensive fantasy.

## Value: This Is Where Elyte Punches Hard

The **Callaway Elyte** does not just cost less.

It costs less while also helping more golfers.

That combination is hard to ignore.

The **P790** can absolutely justify its price for the right buyer. But it has to win with:

- shape
- set identity
- long-term aspirational fit
- the golfer already being ready for it

The **Elyte** gets to win with a simpler argument:

"You can probably hit this better now, and it will usually cost less."

That is a very annoying argument for premium-gear brains.

It is also often the correct one.

## Who Should Buy the P790

Buy the **TaylorMade P790** if:

- you are roughly an 8-14 handicap
- your strike quality is already moving in the right direction
- you want the premium players-distance set that still has real help
- you care about growing into the set more than extracting the easiest possible help today

## Who Should Buy the Elyte

Buy the **Callaway Elyte** if:

- you are roughly a 10-18 handicap
- you still need real forgiveness and easier launch more than boutique feel
- you want a cleaner GI look without pretending you are shopping tour irons
- you would rather buy the iron that helps now than the iron that flatters your self-image

## Final Verdict

For **most golfers comparing these two honestly**, the **Callaway Elyte** is the smarter buy.

It is easier to hit, easier to justify, and easier to recommend without building a whole premium-gear backstory around the purchase.

The **TaylorMade P790** still wins if you are the golfer who:

- wants the premium players-distance icon
- has the strike quality to use it
- values set identity and long-term fit more than broad forgiveness today

That golfer is real.

It is just not every golfer who thinks they are that golfer.
