The **Full Swing KIT** and **Bushnell Launch Pro** are both premium launch monitors.

They are just premium in very different, very golf-brained ways.

The **KIT** says:

"Pay up for a cleaner premium-radar ownership story and stop getting subscription-guilted every time you want to use the thing."

The **Launch Pro** says:

"Pay less up front, accept the software ladder, and get the stronger indoor trust-the-number case."

That is an actual buyer fork.

This comparison is **research-based** and built from Full Swing's official **KIT Launch Monitor** product page and FAQ plus Bushnell Golf's official **Launch Pro** product and launch-monitor pages checked on **July 18, 2026**. No fake "I installed both in my simulator barn and found enlightenment" nonsense.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Bushnell Launch Pro** if you are a serious premium buyer and want the smarter all-around value in this exact tier.

Buy the **Full Swing KIT** only if you specifically want:

- premium radar
- no-unlock core data
- indoor and outdoor flexibility
- a cleaner ownership mood than Bushnell's subscription ladder

That is the honest answer.

The Launch Pro is easier to justify for most premium buyers because it is currently about **$2,000 cheaper upfront** and still makes the stronger indoor-accuracy case. The KIT only becomes the better answer when you value its cleaner radar ownership story enough to pay for it.

## Why the Bushnell Launch Pro Wins for Most Premium Buyers

The simplest reason is also the biggest one:

it is **way cheaper upfront**.

Bushnell's current hardware price is **$2,999.99**. Full Swing's current KIT price is **$4,999**.

That is not a cute little gap.

That is a **two-thousand-dollar gap** before you even start arguing about simulator software or ownership style.

And the annoying thing for Full Swing is that Bushnell is not the weaker product. The Launch Pro still has a very credible premium story:

- camera-based measurement
- strong indoor and simulator credibility
- a better trust-the-number pitch for structured gapping
- serious ball-data depth
- club-data access through the paid software path

So the Launch Pro is not merely cheaper.

It is cheaper **and** still easier to defend for golfers who want premium indoor data confidence.

That is why it remains the smarter first premium click for a lot of shoppers coming out of [Bushnell Launch Pro review](/blog/bushnell-launch-pro-review/), [Bushnell Launch Pro vs FlightScope Mevo+](/blog/bushnell-launch-pro-vs-flightscope-mevo-plus/), and [SkyTrak+ vs Bushnell Launch Pro](/blog/skytrak-plus-vs-bushnell-launch-pro/).

## Why the Full Swing KIT Still Has a Real Lane

Full Swing is not just charging more for a shinier box.

The KIT has a couple of real advantages:

### No-unlock core ownership

Full Swing currently says you get **16 club and ball data points** without needing an unlock.

That is a stronger emotional ownership story than Bushnell's "buy premium hardware, then decide which subscription tier annoys you least" approach.

If that software ladder bugs you on principle, the KIT has immediate appeal.

### Better premium radar identity

The KIT feels like a premium radar product built for golfers who want one unit to move between:

- the range
- indoor practice
- backyard use
- travel

Bushnell's Launch Pro can absolutely go outside. But its whole personality still feels more like a premium indoor-first platform that happens to travel than a premium radar unit meant to live everywhere.

That matters if your launch-monitor use is not going to be locked inside a single simulator room.

### Cleaner app story

Full Swing currently includes a **free cloud-based app** on **iOS and Android** plus **HD swing replay video**.

That makes the day-to-day ownership vibe calmer.

Bushnell's value is still more about what the premium data lets you do. Full Swing's value is more about how much less immediately transactional the premium ownership story feels.

## The Ownership Math Is Where This Gets Interesting

This is the point where a lot of buyers start lying to themselves.

### Bushnell Launch Pro

- hardware: **$2,999.99**
- **Silver**: **$199/year**
- **Gold**: **$499/year**
- **GSPro**: **$250/year** and requires active **Gold**

So yes, Bushnell can absolutely get expensive.

But even with those extra costs, the Launch Pro can still be the smarter buy if the indoor-data confidence is the reason you are shopping premium in the first place.

### Full Swing KIT

- hardware: **$4,999**
- free app and included core data
- **GSPro** and **E6 Connect** compatibility still live behind separately purchased packages
- Full Swing's proprietary gameplay software is tied to **KIT Studio PC packages** with subscription requirements

So the KIT is cleaner, not magically free.

That distinction matters.

If you thought Full Swing was the premium path that lets you stop paying for anything else, that is not really the full picture.
