# Odyssey Ai-ONE Milled vs Scotty Cameron Newport 2: Forgiveness or Fancy Feel?

This is a beautiful little golf argument because both of these putters cost enough to make a sane person question the entire sport.

The **Odyssey Ai-ONE Milled** and the **Scotty Cameron Newport 2** both sit in premium-putter territory. They're both polished, expensive, and aimed at golfers who care way too much about what a putter feels like off the face.

But they're not trying to solve the same problem.

The **Scotty Newport 2** is about feel, craftsmanship, and that pure blade-putter religion golfers never shut up about.

The **Odyssey Ai-ONE Milled** is about giving you premium construction while also admitting an uncomfortable truth: most golfers do not strike every putt flush.

That's why this matchup matters.

## The Quick Verdict

**Buy the Scotty Cameron Newport 2** if you're a confident blade-putter player who values feel above everything and wants the cleanest traditional experience possible.

**Buy the Odyssey Ai-ONE Milled** if you like premium putters but would also enjoy a little help when you miss the center by half a groove, which, let's be honest, happens.

The Scotty is sexier.

The Odyssey is smarter.

## Price: Basically a Draw, Which Makes This More Brutal

|---|---|---|
| **Price** | $449.99 | $449 |
| **Construction** | Milled body with Ai-ONE insert | Milled 303 stainless steel |
| **Head style** | Premium blade / blade-adjacent options | Traditional blade |
| **Alignment help** | Better | Minimal |
| **Mishit forgiveness** | Better | Lower |
| **Resale / status** | Fine | Excellent |
| **Best for** | Golfers who want help without looking cheap | Golfers who want classic blade feel |

Because the prices are basically the same, nobody gets to hide behind value shortcuts here. You're not choosing between cheap and expensive. You're choosing between **two different philosophies at the exact same price**.

## Feel: Scotty Still Has the Magic

The **Newport 2** remains one of the most satisfying putters in golf for a reason. The milled 303 stainless steel construction, the clean topline, the feedback, the sound, the whole thing just feels premium in a way that golfers immediately understand.

On center strikes, the Scotty gives you that crisp, soft-but-not-mushy feedback better players obsess over. It's not fake. The appeal is real.

The **Ai-ONE Milled** feels good too. Better than a lot of golfers expect, actually. Odyssey did a nice job preserving a premium feel even with the insert design. But if your top priority is old-school blade purity, Scotty still owns this category.

**Edge: Scotty Cameron Newport 2**

## Forgiveness: Odyssey Is the Better Real-World Tool

This is where the Odyssey gets interesting.

The whole selling point of the **Ai-ONE insert** is more consistent ball speed when contact drifts away from the middle. That's not sexy, but it is useful. A lot of golfers say they want pure feel, then hit four toe-side putts a round and wonder why everything dies short.

The **Newport 2** is a better feedback machine. The **Ai-ONE Milled** is a better damage-control machine.

If you strike it beautifully all the time, the Scotty's feedback is a feature.

If you don't, and most people don't, Odyssey's built-in forgiveness is just more practical.

**Edge: Odyssey Ai-ONE Milled**

## Alignment: Odyssey Helps, Scotty Assumes You're Already Good

This is one of the most predictable brand differences in golf.

Scotty gives you a clean look and expects you to do the rest. That's great if you're a confident putter and like zero distractions.

Odyssey gives you more visual help at address without making the putter look ridiculous. It's not cartoonish, just more supportive.

For average golfers, that's a meaningful advantage. Better alignment tends to lead to better starting lines, and better starting lines tend to lead to fewer awful misses that you blame on green reading.

The Scotty look is cleaner. The Odyssey setup is easier to use.

**Edge: Odyssey Ai-ONE Milled**

## Craftsmanship and Ownership Feel: Scotty Has the Aura

Let's not do fake objectivity here.

The **Scotty Cameron Newport 2** has an ownership experience people care about. The finish, the headcover, the custom-shop ecosystem, the resale value, the fact that golfers still talk about buying a Scotty like it's some life milestone, all of that is real.

Some of it is irrational, sure. But not all of it. Scotty putters hold value, look incredible, and scratch that premium-equipment itch in a way few clubs do.

Odyssey makes excellent putters. Scotty makes excellent putters that people get weirdly emotional about.

If that stuff matters to you, it matters.

**Edge: Scotty Cameron Newport 2**

## Which One Actually Helps More Golfers Score?

This is the most important part, and also the one premium-putter shoppers try hardest to ignore.

For most golfers, the **Odyssey Ai-ONE Milled** is the putter that will probably lead to more makeable second putts and fewer maddening distance-control misses. That's because forgiveness and alignment help are not fake advantages.

The **Scotty Newport 2** rewards a skilled, repeatable stroke more beautifully. But it asks more from you.

That's the whole decision.

Do you want a putter that rewards precision?

Or do you want a putter that helps when precision slips a little?

- You like cleaner alignment support at address

## The Verdict

The **Scotty Cameron Newport 2** is the more iconic putter.

The **Odyssey Ai-ONE Milled** is the more practical putter.

If you're a really good putter, or a golfer who genuinely values feel above everything else, the Scotty makes sense. It's a classic for a reason.

If you're a normal golfer shopping in a premium lane and trying to make the smartest choice instead of the most emotional one, the Odyssey is hard to ignore. It gives you premium construction, a strong feel story, and more forgiveness where it actually counts.

My take? **The Scotty is the cooler buy. The Odyssey is the better recommendation for more golfers.**
