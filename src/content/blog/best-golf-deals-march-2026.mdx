## 🔥 Deal of the Week: Callaway Rogue ST Max Driver — $249.99

This is a steal. The [Callaway Rogue ST Max](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=callaway+rogue+st+max+driver&tag=birdiereport-20) was a $529 driver when it launched, and it's still an absolute gamer. The Jailbreak AI Speed Frame delivers genuinely impressive ball speeds, and the forgiveness on off-center hits is outstanding.

Is it the newest model? No. Does it perform within 5 yards of the newest model? Also yes. For $250, you're getting 95% of the performance at less than half the price. That's the kind of math I can get behind.

**Who it's for:** Mid-to-high handicappers who want a forgiving, long driver without paying flagship prices. Low handicappers who don't feel like spending $600 on marginal gains.

## TaylorMade TP5 Golf Balls (Prior Gen) — $69.99 for Two Dozen

The [TaylorMade TP5](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=taylormade+tp5+golf+balls&tag=birdiereport-20) is a legitimate tour-quality golf ball, and at $35 per dozen for the previous generation, it's significantly cheaper than the current Pro V1 ($57.99/dozen) while offering comparable performance.

The five-layer construction delivers excellent spin separation — lower spin off the driver, higher spin on approach shots. It's exactly what you want in a premium ball, minus the premium price tag.

I played the TP5 for an entire season and genuinely couldn't tell the difference from a Pro V1 in any meaningful way. Your mileage may vary, but at this price, it's worth finding out.

## Titleist Vokey SM9 Wedge — $119.99

Vokey wedges are the gold standard for a reason, and the [SM9](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=titleist+vokey+sm9+wedge&tag=birdiereport-20) at $120 is a fantastic deal. These were $149 at launch and many shops still sell them at full price.

The spin performance on the SM9 is excellent — those micro-grooves grab the ball and create the kind of check-and-release action that makes you feel like a tour pro. Available in multiple grind options, so you can match it to your swing type and course conditions.

**Pro tip:** If you're between grinds, the M grind is the most versatile for the widest range of players and conditions. Don't overthink it.

## Garmin Approach S12 GPS Watch — $149.99

GPS watches have gotten ridiculously expensive, with some models pushing $500+. The [Garmin Approach S12](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=garmin+approach+s12+golf+gps&tag=birdiereport-20) gives you accurate front/middle/back distances for over 42,000 courses at a price that makes sense.

It's not going to give you slope-adjusted distances or swing analysis. But honestly? Do you need that? A reliable yardage to the center of the green is 90% of what a GPS device needs to do, and the S12 does it well. Battery life is solid, the display is easy to read in sunlight, and it doubles as a regular watch.

If you're still walking up to sprinkler heads to check yardages, do yourself a favor.

## How I Find These Deals

People ask me this all the time. Honestly, it's not complicated — I check Amazon, Golf Galaxy, and a few other retailers several times a week. The best deals tend to hit when new models launch (retailers clear out prior-gen stock) and during seasonal transitions.

The biggest tip I can give you: **last year's model is almost always the best value in golf.** Manufacturers make incremental improvements each year, but the performance gap between this year's flagship and last year's is tiny. The price gap, however, is enormous.

A $550 driver marked down to $250 is a better buy than a $600 driver at full price, almost without exception.
