## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Garmin Approach S50** if you are buying **because of golf**.

Buy the **Samsung Galaxy Watch Ultra** if you are buying **because you want Samsung's flagship smartwatch anyway**, and golf is one of several jobs it needs to handle.

For most golfers making this exact search before buying, I would recommend the **Garmin Approach S50** first.

That is because the **S50** is the cleaner golf product and the cheaper product.

## Why the S50 Gets the Recommendation for Most Golfers

The **Garmin Approach S50** wins because it is already built around the golf job.

You are not adding golf later.

You are not hoping the app layer feels smooth enough.

You are not paying extra for a broader smartwatch identity you may not need.

The S50 already gives you:

- **PlaysLike distance**
- **hazard view**
- **green view**
- **43,000-plus courses**
- a clean golf-first interface
- enough health and wellness tracking to justify wearing it beyond the course

That is why the [full S50 review](/blog/garmin-approach-s50-review/) lands where it does in the current GPS cluster.

## Why Samsung Still Has a Real Case

Because some golfers do not actually want a golf watch.

They want a **premium Android smartwatch** that can also cover golf.

That is Samsung's whole lane here.

The official Galaxy Watch Ultra page is selling:

- titanium rugged hardware
- **dual-frequency GPS**
- strong battery headlines
- broader outdoor and health positioning

That makes the Watch Ultra easier to justify if your life already runs through Samsung and Android.

It just does not make it the smarter golf-specific purchase.

## The Golf-App Layer Is the Whole Samsung Problem

This is the deciding issue.

Samsung's Watch Ultra is not sold as a dedicated golf watch with a native golf feature stack that defines the product.

So the golf part still lives through **third-party apps**.

That means extra setup.

It means less golf-specific product identity.

It means the watch is better understood as:

"great smartwatch, decent golf route"

instead of:

"great golf watch"

If that sounds harsh, it is only because the [Garmin Approach S50 review](/blog/garmin-approach-s50-review/) and even the cheaper [Bushnell iON Elite review](/blog/bushnell-ion-elite-gps-watch-review/) make the golf-first case much more directly.

## Battery and Everyday Use

Samsung absolutely fights back here.

Samsung's official battery story is much more ambitious than the average general-purpose smartwatch, and the Watch Ultra has a far stronger everyday-wear case than a lot of golf devices.

That matters if your watch also needs to cover:

- workday notifications
- health tracking
- workouts
- payment
- general phone-life utility

The **S50** still works beyond golf. That is part of why it exists. But it is still trying to be a golf smartwatch first and a general wearable second.

The **Galaxy Watch Ultra** flips that order.

## Why the Price Math Still Helps Garmin

This is what hurts Samsung on a pure golf page.

The **Galaxy Watch Ultra** starts at **$499.99 before trade-in**.

The **Garmin Approach S50** sits at **$399.99**.

So Samsung is asking you to spend more money on the watch that is less directly built around golf.

That can still be fine if you value the broader Android-smartwatch life.

It is just a much narrower buy case for golfers.
