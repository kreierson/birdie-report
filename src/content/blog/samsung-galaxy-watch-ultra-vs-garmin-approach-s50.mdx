This is one of those golf-tech searches where the answer gets easier the second you stop pretending the products are trying to do the same job.

The **Samsung Galaxy Watch Ultra** is trying to be your premium Android smartwatch that can also handle golf.

The **Garmin Approach S50** is trying to be the smarter golf smartwatch for people who want more than a basic yardage watch without paying full **S70** money.

For most golfers making this exact search before buying, I would recommend the **Garmin Approach S50**.

This is a **research-based comparison** built from Samsung's current **Galaxy Watch Ultra (2025)** product and compare pages plus Garmin's current **Approach S50** product page and January 21, 2025 launch release, all checked on **Friday, July 24, 2026**. The golf-side Samsung framing also leans on Birdie Report's existing [Samsung Galaxy Watch Ultra golf review](/blog/samsung-galaxy-watch-ultra-golf-review/), because Samsung still is not selling this watch as a golf-first product. No fake "I became spiritually bilingual in Wear OS and Garmin Connect" routine.

Buy the **Garmin Approach S50** if golf is the main reason you are shopping.

Buy the **Samsung Galaxy Watch Ultra** if you already want Samsung's flagship Android smartwatch anyway and just need golf to be good enough through apps.

That is the useful split:

- **S50** for the smarter golf buy
- **Galaxy Watch Ultra** for the broader Android-smartwatch lifestyle

## Why the Garmin S50 Gets the Recommendation

Garmin makes this easy because the current **Approach S50** product story is clean.

The official materials give you:

- **43,000 preloaded courses**
- a **1.2-inch AMOLED display**
- **PlaysLike distance**
- **heart rate**
- **sleep**, **stress**, and **Body Battery**
- **Morning Report**
- **Garmin Pay**
- music support through compatible services
- up to **15 hours** of battery in GPS mode

That is a very coherent golf-smartwatch package at **$399.99**.

It is also why the [Garmin Approach S50 review](/blog/garmin-approach-s50-review/) keeps landing in the same place: this is the Garmin middle that finally makes sense.

You are not paying for a stripped-down golf watch.
You are also not paying full flagship money just to say your wrist lives on the premium rack.

## Why Samsung Still Has a Real Case

Because some golfers do not actually want a golf watch.

They want a **premium Android smartwatch** that also covers golf, workouts, health tracking, notifications, and the rest of normal life.

Samsung's current **Galaxy Watch Ultra (2025)** pages sell that story with:

- **titanium** construction
- **47 mm** sizing
- **60.5 g** weight
- **590 mAh** battery
- **Wear OS powered by Samsung**
- **2 GB RAM and 64 GB internal storage**
- **Dual GPS (L1 and L5 Band)** plus LTE
- **5ATM / IP68 / MIL-STD-810H**
- **Energy Score**, heart monitoring, sleep coaching, and broader wellness tooling

That is a strong smartwatch case.

It is just not a golf-first case.

## The Golf-App Dependency Is Still the Whole Samsung Problem

This is the deciding issue.

The **Galaxy Watch Ultra** is not a dedicated golf product with native golf identity baked into the hardware and the buying pitch.

Golf still arrives through **third-party apps**.

That means:

- more setup
- more friction
- more chances to realize you paid a lot for something that still treats golf like a side quest

If you already wanted Samsung's flagship watch, that compromise may be totally fine.

If you are shopping because of golf, it is much harder to justify.

That is why Birdie Report's existing [Samsung Galaxy Watch Ultra golf review](/blog/samsung-galaxy-watch-ultra-golf-review/) keeps pushing buyers back toward dedicated golf-first options.

## Price Makes This Easier, Not Harder

The **Galaxy Watch Ultra** currently sits at **$649.99** on Samsung US.

The **Approach S50** currently sits at **$399.99** on Garmin.

So Samsung is asking you to spend **$250 more** for the watch that is less directly built around golf.

That does not make the Samsung a bad product.

It just narrows the buyer:

- you want the Android-smartwatch life first
- you care about the broader Samsung ecosystem
- and you are okay with golf being one part of the job, not the whole job

If that is not you, the **S50** is the more rational receipt.

The **Samsung Galaxy Watch Ultra** is the better **Android lifestyle smartwatch**.
