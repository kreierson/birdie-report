This is one of the better premium golf-shoe decisions on the site because both shoes live in the same broad spikeless lane but sell very different versions of why you should care.

The **Nike Air Zoom Infinity Tour NEXT%** are trying to win with athletic energy, style, and a more active ride.

The **FootJoy Pro/SL 2026** are trying to win with broader practicality, stronger one-pair logic, and less downside.

That is a real buyer fork.

This comparison is **research-based** and built from current product positioning, listed pricing context, and recurring golfer feedback patterns reflected across our existing shoe coverage in **June 2026**. No fake "I changed shoes at the turn and became a footwear scholar" nonsense.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **FootJoy Pro/SL 2026** if you want the smarter all-around premium spikeless buy.

Buy the **Nike Air Zoom Infinity Tour NEXT%** if you specifically want the more athletic-feeling, more style-forward option and you are comfortable trading some practicality for that.

For most golfers, I would pick **Pro/SL 2026**.

For the golfer who hates safe golf shoes and wants something that feels more alive underfoot, the **Nike** absolutely have a case.

## Comfort and Ride: Depends on What You Mean by Comfort

The **Nike** feel more active.

The **FootJoy** feel more settled.

That is the cleanest possible way to explain it.

If your ideal golf shoe feels closer to a modern trainer, the Nike lane makes more sense.

If your ideal golf shoe feels like it quietly does everything well without demanding attention, the FootJoy lane makes more sense.

Neither answer is wrong. They are just solving different emotional parts of the purchase.

## Traction and Conditions Fit: FootJoy Win on Practical Confidence

The **Nike** traction is good enough for plenty of golf.

The **FootJoy** traction is easier to trust for the widest range of normal conditions.

That matters if you are buying one premium pair and want fewer excuses, fewer caveats, and less "well, except when..." attached to the recommendation.

## Fit and Everyday Ownership: FootJoy Stay Easier

This is another category where the **Pro/SL 2026** keep pulling ahead.

The more selective, athletic Nike fit can be a major plus if that shape suits you.

It can also be a problem if it does not.

The **FootJoy** broad-market fit story is simply easier to recommend when you do not know the buyer personally. That is one of the biggest reasons the shoe keeps winning in review and shortlist contexts.

**Edge: FootJoy Pro/SL 2026**

## Style: Nike Win by a Lot

This one does not need a committee meeting.

If you want the cooler-looking shoe, it is the **Nike**.

If you want the safer-looking shoe, it is the **FootJoy**.

That is not trivial. Golfers keep wearing the shoes they enjoy seeing on their feet.

**Edge: Nike Air Zoom Infinity Tour NEXT%**
