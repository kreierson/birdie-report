*Image: The Open / R&A*

If **Tommy Fleetwood** were leading this Open by two on Sunday morning, the storyline would be easier.

It would also be worse.

According to **The Open's official Saturday coverage and Sunday leaderboard**, checked on **Sunday, July 19, 2026**, Fleetwood starts the final round of **The 154th Open at Royal Birkdale** at **5-under**, five shots behind leader **Sam Burns**. The same official coverage says Fleetwood got within **one shot of the lead** on the back nine Saturday before bogeys at **15** and **18** dropped him back.

Perfect.

This column is based on **The Open's official July 18 Saturday recap**, its **Fleetwood crowd-feature story published July 18**, and the **Sunday leaderboard page**, all checked on **July 19, 2026**. No pretending I was in Southport inventing local folklore while hiding in the dunes.

For the straight-news frame first, read [our Sunday setup story on Burns, Ryan Fox, Si Woo Kim, and the rest of the board](/blog/sam-burns-open-final-round-birkdale-2026/), plus the earlier [Friday halfway recap](/blog/lucas-herbert-open-halfway-lead-birkdale-2026/) and [Saturday scoring-defense column](/blog/royal-birkdale-does-not-need-to-apologize-for-62s-opinion-2026/).

## A Hometown Coronation Would Have Been Too Neat

Golf people love getting drunk on the obvious script.

Local star.
Local crowd.
Historic venue.
Everybody starts writing the closing montage before the final group reaches the 6th tee.

That is not tension. That is prepackaged content pretending to be sports.

Fleetwood sitting **five back** is better because it keeps the emotional hook without making the championship revolve around one sentimental fantasy. The Open's official Fleetwood story says the crowd has been "incredible," that it has made a "massive difference," and that this week is already one of the most special experiences of his life. Fine. Great. Keep all of that.

But make him earn the scary part.

Make him go get it.

## Five Back Means the Roars Actually Matter

This is the useful thing about Fleetwood's position.

He is not close enough for the crowd to spend all day protecting a lead.
He is close enough that one burst changes the temperature of the whole golf course.

That is a huge difference.

At **7-under** on Saturday, Fleetwood had the place half delirious. The official Open recap says birdies at **5, 7, and 11** got him within one. The separate crowd story says the atmosphere reached "fever pitch" and quoted Fleetwood saying the support is the kind of thing he will have for life.

That is exactly the energy you want on a Sunday:

- not passive admiration
- not ceremonial clapping
- actual noise with something at stake

If Fleetwood birdies two of his first five holes, the whole championship gets louder and stranger in a hurry.

That is much better than a tidy morning where everyone just waits for him not to mess it up.

## Royal Birkdale Deserves More Than One Script

Another reason the chase version works better is that it leaves room for the rest of the leaderboard to matter.

**Sam Burns** is at **10-under**.
**Ryan Fox** and **Si Woo Kim** are at **8-under**.
**Ryan Gerard** and **Lucas Herbert** are at **7-under**.
**Ludvig Aberg**, **Bryson DeChambeau**, and **Jackson Suber** are at **6-under**.

That is a real Open board, not a stage set built for one local hero shot.

We already spent most of this week appreciating the fact that [Royal Birkdale started firm and weird](/blog/royal-birkdale-open-starts-baked-out-2026/), that [the debutants were allowed to crash the story](/blog/jackson-suber-open-debutants-good-opinion-2026/), and that [two 62s did not break the championship](/blog/royal-birkdale-does-not-need-to-apologize-for-62s-opinion-2026/).

Why ruin that now by pretending the only valid ending is Fleetwood floating gently into destiny?

No thanks.

## Fleetwood Gets to Be the Threat, Not the Burden

This is the best part.

Fleetwood does not have to carry the whole town from the first tee.

He gets to hunt.

The Open's official crowd story has him saying there is no real pressure on him except his own expectations, and that if he can "get the crowd going a little bit" then "you never know." That is the right shape. It is lighter. It is more dangerous. It turns him from symbolic hope into an actual disruptor.

That matters because Fleetwood at his best looks freer when he is chasing than when everybody starts building a national emotional support structure around him.

If he posts a number, then the pressure belongs to Burns.
Then it belongs to Fox.
Then it belongs to Kim.

That is how this should work.

## Golf Needs More Earned Roars and Fewer Prewritten Endings

The broader point is simple.

Sports are better when the noise has to be summoned, not scheduled.

A Fleetwood Sunday charge from **five back** gives Royal Birkdale a chance to produce the best kind of crowd energy: the kind that arrives because something is actually happening, not because everybody agreed in advance to treat the day like a tourism-board campaign.

If he shoots **65** and scares the hell out of the leaders, that rules.
If he gets hot early and then the back nine bites him again, that also feels real.
If Burns survives all of it and wins anyway, then the crowd still mattered because it made the champion carry something extra.

Every version of that is better than fake inevitability.

## Bottom Line

**Tommy Fleetwood** starting **Sunday** at **5-under**, five shots behind **Sam Burns**, is the right kind of hometown-major setup for **Royal Birkdale**.

It keeps the crowd live, the pressure honest, and the championship open to more than one boring script.
