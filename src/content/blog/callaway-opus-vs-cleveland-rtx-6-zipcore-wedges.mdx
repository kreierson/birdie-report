## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Cleveland RTX 6 ZipCore** if you want the smarter all-conditions wedge for the money.

Buy the **Callaway Opus** if you care more about shape, Callaway's spin-tech story, and a cleaner players-wedge vibe at address.

That is the honest split.

At current official pricing, the **Opus Brushed Chrome** starts at **$149.99** and the **RTX 6 ZipCore Tour Satin** sits at **$139.99**. So this is not really a "premium versus bargain" fight. It is a **which wedge philosophy fits your golf better** fight.

For more wedge context, start with [Callaway Opus Wedge Review](/blog/callaway-opus-wedge-review/), [Cleveland RTX 6 ZipCore Wedge Review](/blog/cleveland-rtx-6-zipcore-wedge-review/), and [Best Wedges 2026](/blog/best-wedges-2026/).

## Price Is Close Enough That the Design Matters More

This is good news, because huge price gaps often make comparisons boring.

Callaway's official Opus page lists:

- **Opus Brushed Chrome** from **$149.99**
- **Spin Gen Face Technology**
- aggressive face blast
- groove-in-groove shaping in **54 through 60 degree** lofts

Cleveland's official RTX 6 page lists:

- **RTX 6 ZipCore Tour Satin** at **$139.99**
- **HydraZip**
- **UltiZip grooves**
- **ZipCore**
- loft and bounce options that run through **Low/Low+**, **Mid**, and **Full** families

Because the gap is only about **ten bucks**, you can stop pretending price alone should decide this.

It should not.

## What the Opus Is Really Selling

The **Opus** is selling a cleaner premium-players-wedge identity.

Callaway leans into:

- its **most Tour-validated shapes**
- **Spin Gen Face Technology**
- a more refined address look
- more modern wedge branding than the old Jaws line

That matters for golfers who judge wedges like grown adults pretending they are not absolutely ruled by aesthetics.

If a wedge sits beautifully, frames the ball well, and looks like the club you want to hit touch shots with, that matters. Especially in the scoring clubs.

The Opus is the wedge I would steer toward the golfer who says:

"I want the one that looks right, feels like a real players wedge, and still gives me modern spin tech."

## What the RTX 6 Is Really Selling

The **RTX 6 ZipCore** is selling practicality.

Not boring practicality. Useful practicality.

Cleveland's whole pitch is about spin surviving **real-world lies**, especially wet and sketchy ones. HydraZip is the center of that story, and it is one of the rare equipment-tech pitches that maps to an actual thing golfers deal with:

- morning dew
- soft turf
- damp rough
- not-everything-is-perfect conditions

That is why the RTX 6 keeps appealing to golfers who would rather buy the wedge that solves ugly golf problems than the wedge that wins the prettiest-at-address contest.

That is also why Cleveland still owns such a useful lane in this category.

## Grind Story and Turf Fit

This is where the comparison gets more interesting.

Callaway's Opus platform offers broader grind coverage and a more premium fit story, but the Cleveland page is easier to read in normal-human language:

- **LOW/LOW+**
- **MID**
- **FULL**

That is clean.

It gives golfers a more intuitive way to think about turf and delivery without acting like they need a wedge PhD before checkout.

So while the Opus may look sexier on paper to some players, the RTX 6 often becomes the easier self-fit purchase for golfers who just want to be honest about their delivery and turf conditions.

If you know you are shallow and like opening the face on firmer turf, you can gravitate lower.

If you know you are steeper or play softer conditions, the fuller sole lanes start making more sense immediately.

That simplicity is valuable.

## Which One Wins on Spin?

This depends on what kind of spin story you care about.

The **Opus** wins if your brain likes the idea of:

- groove-in-groove shaping
- aggressive face blast
- a modern premium face package centered on partial-shot control

The **RTX 6** wins if your brain likes the idea of:

- wet-condition retention
- practical spin on ugly lies
- technology that is explicitly built around inconsistent course conditions

For me, the Cleveland story is more differentiated.

Lots of wedges promise spin.

Fewer wedges make a clearer case for **preserving spin when the lie or weather is being a pain in the ass**.

That is why I lean **RTX 6** for the broader population.

## Final Verdict

The **Callaway Opus** is the better choice for the golfer who buys wedges with one eye on performance and one eye on how the club looks sitting behind the ball.

The **Cleveland RTX 6 ZipCore** is the better choice for the golfer who wants the wedge that makes the most sense once the turf gets weird and the conditions stop being perfect.

My call for most golfers: **RTX 6 ZipCore**.

My call for the golfer who already knows they want the sleeker Callaway shape and does not mind giving up a little of Cleveland's wet-condition identity: **Opus**.
