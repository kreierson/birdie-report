The **Ping G440** is the iron a lot of those same golfers probably should buy.

If you want the quick answer before premium-iron vanity kicks in:

- buy **P790** if you want the broader premium players-distance set and your strike quality can actually use it
- buy **Ping G440** if you want the smarter forgiveness-first purchase that helps more golfers sooner

## Quick Verdict

For **most 10-to-18 handicaps**, I would buy the **Ping G440** first.

For **the 8-to-14 handicap who already wants a premium players-distance set and is getting enough consistent strike quality to justify it**, I would buy the **TaylorMade P790**.

That is the real split.

The **Ping G440** is the better broad recommendation.

The **P790** is the better premium-specific recommendation.

## Why This Matchup Is So Common

The **P790** and **G440** both appeal to golfers who want:

- distance that feels modern
- a shape that does not feel stupid at address
- enough help that the set is actually playable
- something that looks like an upgrade

The difference is how much honesty the purchase requires.

The **P790** says:

"I want the premium players-distance answer and I am willing to meet it halfway."

The **G440** says:

"I would rather lower scores than cosplay as a better striker."

That second sentence is not glamorous.

It is often the smarter sentence.

## Forgiveness and Launch: Ping Covers More of Your Garbage

This is the easiest section on the page.

The **Ping G440** is the easier club to hit.

It launches easier.

It covers more heel-and-toe nonsense.

It asks less from your contact.

That is the entire product brief, and it is why the G440 works.

If your current iron life still includes:

- chunky contact
- thin misses
- long irons that feel like a negotiation
- days when the face just is not finding the middle

the **G440** is the better answer.

That is also why it keeps holding up as the practical score-saving route in [Best Irons for High Handicappers 2026](/blog/best-irons-high-handicappers-2026/) and the broader-help branch of [Best Irons for Mid Handicappers 2026](/blog/best-irons-mid-handicappers-2026/).

If the point is to shoot lower numbers, Ping starts this page ahead.

## Feel and Set Identity: P790 Still Wins the Aspirational Side

This is the entire **P790** case.

The **P790** looks better.

It feels more like a premium players-distance set.

It gives golfers the sense that they bought a "real" iron without fully abandoning forgiveness.

That matters because golf equipment is not purely rational. If it were, half the premium categories would not exist.

The **P790** makes sense for golfers who want:

- a more compact shape
- a more premium in-the-bag identity
- stronger long-iron speed appeal
- a set they can grow into as their strike quality improves

If that sounds like you, the fuller product argument lives in the [TaylorMade P790 irons review](/blog/taylormade-p790-irons-review/).

## Distance and Control: Depends What Kind of Distance You Want

The **P790** is the hotter-feeling premium iron.

The **G440** is the more calm, stable iron.

Those are not the same kind of distance experience.

The **P790** gives you more of the:

- premium-speed sensation
- compact-head launch help
- "this set feels expensive for a reason" personality

The **G440** gives you more of the:

- dependable carry window
- easier-launch GI help
- same-shot, same-shot, same-shot predictability

So the real question is not:

"Which one goes farther?"

It is:

"Do I want more premium pop or more normal-round forgiveness?"

That is a much more useful buyer question.

## Value: Ping Makes the P790 Earn Every Dollar

This is where the **P790** needs a specific buyer.

The **G440** costs less and helps more golfers.

That is a very annoying sentence if you love premium-iron shopping.

The **P790** can still justify itself, but it justifies itself through:

- shape
- set identity
- grow-into appeal
- a golfer already striking it well enough to benefit

If that is not you, the **G440** wins the page on pure logic.
