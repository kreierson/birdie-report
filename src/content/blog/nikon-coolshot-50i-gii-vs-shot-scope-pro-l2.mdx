## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Shot Scope PRO L2** if you want the smarter buy for most golfers.

Buy the **Nikon COOLSHOT 50i GII** if you specifically want brighter optics, longer listed range, and the more premium-feeling experience.

For **most golfers comparing these two directly at current prices**, I would recommend the **PRO L2**.

For **golfers who care more about premium glass and better overall feel than about saving a hundred bucks**, I would recommend the **Nikon**.

## Why the PRO L2 Gets the Recommendation for Most Golfers

The **PRO L2** wins the most important consumer category:

**not making you spend more than you need to.**

Shot Scope's current materials give it a pretty loaded practical feature list for **$149.99**:

- **Adaptive slope**
- **target-lock vibration**
- **built-in cart magnet**
- **700-yard range**
- **free GPS hole maps**
- **24-month warranty**

That is a lot for the money.

And more importantly, it is enough for the way most golfers actually use a rangefinder:

- lock onto a flag
- get the number
- maybe use slope
- move on with life

If that is your actual use case, the **PRO L2** makes a ton of sense.

## Why Nikon Still Has the Better Premium Case

The **COOLSHOT 50i GII** earns its price mostly in how it sounds and feels to use.

Nikon's current product page leans on:

- **6 to 1,200 yard** range
- **6x magnification**
- **built-in magnet**
- **ID incline/decline mode**
- **8-second continuous scanning**
- **DUAL LOCKED ON QUAKE**

And yes, the optics story matters.

Nikon has the stronger case if you are the golfer who notices:

- viewfinder brightness
- display clarity
- better premium feel in the hand
- cleaner confidence when ranging targets

That does not automatically make it the smarter purchase.

It does make it the better product.

## Price Is the Real Fight

This is where the decision stops being abstract.

On the current sources checked **June 7, 2026**:

- **Nikon COOLSHOT 50i GII** showed **$249.95**
- **Shot Scope PRO L2** showed **$149.99 MSRP**

That is a **$100 gap**.

And in this category, a hundred bucks matters because the devices are still doing one main job: telling you how far away something is.

So the question becomes:

is Nikon better by enough to justify the extra hundred?

For some golfers, yes.

For most golfers, probably not.

## Feature Story: Closer Than the Price Gap Suggests

This is the part people underestimate.

The **PRO L2** is not just cheap. It still gives you most of the actual golf features buyers care about:

- slope
- magnet
- vibration confirmation
- usable range
- tournament-legal slope off-switch

That means this is not a comparison between:

- premium full-featured rangefinder
- stripped-down budget toy

It is a comparison between:

- **better optics and more premium feel**
- **better value and enough features for normal golfers**

That is why the PRO L2 stays so dangerous in this price tier.

## Where Nikon Can Still Be the Smarter Buy

I do not want to undersell the Nikon.

It makes complete sense if you:

- want a more premium-feeling unit
- care about bright, clear optics
- want the longer listed range
- like Nikon's DUAL LOCKED ON QUAKE confirmation system
- do not mind paying more for the nicer experience

This is especially true if your shopping brain keeps drifting toward the premium rangefinder tier but you still do not want to pay full Bushnell money.

That is exactly where Nikon lives best.
