Do you want the **nicer golf smartwatch**, or do you want the **watch that quietly gives you the number and does not ask for a relationship**?

That is the whole **Garmin Approach S50 vs Garmin Approach S12** decision.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Garmin Approach S12** if you want the smarter golf purchase for most golfers.

Buy the **Garmin Approach S50** if you specifically want:

- **PlaysLike distance**
- an **AMOLED** screen
- health and wellness tracking
- a watch you will actually wear when you are not golfing

For golfers who mostly want yardages, battery life, and less nonsense, I would recommend the **S12** first.

For golfers who want a better overall wearable and are fine paying for it, I would recommend the **S50**.

## Why the S12 Gets the Recommendation for Most Golfers

The **S12** keeps winning because it does not waste your money on features a lot of golfers only pretend they want.

It gives you:

- front, middle, and back distances
- hazards and doglegs
- Big Numbers mode
- button controls
- battery life that basically laughs at charger anxiety

That is enough for a ton of golfers.

It is also the same reason the **S12** keeps making sense against the [Bushnell iON Elite](/blog/bushnell-ion-elite-vs-garmin-approach-s12/), the [Shot Scope V5](/blog/shot-scope-v5-vs-garmin-approach-s12/), and even Garmin's prettier middle watches.

The S12 is not exciting.

That is part of the appeal.

## What the Extra $200 Buys on the S50

Garmin does have a real answer for the price jump.

The **S50** adds:

- **PlaysLike distance**
- a much nicer **AMOLED** screen
- **heart rate**, **sleep**, **stress**, and **Body Battery**
- **activity profiles**
- **Morning Report**
- **Garmin Pay**
- music controls with supported services

That is why the **S50** exists.

It is not trying to beat the S12 at simplicity.

It is trying to give golfers a watch that still matters after they leave the course.

If that matters to you, the S50 is not overpriced. It is just aimed at a different buyer.

## Golf Features: The S50 Is Better, but Not in a Way Everyone Needs

The **S50** absolutely gives you the better watch experience on the course.

The better display, the cleaner graphics, and **PlaysLike** all make it feel more premium and more useful.

But it is important to say this cleanly:

the **S50** does not transform the basic golf-watch job enough to make the **S12** look stupid.

The S12 still gives you the number.

And the number is still the main reason most golfers wear one of these in the first place.

That is why this comparison stays honest:

- **S50** if you want golf plus lifestyle utility
- **S12** if you want golf utility and nothing extra

## The Real Tradeoff Is Battery and Friction Versus Polish and Utility

The easiest question to ask yourself:

**What annoys you more?**

- a basic-looking watch that just works
- or a nicer watch that asks for more money and more charging

If the first one annoys you more, buy the **S50**.

If the second one annoys you more, buy the **S12**.

That is the cleaner buyer frame than pretending this is just "more features good, fewer features bad."

The **Garmin Approach S50** is the better watch.
