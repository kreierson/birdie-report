## Quick Verdict

If you want the straightforward premium recommendation **today**, buy the **Full Swing KIT**.

If you find a **SkyTrak+** through remaining inventory, certified/pre-owned channels, or a real marketplace discount and your whole priority is indoor-simulator value, the **SkyTrak+** can still be the smarter buy.

That availability caveat is not some tiny disclaimer.

It is the entire comparison.

As of **Sunday, July 19, 2026**, SkyTrak's official **ST+** product page shows a **$1,995** sale price and also says the launch monitor **"is no longer available."**

So this is not a simple new-retail head-to-head. It is a buying-reality head-to-head.

## Why the SkyTrak+ Still Has a Real Case

The **SkyTrak+** is not interesting because it is perfect.

It is interesting because SkyTrak's current ST+ page still makes a pretty credible indoor-first case:

- **dual doppler radar** plus an **advanced photometric camera system**
- indoor and outdoor use
- **4-hour** rechargeable battery
- **2 x 2** hitting zone
- club data including **club head speed, smash factor, club path, face angle, and face-to-path**
- ball data including **carry distance, total distance, ball speed, back spin, side spin, descent angle, side angle, launch angle, and shot shape**

That is not fake-premium fluff. That is a real feature set.

And at the current official **$1,995** sale price, it creates a very different buying conversation from Full Swing's **$4,999** tag.

That is why the ST+ still matters in Birdie Report's launch-monitor cluster even with its current weird product status.

## Why the Full Swing KIT Still Feels Cleaner

The **Full Swing KIT** wins the recommendation for one reason that has nothing to do with ego and everything to do with shopping friction:

it is still a current, supported premium product with a coherent product story.

Full Swing's current page says the KIT includes:

- **16 club and ball data points**
- **HD swing replay video**
- a **free cloud-based app** on **iOS and Android**
- compatibility with **GSPro for PC** and **E6 Connect for iPhone and iPad**, with packages available separately
- a **30-day money-back guarantee**

That is cleaner.

You can disagree with the price. You should, honestly.

But the product story itself is cleaner than SkyTrak's current "sale price, also unavailable" situation.

That is what premium buyers are really paying for here:

- current support
- simpler buy-now confidence
- a premium radar identity
- less weirdness around sourcing the product

## The Real Buyer Split

This comparison is not about which spec list is prettier.

It is about what type of buyer you are.

### Buyer 1: The indoor-sim value hunter

This buyer wants:

- a serious indoor-first launch monitor
- tighter-space friendliness than some behind-the-ball radar setups
- a lower buy-in than Bushnell or Full Swing
- the best performance they can get without going fully insane on price

If that is you, the **SkyTrak+** can absolutely still make sense, **if you can actually find one**.

### Buyer 2: The premium current-product buyer

This buyer wants:

- a premium radar unit
- a clean buy-now path
- a better ownership mood out of the box
- less concern about product-status weirdness

If that is you, the **Full Swing KIT** is the better fit even though the price is painful.

That is the honest answer, even if it is less dramatic than pretending this is a normal shelf-vs-shelf fight.

## Software and Membership Friction

Neither of these is a perfectly pure one-time purchase.

SkyTrak's current ST+ page explicitly says basic features are included, but many advanced tools and course-play/game-improvement features require a paid **SkyTrak Membership**.

Full Swing's cleaner no-unlock story is better for the core product, but simulator paths still involve separate software costs depending on how deep you go.

So no, neither company is offering some magical golf-tech paradise where you pay once and never think again.

The difference is that Full Swing's friction is easier to understand, while SkyTrak's friction is wrapped inside a product that is also no longer cleanly available.
