The **Women's Scottish Open** is not showing up this week with one nice headliner and a bunch of polite filler.

It is showing up loaded.

According to the **LPGA's official July 20, 2026 field announcement**, **field breakdown**, **watch guide**, and the tour's current **tournaments page**, all checked on **Tuesday, July 21, 2026**, the **ISPS HANDA Women's Scottish Open** returns to **Dundonald Links** in **Gailes, North Ayrshire, Scotland** from **Thursday, July 23 through Sunday, July 26** with **seven of the Rolex Women's World Golf Rankings top 10**, **nine 2026 LPGA winners in the field**, and defending champion **Lottie Woad** back for a title defense that does not feel ceremonial at all.

That is a serious board for a week people sometimes lazily frame as little more than a bridge into the **AIG Women's Open**.

This piece is based on the **LPGA's official July 20 field stories and watch guide**, plus the tour's current **tournaments page**, all checked on **Tuesday, July 21**. No pretending I had a private walk-and-talk with half the field on the Dundonald range.

If you want the bigger women's-golf runway first, read [our opinion on why the AIG Women's Open is finally acting like a major](/blog/aig-womens-open-is-acting-like-a-major-2026/), [the hard-news breakdown of that $10 million AIG purse jump](/blog/aig-womens-open-prize-fund-broadcast-2026/), and [our earlier take that Lottie Woad already graduated from soft-focus prospect framing](/blog/lottie-woad-second-lpga-win-opinion-2026/).

## Seven of the Top 10 Is Not a Warm-Up Field

The cleanest signal is the rankings count.

The LPGA's official **field breakdown** says **seven of the current top 10** in the **Rolex Women's World Golf Rankings** are in this week's field:

- **Nelly Korda** at **No. 1**
- **Hyo Joo Kim** at **No. 4**
- **Lottie Woad** at **No. 5**
- **Miyu Yamashita** at **No. 6**
- **Charley Hull** at **No. 7**
- **Hannah Green** at **No. 9**
- **Sei Young Kim** at **No. 10**

That is not some thin little tune-up roster.

That is the kind of field depth that should make the week matter on its own, even before you remember the next major lands immediately after it.

## The 2026 Winner List Gives This Thing Actual Bite

The rankings are one thing.

The active-winner list is the more fun part.

The LPGA's **field breakdown** says the field includes **nine 2026 LPGA winners**:

- **Nelly Korda**
- **Hannah Green**
- **Mi Hyang Lee**
- **Hyo Joo Kim**
- **Lauren Coughlin**
- **Lottie Woad**
- **Yana Wilson**
- **Gina Kim**
- **Miyu Yamashita**

It also lists **13 2026 LET winners** in the field, including **Charley Hull**, **Hannah Green**, **Lauren Coughlin**, **Helen Briem**, and **Carolina Chacarra**.

So this is not just a field with name value.
It is a field with players who have already been collecting trophies this year.

That matters because a week gets a lot less sleepy when the board is full of people who have already proven they can close.

## Lottie Woad Is Defending, and This Does Not Feel Like Nostalgia Theater

The easiest lazy version of this story would be: defending champion comes back, everybody smiles, next question.

That is not the shape here.

Woad is back as the **2025 Women's Scottish Open** champion, but now she arrives as a player the LPGA already has ranked **No. 5 in the world**, not just a sentimental returning winner. That is a lot different from defending a title as a nice memory.

We already argued in [our May opinion piece on Woad's second LPGA win](/blog/lottie-woad-second-lpga-win-opinion-2026/) that golf should stop talking about her like she is merely "coming soon." This field setup kind of hammers the same point home. When a defending champion is also one of the best players on the planet right now, the defense stops being a sidebar and starts being one of the main reasons to watch.

## Scotland Gets the Tour's Only Stop There, and Dundonald Has Earned Better Framing Than "Before the Major"

This is another useful detail from the LPGA's official **field announcement**:

The tour says this is the **only LPGA event staged in Scotland** in **2026**.

That alone should give the week more dignity than the usual "pre-major prep" label. The same LPGA release says last year's event welcomed **more than 11,000 spectators across the week**, and this year marks the **fifth consecutive year** the championship has been played at **Dundonald Links**. The release also notes it is the **third year** with **ISPS HANDA** as title sponsor.

In other words, this is not some random placeholder week.

It has a venue identity now.
It has continuity.
It has a credible field.
It has a defending champ who matters.

That is a real event.

## There Is Some Useful Local Flavor Too

The LPGA's **field breakdown** also flags a couple of local and sponsor-exemption notes that make the week feel a bit less generic:

- **Hannah Darling** and **Gemma Dryburgh** are listed as the field's **Scotland natives**
- the **sponsor exemptions** are **Ashleigh Buhai**, **Kiara Romero (a)**, **Gemma Dryburgh**, and **Hannah Darling**

That is a nice mix of actual top-end talent and the kind of local grounding a national stop should want.

## Yes, the AIG Women's Open Is Next. No, That Does Not Make This Disposable

The LPGA's current **tournaments page** lists this week's Scottish Open at **$2 million** with **500 Race to CME Globe points**, followed immediately by next week's **AIG Women's Open** at **Royal Lytham & St Annes** with a **$10 million purse** and **650 points**.

So yes, the calendar context is obvious.

But the right takeaway is not that this week is secondary.

The right takeaway is that the LPGA just backed itself into a very good two-week run.

That is a different thing.

The **2026 Women's Scottish Open** field is officially set, and it is better than any lazy pre-major framing suggests.

The **LPGA's July 20 field updates**, checked on **Tuesday, July 21, 2026**, show a Dundonald field with **seven of the top 10 in the Rolex rankings**, **nine 2026 LPGA winners**, and defending champion **Lottie Woad** back in the mix.
