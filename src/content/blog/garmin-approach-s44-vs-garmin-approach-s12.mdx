This is a very clean golf-buying question.

Not Garmin versus Apple.

Not Garmin versus Bushnell.

Just this: do you want the **nicer Garmin golf watch**, or do you want the **simpler Garmin golf watch** that quietly does the job and shuts up about it?

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Garmin Approach S12** if you want the smarter no-frills purchase. It gives you the core golf stuff, absurd battery life, and less chance of getting annoyed.

Buy the **Garmin Approach S44** if you want a visibly nicer watch and actually care about the better screen, Green View, and richer Garmin presentation.

For most golfers who mainly want yardages, I would recommend the **S12**.

For golfers who want a more premium Garmin feel without paying [S70 money](/blog/garmin-approach-s70-vs-garmin-approach-s44/), I would recommend the **S44**.

This comparison is research-based and built from Garmin's current S44 product positioning, Garmin's official S12 feature set, and how both watches fit the broader golf-watch market. No fake "I wore both for three months and achieved inner peace" routine.

If you want the surrounding cluster first, start with [Best Golf GPS Watches 2026](/blog/best-golf-gps-watches-2026/), the full [Garmin Approach S44 review](/blog/garmin-approach-s44-review/), and the value-focused [Bushnell iON Elite vs Garmin Approach S12](/blog/bushnell-ion-elite-vs-garmin-approach-s12/).

## The Price Gap Is the Entire Story

| | Garmin Approach S44 | Garmin Approach S12 |
|---|---|---|
| **Typical price lane** | $299.99 | about $199.99 |
| **Display** | 1.2-inch AMOLED | sunlight-readable monochrome display |
| **Battery life in GPS mode** | up to 15 hours | up to 30 hours |
| **Course coverage** | 43,000+ courses | 42,000+ courses |
| **Visual features** | hazard view, Green View, richer presentation | basic yardages, hazards, doglegs |
| **Best for** | golfers who want nicer golf tech | golfers who want simple golf utility |

The S44 is not trying to beat the S12 on simplicity.

The S12 is not trying to beat the S44 on looks or on-wrist polish.

That sounds obvious, but it matters because these two watches are aimed at different forms of "good enough."

## Screen and Visual Experience: S44 Wins Easily

This is the S44's whole case.

Garmin launched it with a **1.2-inch AMOLED display**, **43,000-plus preloaded courses**, and golf-first features like **hazard view**, **Green View**, and front-middle-back distances. That is the pitch: keep the Garmin golf ecosystem, make it look better, and stop short of flagship pricing.

The **S12** is much more basic:

- front, middle, and back distances
- hazard and dogleg yardages
- a simple high-resolution display
- Big Numbers mode
- button controls

That is not an insult. That is the reason the watch exists.

If you want a watch that feels more current and more visual, the **S44** wins comfortably.

If you just want a number and do not care whether the screen looks premium, the S12's simplicity becomes a strength, not a weakness.

## Battery Life: S12 Wins by a Mile

This part is not close.

Garmin gives the **S44** up to **15 hours** in GPS mode.

Garmin gave the **S12** up to **30 hours** in GPS mode.

That is the difference between:

- charging like a normal modern golf watch
- basically forgetting the charger exists

For frequent players, walkers, golf-trip people, or anyone who hates babysitting electronics, the **S12** has a real ownership advantage here.

The S44 battery is fine.

The S12 battery is one of the best reasons to buy it.

## Simplicity vs Niceness: Know What Actually Irritates You

The S12 is the watch for golfers who hate friction.

No touchscreen.

No nicer visuals asking to be appreciated.

No temptation to turn a quick yardage check into a tiny software project on your wrist.

The S44 is better if you enjoy a more polished watch experience and want Garmin to feel a little less stripped down. The nicer display genuinely matters if you glance at the watch a lot and want the information to feel cleaner and more modern.

So the right question is not "which watch has more stuff?"

It is "what kind of tech annoyance am I least willing to tolerate?"

If your answer is ugly screens and basic presentation, buy the **S44**.

If your answer is charging, menu fuss, and unnecessary watch drama, buy the **S12**.

## Golf Features: S44 Gives You More Context, S12 Gives You Enough

The **S44** gives you the more complete visual golf-watch experience. Garmin positions it as the essential-feature AMOLED watch with:

- hazard view
- Green View
- 43,000-plus courses
- smart notifications
- compatibility with Garmin's club-tracking ecosystem

Garmin also gates some premium features behind **Garmin Golf membership**, which matters. Features like **PlaysLike Distance** and some enhanced mapping perks are not just magically included because you bought the S44.

The **S12** is much more honest about what it is. It gives you yardages, hazards, doglegs, and readable numbers. That is enough for a lot of golfers, especially if you already use a [rangefinder](/blog/best-rangefinders-2026/) for exact pin distance.

If you are mostly using the watch as a quick-glance support tool, the S12's lighter feature set is not really a sacrifice.

## Which Watch Is the Better Value?

For most golfers, the **S12**.

That is the blunt answer.

The S44 is the **nicer product**.

The S12 is the **cleaner value**.

The S44 makes sense if you know the nicer display and better visual experience will make you happier every round. That is a real reason to spend money.

But if you are comparison-shopping rationally, the S12 gets you most of the golf utility for less money, with much better battery life, and with less nonsense.

That is hard to beat.

## Who Should Buy the Garmin Approach S44

Buy the **S44** if:

- you want the better-looking Garmin golf watch
- you care about AMOLED screen quality and richer on-wrist visuals
- you like Green View and hazard visuals more than ultra-simple yardage delivery
- you want Garmin without going all the way to the premium [S70 lane](/blog/garmin-approach-s70-vs-garmin-approach-s44/)

## Who Should Buy the Garmin Approach S12

Buy the **S12** if:

- you want the simpler Garmin purchase
- battery life matters a lot
- you prefer buttons and clean yardages over prettier watch graphics
- you are pairing it with a [rangefinder](/blog/best-rangefinders-2026/) and do not need your watch to do too much

## Final Verdict

The **Garmin Approach S44** is the nicer golf watch.

The **Garmin Approach S12** is the smarter buy for most golfers.

That is the whole answer.

If you want premium-ish Garmin polish without spending ridiculous money, the S44 is a good fit.

If you want a Garmin golf watch that is simple, dependable, long-lasting, and easier to justify, buy the **S12** and keep the extra hundred bucks for [balls](/blog/best-golf-balls-2026/), greens fees, or a better [rangefinder](/blog/best-rangefinders-2026/).
