This is one of the better golf-tech comparisons because the money is close, but the products are trying to solve very different problems.

The **Shot Scope V5** says: buy the watch that gives you GPS yardages, **16 included tags**, and actual post-round learning without recurring fees.

The **Garmin Approach S12** says: or buy the watch that simply gives you the damn number, lasts forever, and never asks you to think harder than necessary.

That is a real buying fork.

This is a **research-based comparison** built from the current value and feature lanes already documented across Birdie Report's GPS coverage, including the new [Shot Scope V5 review](/blog/shot-scope-v5-review/), the main [best golf GPS watches 2026 guide](/blog/best-golf-gps-watches-2026/), [Garmin Approach S44 vs Garmin Approach S12](/blog/garmin-approach-s44-vs-garmin-approach-s12/), and the existing Shot Scope-versus-Garmin/Bushnell pages. No pretending I wore both for six months and entered some enlightened relationship with yardages.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Shot Scope V5** if you want the smarter long-term golf purchase.

Buy the **Garmin Approach S12** if you want the simplest no-drama GPS watch you can strap on your wrist.

For most golfers who will actually use the tags and the post-round data, I would recommend the **Shot Scope V5**.

For golfers who know they just want yardages, huge battery life, and zero extra friction, I would recommend the **Garmin S12**.

## Why the V5 Has the Better Value Story

This is the whole Shot Scope argument:

- GPS watch
- **16 included tags**
- **100-plus stats**
- no subscription

That is a nasty little value stack at roughly the same money as the S12.

If you are already curious about where your strokes actually disappear, the V5 gives you a real path into that without pushing you toward a recurring bill or a more complicated sensor ecosystem. That is why it holds up so well next to the [Bushnell iON Elite](/blog/bushnell-ion-elite-vs-shot-scope-v5/), the [Garmin Approach S44](/blog/garmin-approach-s44-vs-shot-scope-v5/), and subscription-heavy tracking plays like [Arccos](/blog/arccos-vs-shot-scope-shot-tracking/).

The **S12** does not really try to answer that value stack.

It answers a different question:

**what if I just want a watch that gives me yardages and leaves me alone?**

## Why the S12 Still Has a Strong Case

The **Garmin Approach S12** is one of the better examples of a product understanding its own job.

It gives you:

- front, middle, and back distances
- hazard and dogleg yardages
- button controls
- **Big Numbers mode**
- **up to 30 hours** of GPS battery life

That is enough for a lot of golfers.

Not everybody wants a stats dashboard waiting after the round. Not everybody wants to think about tags. Not everybody wants the watch to do anything beyond helping choose between a smooth 7 and a smoked 8.

For that buyer, the **S12** is still excellent.

It is also why the S12 keeps making sense in the broader Garmin lane alongside the [Garmin Approach S44 review](/blog/garmin-approach-s44-review/) and the direct [Garmin Approach S44 vs Garmin Approach S12 comparison](/blog/garmin-approach-s44-vs-garmin-approach-s12/).

## Tracking Value vs Battery and Simplicity

This is the whole damn decision.

Choose the **Shot Scope V5** if you care most about:

- learning where you actually lose shots
- getting tracking tags in the box
- avoiding subscriptions
- buying the watch with more improvement upside

Choose the **Garmin S12** if you care most about:

- long battery life
- dead-simple controls
- a watch that does not ask for any post-round commitment
- yardages first, everything else second

The **V5** is better if you want your watch to help both your round **and** your practice.

The **S12** is better if you want your watch to stay in its lane.

## Which One Is Better for Most Golfers?

This depends on whether "most golfers" means **most golfers in theory** or **most golfers in real life**.

In theory, the **Shot Scope V5** should help more because it gives you more information and a clearer improvement path.

In real life, some golfers are never going to touch the data. They will look at one dashboard, feel briefly exposed, and never open it again.

So the honest answer is:

- if you will use the tracking, the **V5** is the better buy
- if you will not, the **S12** is the smarter buy

That is also why the V5 review itself matters here. The watch is genuinely good, but its recommendation strength depends on you wanting the actual **tracking** part, not just the word on the box.

## Final Verdict

The **Shot Scope V5** is the better golf-tech value.

The **Garmin Approach S12** is the better anti-annoyance tool.

For the golfer who wants the watch to help them improve, not just navigate, I would buy the **V5** first. The included tags, **100-plus stats**, and no-subscription model are too good to ignore at this price.

For the golfer who wants a watch that quietly spits out yardages and survives golf-trip battery abuse, the **S12** remains a very smart buy.
