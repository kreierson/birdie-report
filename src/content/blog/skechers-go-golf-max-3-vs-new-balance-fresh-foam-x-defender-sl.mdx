## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Skechers GO GOLF Max 3** if you want the cheaper walking-value play and do not need much more than comfortable-enough golf shoes.

Buy the **New Balance Fresh Foam X Defender SL** if you are willing to spend a little more for:

- softer cushioning
- a better long-round comfort story
- and a more forgiving fit lane

For most golfers who walk regularly, I would lean **New Balance**.

For golfers who play more casually, ride plenty, or are simply trying not to overspend on shoes, the **Skechers** are still a very fair answer.

## Why the Skechers Make Sense

The **GO GOLF Max 3** win this comparison the moment price becomes the first filter.

That happens a lot.

The Skechers case is strong if you want:

- the lower receipt
- a shoe that feels fine immediately
- a casual spikeless option for normal golf
- and no big premium-shoe expectations

That is a perfectly rational buying profile.

The **Skechers GO GOLF Max 3 review** exists because plenty of golfers are not looking for the most advanced shoe on the course. They are looking for the cheapest shoe that still feels like a real golf shoe.

Skechers get closer to that target than most bargain-bin junk.

## Why the New Balance Are the Better Step-Up

The **Fresh Foam X Defender SL** ask for a little more money, but the reason is easy to understand.

They give you a cleaner case for:

- softer cushioning
- better long-round comfort
- roomier fit logic
- and a more convincing walking-first identity

That is why the full [New Balance Fresh Foam X Defender SL review](/blog/new-balance-fresh-foam-x-defender-sl-review/) matters in this decision.

The **New Balance** are the better answer for golfers whose feet, knees, or lower back start complaining once the walk gets longer.

They are also the better answer if shoe fit is a recurring problem and you already know average-width budget shoes are a risky game.

## Comfort: New Balance Wins the More Serious Walking Argument

This is where the price gap starts to justify itself.

The **Skechers** do enough comfort-wise to make the budget case work.

The **New Balance** do more.

That difference matters most if you:

- walk 18 often
- play back-to-back days on trips
- want more shock absorption late in the round
- or simply know your body prefers softer shoes

If your brief is "I just need something comfortable and cheap," Skechers stay alive.

If your brief is "I need this shoe to keep feeling good at hole 15," New Balance pull ahead.

## Fit and Width: Another Reason the New Balance Win More Buyers

Not every golfer can treat shoe fit like an afterthought.

If you usually need:

- more forefoot room
- wider sizing
- or a generally more forgiving fit

the **New Balance** have the better real-world argument.

That does not make the **Skechers** a bad fit. It just makes them a more basic fit story.

For some golfers, that is enough.

For plenty of others, it is the exact reason to pay the extra money.
