This is one of those golf-tech decisions where the more expensive product is obviously better and still not obviously the right buy.

The **Garmin Approach S50** is the more complete watch.

The **Bushnell iON Elite** is the cheaper golf-specific answer that keeps a shocking amount of useful stuff intact for about **half the money**.

This is a **research-based comparison** built from Garmin's official Approach S50 launch materials and Bushnell Golf's current iON Elite product page checked on **May 29, 2026**. No fake "I wore both for six weeks and found inner peace through wrist yardages" routine.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Bushnell iON Elite** if you want the smarter golf-value purchase.

Buy the **Garmin Approach S50** if you specifically want:

- **PlaysLike distance**
- a better **AMOLED display**
- **health and wellness** tracking
- **activity profiles**
- a watch you will actually wear **outside golf**

For most golfers who mainly want useful on-course help, I would recommend the **iON Elite**.

For golfers who want one watch to cover golf plus normal life and training, I would recommend the **S50**.

## Why the iON Elite Gets the Recommendation for Most Golfers

The **iON Elite** does not pretend to be your life coach.

It just tries to be a useful golf watch.

Bushnell's current page still gives it a pretty loaded golf stack for the money:

- **slope-compensated distances**
- **HoleView**
- **Shot Planning**
- **GreenView with movable pin placement**
- **Dynamic Green Mapping**
- **12+ hours of battery life**

At **$189.99**, that is a real argument.

And more importantly, it is a real argument for the golfer who does not care whether their golf watch also thinks it should comment on recovery, stress, or last night's sleep.

If your actual watch behavior is:

- check yardage
- glance at the hole
- maybe drag the pin
- hit the shot

then the iON Elite keeps the purchase clean.

## What the Extra $210 Buys on the S50

This is where Garmin has a legitimate case.

Garmin's official launch stack says the **S50** adds:

- **PlaysLike distance**
- wrist-based **heart rate**
- **sleep**, **stress**, and **Body Battery**
- **activity profiles** for strength, yoga, cardio, and more
- **Morning Report**
- **Garmin Pay**
- music and podcast controls with supported services
- a **ComfortFit nylon strap**

That is not fake differentiation.

It is just not purely golf differentiation.

The S50 is the better buy when you want the watch to matter on days you do not play golf.

That is the whole point of it.

## This Is Really Golf Tool Versus Golf Smartwatch

The easiest way to think about the matchup:

- **iON Elite** if you want a golf watch
- **S50** if you want a golf smartwatch

That sounds like a tiny wording difference, but it is actually the entire decision.

The Bushnell is more specialized. The Garmin is more complete.

The Bushnell gives you better pure-golf value.

The Garmin gives you a much better everyday-wear story.

## Golf Features: Closer Than the Price Gap Suggests

This is why the page is useful.

The **S50** absolutely wins on polish, and **PlaysLike distance** is a meaningful golf feature advantage.

But the **iON Elite** is not some stripped-down joke watch.

It still gives you:

- full hole mapping
- pin-adjustable green views
- slope distances
- shot planning

That means a lot of golfers are not comparing "full golf watch" versus "basic golf watch."

They are comparing:

- **nicer watch with better lifestyle value**
- **cheaper watch with more than enough golf help**

That is a much tighter fight than the logo alone suggests.

## Where the S50 Can Still Be the Smarter Buy

I do not want to undersell the **S50**.

It makes complete sense if you:

- want **PlaysLike** built in
- prefer the **Garmin** app and ecosystem
- care about a better screen
- know you will use health and activity tracking
- do not want a dedicated golf-only watch

That buyer should not talk themselves down into the Bushnell just because the Bushnell is cheaper.

If you already know you want the more complete wearable, buy the better wearable.
