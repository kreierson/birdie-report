Not what looks coolest on the shelf.

Not what sounds more serious in the group chat.

The real split in **adidas Tour360 24 vs ECCO Biom C4** is simple:

- one is the traction-first premium spike
- one is the all-day premium walking shoe

This is a research-based comparison built from the current Birdie Report shoe coverage and manufacturer positioning reflected in that coverage. No fake "I walked 72 in both this weekend and reached enlightenment" nonsense.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **ECCO Biom C4** if you want the better all-around premium shoe. It is the cleaner recommendation for golfers who walk a lot, want serious comfort, and do not specifically need spikes.

Buy the **adidas Tour360 24** if you know traction is the reason you are shopping. Wet turf, hilly lies, and a more planted feel through the swing are where the adidas case gets stronger fast.

For most golfers, I would recommend the **ECCO Biom C4**.

For golfers who still genuinely need spikes, I would recommend the **adidas Tour360 24**.

## Walking Comfort: ECCO Wins

The **Biom C4** has the clearer walking story.

That comes from the whole package:

- stronger day-long comfort reputation
- better breathable-waterproof balance
- a more natural spikeless feel
- the kind of support that matters on hole 15, not just on the first tee

This is exactly why the [ECCO Biom C4 review](/blog/ecco-biom-c4-golf-shoes-review/) keeps landing where it does. Golfers buy the C4 because they want a premium shoe that stays comfortable when the round turns into a real walk instead of a quick loop from cart to green.

The **Tour360 24** is more walkable than a lot of spikes because the **BOOST** cushioning is legit. But it is still the more committed shoe. Heavier. More structured. More obviously built around grip first and lifestyle second.

**Edge: ECCO Biom C4**

## Traction and Stability: adidas Wins Clearly

This is the Tour360's whole reason for existing.

If you are shopping because:

- you play early, wet rounds
- your course has real slopes
- you swing hard enough that you notice your feet when the ground gets sketchy
- you simply trust spikes more

then the **Tour360 24** has the better case.

That is also why the shoe sits so naturally in [Best Spiked Golf Shoes 2026](/blog/best-spiked-golf-shoes-2026/) and why the full [adidas Tour360 24 review](/blog/adidas-tour360-24-review/) leans so hard into the planted-feel story.

The **Biom C4** still has strong traction for a spikeless shoe. It is not some slippery casual sneaker pretending to be golf-ready. But once the turf gets sloppy, true spikes still matter.

**Edge: adidas Tour360 24**

## Weather Fit: Depends on the Kind of Bad Weather

This is where golfers need to stop lumping all conditions together.

The **Tour360 24** is better if bad weather means:

- wet ground
- soft turf
- slippery footing
- the kind of round where you want to feel bolted down

The **Biom C4** is better if bad weather means:

- damp mornings
- mixed conditions
- long walking rounds
- needing waterproofing without cooking your feet

That is the real value of the C4's breathable-waterproof setup. It is not just waterproof. It is easier to live with across more kinds of golf weather.

## Which Shoe Is Easier to Buy Well?

For most golfers, the **Biom C4**.

That does not mean it is better in every category. It means it is easier to recommend without a bunch of conditions attached.

The C4 makes sense if you:

- walk a lot
- want one premium shoe
- do not need true spikes
- care about comfort almost as much as performance

The Tour360 makes sense if you:

- already know spikes are your thing
- play in conditions that justify them
- prefer a more performance-first, grounded feel
- are less interested in versatility

That is a narrower buyer, but it is still a real buyer.

## Value Depends on Whether You Actually Need Spikes

This is the category people screw up.

If you **need spikes**, the **Tour360 24** is the better value because it gives you exactly what you are paying for without asking you to spend more on a spikeless shoe that does not solve the right problem.

If you **do not need spikes**, the **Biom C4** is the better value because it is easier to wear, easier to walk in, and better suited to normal golf life.

That is why this should not be framed as:

- cheaper shoe versus pricier shoe

It should be framed as:

- traction-specific shoe versus broader premium shoe
