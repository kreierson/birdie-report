This is one of the cleaner premium golf-shoe decisions on the site right now because the products are close in price but not close in personality.

The **FootJoy Premiere Series Packard** are trying to win with premium leather, traditional shape, and a more polished heritage feel.

The **adidas Tour360 24** are trying to win with modern support tech, a more athletic performance build, and the stronger "I want this shoe to feel engineered" pitch.

That makes this a natural high-intent buyer question.

This comparison is **research-based** and built from the current official FootJoy Packard page, adidas TOUR360 24 official product and launch materials, current listed pricing, and recurring buyer-feedback patterns checked on **May 22, 2026**. No fake "I swapped them by hole" storytelling.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **adidas Tour360 24** if you want the stronger premium performance spike for most golfers.

Buy the **FootJoy Premiere Series Packard** if you specifically want the premium classic option and will actually value the nicer leather, dressier finish, and stronger waterproof guarantee.

That is the split.

For the majority of buyers, I would lean **Tour360 24** because the performance story is cleaner and the price is **$50** lower.

For the buyer who knows he wants one very good traditional pair and is not pretending otherwise, the **Packard** absolutely make sense.

This is not really an argument about whether either shoe is good. Both are good.

It is an argument about **what kind of premium you actually want to pay for**.

## Traction and Stability: Slight Edge to adidas

The **Packard** still have a serious traction story:

- **ARCTrax outsole technology**
- **renewable Pulsar LP cleats**
- **most stable** positioning on FootJoy's current spec sheet

That is not lightweight lifestyle fluff.

The issue is that adidas push even harder into the modern-performance lane with:

- **Internal 360Wrap**
- **TORSION BRIDGE**
- **JET BOOST in the heel**
- **LIGHTSTRIKE in the forefoot**
- **replaceable THINTECH 7-spike outsole**

That is a lot of support language, but in this case it lines up with the product category. The Tour360 are for golfers who want to feel locked in through the swing, especially in wet or uneven conditions.

If you are buying spikes mainly for traction confidence and stability, the **Tour360 24** have the better argument.

## Comfort and Ride: Depends on the Kind of Comfort You Mean

This category is not "which one is softest?" because if softness is the whole point, you probably should not be in this premium spike lane anyway.

The **Packard** comfort pitch is:

- firmer performance cushioning
- OrthoLite comfort pieces
- traditional structure that feels more refined than brutal

The **Tour360 24** comfort pitch is:

- more athletic underfoot tech
- a support-first platform
- better blend of cushioning and stability for golfers who like a more engineered ride

So the real split is:

- **Packard** for golfers who want premium classic comfort
- **Tour360** for golfers who want premium performance comfort

That second group is larger, which is part of why the adidas end up as the broader recommendation.

## Waterproofing: FootJoy Have the Clearer Reassurance

This is one of the better Packard selling points.

FootJoy explicitly list a **2-year waterproof warranty** for the Packard. That is clean, direct, and useful.

adidas clearly position the **Tour360 24** with a **waterproof leather upper**, which absolutely matters for the type of golfer who still buys spikes. But the public-facing story is more feature-led than guarantee-led.

So the read here is:

- **FootJoy** win on explicit warranty reassurance
- **adidas** still make plenty of wet-condition sense from a product-design standpoint

If bad-weather trust is a major part of why you are paying premium-shoe money, that helps the Packard hold their ground.

## Value: adidas Win for Most People

This is the category that should probably decide the purchase unless you are already a committed Packard guy.

The **Tour360 24** cost **$200**.

The **Packard** cost **$250**.

That extra **$50** does not buy you a clearly better performance shoe. It buys you:

- richer materials
- more classic polish
- stronger waterproof warranty language
- a more upscale old-school ownership feel

Those things matter. They just do not matter to most golfers more than the adidas performance story plus the savings.

That is why the **Tour360 24** are the smarter buy for the larger audience.

## Style and Ownership Feel: Packard Win Easily

This is where the Packard take the board back.

If you care about:

- premium leather aesthetics
- heritage-style golf shoes
- a cleaner traditional silhouette
- the satisfaction of owning the nicer classic pair

then the Packard are plainly more appealing.

The **Tour360 24** look like a modern performance golf shoe because that is exactly what they are. If you want technical design, good. If you want classic style, wrong lane.

This is not trivial. Golf shoes are performance gear, but they are still something you choose to look at every round.

The **adidas Tour360 24** are the better premium spike for most golfers because the performance story is stronger, the price is friendlier, and the reasons to buy them are easier to defend.

The **FootJoy Premiere Series Packard** are the better premium spike for the golfer who wants classic leather refinement on purpose and is willing to pay for it.

That is not a hedge. That is the actual answer.

If I had to recommend one shoe blind to the highest number of buyers, I would point to the **Tour360 24**.
