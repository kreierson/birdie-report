If you are choosing between the **Garmin Approach S70** and the **Garmin Approach S50**, here is the short answer:

Buy the **S50** if you want the smarter Garmin buy for most golfers.

Buy the **S70** if you specifically want the flagship golf-watch experience and know you will actually use the extra golf intelligence often enough to justify the jump.

That is the real split.

This is a **research-based comparison** built from Garmin's current official **Approach S50** and **Approach S70** product pages plus Garmin's official launch materials checked on **July 23, 2026**. Garmin's **January 21, 2025** S50 launch release lists the **S50** at **$399.99**, while Garmin's **May 23, 2023** S70 launch release lists the **S70** starting at **$649.99**. No fake "I wore both for six weeks and the yardages revealed themselves" routine.

## Quick Verdict

The **S50** is the better recommendation for most golfers making this comparison.

The **S70** is the better watch.

Those are not the same thing.

The **S50** wins for most buyers because it gives you:

- the bright **AMOLED** screen
- **43,000-plus** courses
- **PlaysLike distance**
- heart-rate and wellness tracking
- a real everyday-watch case

without immediately dragging you into full flagship money.

The **S70** only becomes the smarter buy when you know the extra golf upside matters more than the extra **$250-plus**.

## Why the S50 Wins for Most Golfers

The **S50** is the sweet spot because it adds the first extras that actually change the ownership story in a useful way.

Garmin's official **January 21, 2025** launch release gives the **S50**:

- **PlaysLike distance**
- wrist-based **heart rate**
- **Body Battery**
- **Morning Report**
- activity profiles
- **Garmin Pay**
- music support

That is enough to make it a real all-day watch instead of just a golf watch that occasionally pretends to care about the rest of your life.

More importantly, it does that at **$399.99**, which still feels like expensive golf tech, but not "I could have bought a driver" golf tech.

That is why the S50 works.

It gives you a real upgrade over the cheaper [Garmin Approach S44 review](/blog/garmin-approach-s44-review/) path without forcing you all the way into premium-flagship logic.

## Where the S70 Actually Earns the Extra Money

The **S70** is still Garmin's best golf watch for a reason.

Garmin's official **May 23, 2023** launch release says the **S70** adds:

- **Virtual Caddie**
- enhanced **PlaysLike Distance** that accounts for wind, temperature, and air pressure
- optional **Green Contour Data** with Garmin Golf membership
- up to **20 hours** of golf GPS battery life
- more premium materials and a stronger general flagship feel

That is not fake differentiation.

It is just expensive differentiation.

If you are the golfer who plays a lot, likes data, and actually wants the watch helping with club choice instead of just showing yardage, the **S70** still makes the strongest case in the category. ## This Is Really Golf Ceiling Versus Buying Discipline

That is the cleanest way to think about the comparison.

The **S70** has the higher ceiling.

The **S50** demands less self-delusion.

Plenty of golfers read the **S70** feature list and think:

"Yeah, obviously I want Garmin's best one."

Then their actual round behavior is still:

- check front / middle / back
- glance at hazards
- occasionally use PlaysLike
- go hit the shot

If that is you, the **S50** is the more honest buy.

If you are the golfer who really will lean on **Virtual Caddie**, play enough to feed the Garmin ecosystem good data, and wear the watch enough to appreciate the bigger premium step-up, the **S70** has a real case.
