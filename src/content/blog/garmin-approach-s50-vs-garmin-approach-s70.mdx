This is the Garmin golf-watch question that actually separates buyers from spec-sheet tourists.

Not whether the **S70** is better.

It is.

The real question is whether it is **better enough** to justify paying **$200 to $300 more** than the **Approach S50**.

That is the page.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Garmin Approach S50** if you want the smarter purchase for most golfers.

Buy the **Garmin Approach S70** if you specifically want:

- **Virtual Caddie**
- the best overall Garmin golf-watch experience
- stronger premium-watch feel
- the full "one watch for everything" flagship case
- zero lingering doubt that you bought Garmin's top golf model

For most buyers, the **S50** is the sharper decision because it keeps the price under control while still giving you the most important middle-tier upgrades: **AMOLED**, **PlaysLike**, and real off-course usefulness.

For golfers who play a lot, love data, and actually want the flagship experience, the **S70** still has the better ceiling.

## Why the S50 Gets the Recommendation for Most Golfers

The **S50** fixes the biggest practical problem in Garmin's lineup.

Before it showed up, the fork often felt like this:

- save money and buy the **S44**
- or jump all the way to the **S70**

That was not a great ladder for golfers who wanted:

- a nicer watch than the S44
- **PlaysLike distance**
- some actual daily-watch justification
- without going full flagship lunacy

The **S50** handles that lane really well.

It gives you the bright **AMOLED** screen, the Garmin ecosystem, the **43,000-plus course** base, **PlaysLike**, and enough health-and-wellness utility that the watch has a job beyond golf.

That is why the full [Garmin Approach S50 review](/blog/garmin-approach-s50-review/) lands where it does. The S50 is not cheap, but it is finally a middle-tier Garmin that feels like a real answer.

## What the Extra Money Buys on the S70

This is where the **S70** still earns its keep.

The flagship difference is not imaginary.

From the existing S70 coverage in the repo, the **S70** still brings:

- **Virtual Caddie**
- stronger premium-watch feel
- deeper advanced-golf positioning
- green-contour and course-management-first swagger
- longer stated golf battery life
- a better overall "this is my one serious watch" story

If those things matter to you, the **S70** is not just a richer man's version of the same watch. It is a more complete golf tool.

That is why the [Garmin Approach S70 review](/blog/garmin-approach-s70-review/) still calls it the best golf watch money can buy.

## This Is Really "Enough Flagship" Versus "Actual Flagship"

The easiest way to think about it:

- **S50** if you want enough premium Garmin
- **S70** if you want actual premium Garmin

The S50 is the watch for golfers who want most of the important experience without lighting another **$200 to $300** on fire.

The S70 is the watch for golfers who know they want the top model and do not want to wonder later whether the missing features would have mattered.

That is a much cleaner emotional split than pretending this is just about specs.

## Where the S70 Can Still Be Overkill

This part matters because the **S70** is easy to romanticize.

A lot of golfers do not actually need:

- **Virtual Caddie**
- extra flagship golf intelligence
- the bigger premium-watch pitch
- the added cost of the 47mm model

They need:

- front, middle, and back yardages
- hazard context
- hole mapping
- **PlaysLike**
- a watch that is good enough to wear when they are not golfing

The **S50** already does that job well.

If that sounds like your actual use case, paying up for the **S70** starts looking more like ego insurance than good buying.

## Where the S50 Can Still Be the Wrong Buy

The **S50** only wins if its balance is what you actually want.
