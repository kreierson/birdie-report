The **Ping PLD Milled Anser** versus **Scotty Cameron Newport 2** argument is the kind of golf-shopping fork people overcomplicate because both putters live in the same expensive neighborhood.

Same basic lane.

Same general buyer.

Same need for a small honesty check before you spend four hundred fifty dollars on a putter that is definitely not going to read greens for you.

This is a premium-blade decision, not a general putter decision.

If you are not already committed to a blade shape, stop here and read [Best Putters 2026](/blog/best-putters-2026/) or [Blade vs Mallet Putters](/blog/blade-vs-mallet-putters/). Those are more useful than pretending a luxury blade showdown is the right place to solve a beginner problem.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Scotty Cameron Newport 2** if you want the cleaner default premium-blade answer, crisper feedback, and the full Scotty ownership/resale ecosystem.

Buy the **Ping PLD Milled Anser** if you want a softer-feeling, quieter luxury-blade alternative and the Scotty identity has never fully charmed you.

The Scotty is the more obvious buy.

The Ping is the more taste-driven buy.

For golfers who already trust their stroke and want the safer broad recommendation, I lean **Newport 2**.

For golfers who know they love the Anser shape and prefer Ping's calmer premium flavor, the **PLD Milled Anser** has a very real case.

## Setup and Shape: More Similar Than Different

Both of these putters are built for golfers who want:

- a compact blade profile
- a traditional address picture
- minimal visual clutter
- feedback that feels more direct than a modern oversized mallet

That means both products make the same basic demand:

you need to like a blade enough that the lack of built-in help does not scare you.

The difference is not really in whether one is traditional and the other is not.

The difference is in flavor.

The **Scotty Newport 2** feels like the most universally recognized premium blade template.

The **Ping PLD Milled Anser** feels like the more understated, slightly more connoisseur-coded version of the same kind of purchase.

## Feel: Ping Leans Softer, Scotty Leans Crisper

This is the cleanest real split in the matchup.

The **Ping PLD Milled Anser** tends to appeal to golfers who want a softer, more cushioned premium-blade feel story.

The **Scotty Cameron Newport 2**, based on Scotty Cameron's official product details, leans on a **303 stainless build**, **dual-milled face technology**, **customizable tungsten sole weights**, and that firmer, more direct Scotty feedback a lot of better players still love.

Neither one is wrong.

But the type of golfer who falls for each one is usually different.

The Ping buyer tends to talk about feel, shape, and subtlety.

The Scotty buyer tends to talk about feedback, craftsmanship, and the fact that yes, they know it costs too much and they do not care.

**Edge: Ping for softer feel preference, Scotty for crisper feedback preference**

## Ownership Appeal: Scotty Still Has the Bigger Gravity

This part is not fake.

The **Newport 2** is easier to justify for a lot of buyers because the Scotty ecosystem is bigger:

- stronger resale expectations
- more obvious flagship status
- more emotional pull
- more recognition from golfers who shop equipment like it is also a personality test

That does matter, even if it is a little ridiculous.

The **Ping PLD Milled Anser** has a different appeal. It feels more understated. Less theater. More "I know exactly what shape I want and do not need the entire Scotty lifestyle package to validate it."

Which one you prefer says more about your buying personality than your stroke.

**Edge: Scotty Cameron Newport 2**

## Forgiveness and Alignment: Neither One Is Trying to Babysit You

This is where you need to be honest.

Neither the **PLD Milled Anser** nor the **Newport 2** is a practical help-first putter.

They are both premium blades.

That means:

- alignment help is limited
- forgiveness is modest compared with mallets
- the buying logic depends on you already preferring this style

## Which One Fits More Golfers?

This is where I stop pretending the answer is perfectly symmetrical.

The **Scotty Newport 2** fits more golfers in this exact premium-blade buyer set because:

- the model identity is clearer
- the buying path is simpler
- the resale story is stronger
- the official current specs and positioning are easier to trust as a known quantity

That does not mean the Ping is worse.

It means the Scotty is the easier recommendation when two products cost about the same and both are aimed at the same type of golfer.

The **Ping PLD Milled Anser** becomes the smarter buy when you specifically want:

- softer-feeling premium feedback
- a quieter brand identity
- the Anser-shape lineage done the Ping way
