This is one of the most natural shoe comparisons Birdie Report was still missing.

The **adidas Codechaos 25** and **Nike Air Zoom Infinity Tour NEXT%** are both aimed at golfers who want a sportier spikeless shoe and have no patience for conservative golf-footwear energy.

But they do not make the same pitch.

The **Codechaos 25** pitch is smarter value, better sale math, and easier walking logic.

The **Nike Infinity Tour NEXT%** pitch is stronger athletic identity, livelier ride, and more obvious "hell yes, those look good" appeal.

This comparison is **research-based** and built from Birdie Report's existing 2026 shoe coverage, including repeated adidas Codechaos sale checks already logged on the site, Nike's current product positioning, and recurring golfer-feedback patterns already reflected in the cluster. No fake "I walked six courses in both and transcended footwear" nonsense.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **adidas Codechaos 25** if you want the smarter buy for most golfers, especially when adidas are discounting them the way they kept doing in spring 2026.

Buy the **Nike Air Zoom Infinity Tour NEXT%** if you specifically want the more energetic, more Nike-feeling premium athletic shoe and you are comfortable paying extra for that vibe.

For most shoppers, I would recommend **Codechaos 25** first.

For the buyer who wants the most athletic-feeling option and knows Nike's fit and style lane already works, the **Infinity Tour NEXT%** still have a real case.

## Walking Comfort: adidas Have the Cleaner Practical Case

The **Codechaos 25** feel like the more practical athletic shoe because the design story is still sporty without demanding that you buy the absolute loudest or priciest version of sporty.

The current case rests on:

- **Lightstrike** cushioning
- waterproof upper construction
- **Twist Grip** traction
- **3D Torsion** support
- repeated sale pricing that makes the shoe feel like a smart decision, not just a fun one

That makes the adidas lane easier for golfers who want one lighter, modern, walkable shoe without spending premium money every time they want something with style.

If walking ease is the main thing you care about, this page and [Best Golf Shoes for Walking 2026](/blog/best-golf-shoes-walking-2026/) should probably be your main shoe reads.

## Athletic Feel and Visual Juice: Nike Still Win the Style Fight

This is the part where Nike stay dangerous.

The **Infinity Tour NEXT%** still do a better job of feeling like a fully committed athletic-shoe answer instead of a golf shoe borrowing some athletic cues.

That matters if you want:

- more underfoot pop
- a more aggressive trainer-style personality
- the strongest sporty look in the premium spikeless lane

The [Nike Air Zoom Infinity Tour NEXT% review](/blog/nike-air-zoom-infinity-tour-next-review/) explains that pretty clearly: Nike are not trying to be the sensible all-around shoe. They are trying to be the fun premium shoe.

If that is what you want, the price premium can still make sense.

## Traction and Conditions Fit: Both Are Credible, Neither Is a Spike

Neither of these shoes is pretending to be a true spike replacement for horrible conditions.

They are both better viewed as:

- modern spikeless shoes with enough grip for a lot of normal golf
- better choices for golfers who prioritize comfort, convenience, and style
- weaker answers if your whole life is dew, mud, and sidehill lies

The **Codechaos 25** maybe get the nod for the better value version of that normal-golf traction story.

The **Nike** maybe get the nod if you care more about lively feel than you do about the raw shopping math.

These shoes overlap, but they still target slightly different people.

### adidas Codechaos 25

Better for golfers who want:

- sporty but still price-aware
- walking-first value
- waterproof practicality
- a shoe that feels modern without pushing all the way into Nike theater

### Nike Air Zoom Infinity Tour NEXT%

Better for golfers who want:

- the more distinct athletic ride
- more visual swagger
- a more obviously Nike-feeling golf shoe

The better question is:

Do you want the **smarter sporty shoe** or the **more exciting sporty shoe**?
