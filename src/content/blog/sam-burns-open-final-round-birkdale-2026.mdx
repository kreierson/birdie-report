**Sam Burns** has the lead, but this thing is nowhere near tidy.

According to **Associated Press reporting published Saturday, July 18, 2026**, plus **The Open's official Saturday recap and Sunday leaderboard page**, checked early on **Sunday, July 19, 2026**, Burns starts the final round of **The 154th Open at Royal Birkdale** at **10-under par**, two shots clear of **Ryan Fox** and **Si Woo Kim** at **8-under**. **Ryan Gerard** and **Lucas Herbert** are one more back at **7-under**, with **Ludvig Aberg**, **Bryson DeChambeau**, and **Jackson Suber** at **6-under**. **Tommy Fleetwood** is five back at **5-under**, and even that still feels annoyingly live with how this week has moved.

That is not a Sunday board built for a victory lap.

This piece is based on **AP's July 18, 2026 Saturday report** and **The Open's official July 18 recap plus the Sunday leaderboard page**, all checked on **July 19, 2026**. No pretending I had a bunker-side credential and a laminated pairing sheet in my pocket.

## Burns Went From Maybe Not Playing to Being the Man to Beat

The most absurd part of this leaderboard is still the life-context piece.

AP reported that **Burns** expected to miss the trip because his wife was due to give birth to their second child, but baby **Belle** arrived early, which opened the door for him to come to Southport. The Open's official Saturday report says he backed up Friday's record-tying **62** with a **65**, getting to **10-under** and taking the outright **54-hole lead**.

That is a ridiculous turn.

And it was not just a putting contest either. The Open's recap says Burns birdied **2, 4, 7, and 8** on the front nine, then added birdies at **14** and **17**. The same story says his combined **127 shots** across rounds two and three are the lowest second-and-third-round total in Open history, beating previous marks from **Fuzzy Zoeller in 1994** and **Shane Lowry in 2019** by **three**.

So yes, the new-dad angle is real and interesting.

But the sharper golf point is that Burns has now turned one crazy **62** into a fully credible two-day takeover.

## Royal Birkdale Still Left Plenty of Trouble Behind Him

If the board were Burns at **10-under** and everybody else at **4-under**, this would be a very different story.

It is not.

The Open's official Sunday leaderboard page shows:

- **Ryan Fox** and **Si Woo Kim** at **8-under**
- **Ryan Gerard** and **Lucas Herbert** at **7-under**
- **Ludvig Aberg**, **Bryson DeChambeau**, and **Jackson Suber** at **6-under**
- **Tommy Fleetwood** at **5-under**

That is why this still feels unstable.

Fox shot a Saturday **62**, matching the major-championship record already equaled by **Burns** and **Herbert** on Friday. Kim is hanging around despite the Achilles issue noted by The Open. Herbert is only three back even after cooling off with a **71**. DeChambeau and Aberg are four behind, which is not comfortable but also not dead in a championship where morning scoring has been available.

And Fleetwood being five back is the exact kind of number that lets everybody keep dreaming without forcing the whole day into a corny hometown-script march.

## The Sunday Shape Is Better Because the Heavyweights Aren't Controlling It

One of the more useful details from The Open's Saturday recap is the one that should make Sunday feel stranger in the best way.

The R&A said **no current major winner is within three shots of the lead**, and that since **2013** only **one player** has come from farther back than that to win the Claret Jug.

That matters because it means Burns is not just playing against generic pressure. He is trying to close out a major with a Sunday board full of players who still smell possibility:

- **Fox** chasing his first major
- **Kim** trying to become the first South Korean men's Open champion
- **Gerard** still hanging around in the best week of his life
- **Herbert** trying to turn Friday's history lesson into an actual trophy

Even the better-known names behind them are not sitting in some classic "Scottie at two back, Rory at three back, everybody else leave the stage" formation.

This Sunday belongs to a weirder bunch than that.

Good.

## Fleetwood and the Crowd Are Still a Real Variable

The straight leaderboard math says **Fleetwood** has work to do.

The emotional math says you would be stupid to ignore him entirely.

The Open's separate Saturday story on Fleetwood says he got within **one shot of the lead** on the back nine before bogeys at **15** and **18** dropped him to **5-under**. It also quoted him saying the crowd has made a "massive difference" all week and that the whole thing has already been one of the most special experiences of his life.

That does not erase the deficit.

But it does mean that if Fleetwood makes an early run, **Royal Birkdale** will sound different from every other pairing on the course.

And in an Open where the board has already been weird for three straight days, crowd force is not nothing.

## Bottom Line

**Sam Burns** starts **Sunday, July 19, 2026** with a **two-shot lead at 10-under** at **Royal Birkdale**, according to **AP's Saturday report** and **The Open's official recap and leaderboard** checked Sunday morning.

That makes him the man to beat.

It does not make this close to over.
