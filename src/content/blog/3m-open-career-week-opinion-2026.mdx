The dumbest habit in golf media is treating the week after a major like everybody should go into a polite little coma.

Nice event.
Nice sponsor.
Nice crowds.
Wake me up when the playoffs start.

No.

The **3M Open** is not dead time.
It is where the **PGA TOUR** season starts getting mean.

According to the TOUR's own **July 21, 2026** late-season storyline package, checked on **Thursday, July 23**, only **three Regular Season events** remain: the **3M Open**, the **Rocket Classic**, and the **Wyndham Championship**. The same TOUR package says only the **top 70** in the **FedExCup** make the **FedEx St. Jude Championship**, and it laid out a bubble that includes names like **Jason Day**, **Keegan Bradley**, **Brian Harman**, and **Tom Hoge** sitting outside the number as the week begins.

That is not transition-week fluff.
That is career-pressure golf.

This column is based on the **PGA TOUR's July 21, 2026 "The Five" late-season storylines package** and the **3M Open's official tournament information**, both checked on **Thursday, July 23, 2026**. No pretending I am in some clubhouse hallway collecting whispered playoff-intensity quotes from people trying to find their coffee.

That is not a sleepy midsummer leaderboard problem.
That is a paycheck, schedule-access, and future-opportunity problem.

## Post-Major Weeks Are Better When They Stop Pretending to Be Lounge Music

The **3M Open's official site**, checked on **July 23**, describes the tournament as an official **PGA TOUR** stop in the **FedEx Cup series** at **TPC Twin Cities**, running **July 20-26, 2026**.

Good.

Treat it like one.

I get why the calendar-brain instinct exists. **The Open** just finished. Everyone is tired. The season's biggest trophies are already spoken for. The temptation is to frame this stretch as a wait room before the playoffs.

But the whole point of a season is that not every meaningful week is a major.

Sometimes the meaningful weeks are the ones where:

- a former star has to prove he still belongs
- a younger player has to stop sliding
- a bubble guy has to find 18 feet of birdie putt at exactly the right time
- or somebody like **Stefano Mazzoli** has to turn a membership move into actual traction immediately

That is still drama.
It is just less polished and more honest.

## Scottie Being Here Does Not Change the Real Story

Yes, **Scottie Scheffler** is in Minnesota.

Yes, that matters.

But the best thing about this week is not that the world No. 1 is around to headline a poster. It is that the week still matters to a huge band of players who are nowhere near his level of security.

That is what makes a tour season feel alive rather than decorative.

A healthy schedule cannot just be "big names at big events, then vibes."
It needs consequence in the middle too.

## My Take

I would rather watch a **3M Open** with people sweating the edges of their professional future than hear another lazy speech about how golf should just fast-forward to the postseason.

This is the postseason pressure.
It is just arriving early and wearing Thursday-Friday clothes.

The TOUR's own numbers say the margins are tight, the names are not anonymous, and the next three weeks can still change who gets playoff access, who gets premium access next year, and who spends the winter explaining what went wrong.

That is not filler.
That is the sport.

## Bottom Line

The **2026 3M Open** should not be treated like empty space between **The Open Championship** and the **FedExCup Playoffs**.

The **PGA TOUR's July 21 late-season rundown**, checked on **Thursday, July 23, 2026**, makes the stakes plain: only **three Regular Season events** remain, the **top-70** bubble is crowded with serious names, and the **top-50** fight now carries even more future value because of the TOUR's coming structure changes.

This is the point in the calendar when the season stops being glamorous and starts being cruel.
That is exactly why it is worth watching.
