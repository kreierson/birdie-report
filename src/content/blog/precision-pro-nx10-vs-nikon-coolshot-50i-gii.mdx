This is a sneaky-good rangefinder comparison because the answer changes a lot depending on the actual price gap.

If the **Precision Pro NX10** were still dramatically cheaper, the recommendation would be easy.

But with current listed pricing sitting around **$279.99** for the NX10 and **$299.95** for the **Nikon COOLSHOT 50i GII**, the decision gets a lot more interesting. Now you are not asking, "should I save a pile of money?" You are asking, "for twenty bucks, why would I not just buy the Nikon?"

That is the whole story.

This comparison is built from current official product pages, listed pricing, and the broader reputation these two models have built in the market. No fake on-course testing diary nonsense.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Nikon COOLSHOT 50i GII** if the price gap really stays this tight. Nikon gives you the cleaner optics story, a built-in magnet, a strong feature set, and current pricing that makes the premium feel pretty damn reasonable.

Buy the **Precision Pro NX10** if you catch it discounted further, if you care about the customizable shells, or if you simply want the more value-branded rangefinder and do not need Nikon's optics edge.

At today's numbers, I would recommend the **Nikon COOLSHOT 50i GII** first.

## Optics and Viewing Experience: Nikon Wins

This is Nikon's lane.

The **COOLSHOT 50i GII** is sold around a simple premise: bright, clear optics from a company that actually knows optics. That is not just brochure fluff. It is the main reason golfers keep bringing Nikon into conversations that would otherwise be dominated by Bushnell.

Precision Pro has improved the NX10's display story with the **DynamicLock HUD**, and that matters. But the NX10 still feels like a value-first rangefinder with good usability. The Nikon feels like a more premium optical tool.

When the money is close, that difference gets harder to ignore.

**Edge: Nikon COOLSHOT 50i GII**

## Simplicity and Cart Convenience

Both of these rangefinders understand one thing: golfers do not want to dig around in a cup holder every time they need a number.

Precision Pro gives you **MagLock**. Nikon gives you a **built-in magnet**. Both are useful. Both keep the device where you can grab it quickly.

The difference is more about overall pitch.

Precision Pro is leaning hard into easy ownership:

- magnetic cart use
- simple slope switching
- a straightforward UI
- customization if you want to make the thing look less generic

Nikon is leaning into premium simplicity:

- strong optics
- built-in magnet
- DUAL LOCKED ON QUAKE confirmation
- a cleaner "buy this and stop overthinking it" vibe

If you just want convenience, both get there. If you want that convenience wrapped in a more premium experience, Nikon has the better case.

## Value: This Used To Be Precision Pro's Easy Win

Not anymore.

The **NX10** still has a legitimate value story, but value only feels overwhelming when the price separation is real. At **$279.99** versus **$299.95**, Precision Pro is not undercutting Nikon enough to win on price alone.

That means the NX10 has to win on preference:

- you like the brand's ownership perks
- you care about interchangeable designs
- you want the more straightforward value brand
- you trust its feature set and do not care as much about optics

That is a narrower lane than it used to be.

The Nikon is simply closer in price than a lot of golfers expect, and that makes it the stronger overall recommendation at current pricing.

**Edge: Nikon COOLSHOT 50i GII**

## Specs That Actually Matter

Nikon's official page gives the **COOLSHOT 50i GII** a **6-to-1,200-yard** measurement range and a listed weight of **7.2 ounces**.

Precision Pro's official page positions the **NX10** as a feature-rich slope rangefinder with a magnetic cart mount, **6x magnification**, and a simpler value-oriented ownership story.

For actual golf use, both have enough range. You are not trying to laser the moon.

What matters more is how these specs shape the buying decision:

- Nikon has the better premium-gear feel on paper
- Precision Pro has the more accessible brand pitch
- both cover the core rangefinder jobs well enough that price and optics do most of the deciding

## Which Golfer Each Model Fits Best

### Buy the Precision Pro NX10 if:

- you find it discounted enough to restore a real value gap
- you want the cheaper option and do not care much about optics nuance
- you like the MagLock and customization angle
- you want a practical first rangefinder without paying for extra polish

### Buy the Nikon COOLSHOT 50i GII if:

- you want the better optics story
- the price stays close enough that the upgrade feels painless
- you want premium without going all the way to Bushnell pricing
- you are willing to spend a little more for a more refined overall package

## Final Verdict

The **Precision Pro NX10** is still a good buy.

The **Nikon COOLSHOT 50i GII** is the better buy right now.

That is the distinction that matters.

If the NX10 drops well below the Nikon again, this conversation changes fast. But at current official pricing, Nikon gives you the cleaner reason to spend the money: better optics, premium feel, and not much extra cost.

So my recommendation is simple:

- buy the **Nikon COOLSHOT 50i GII** if you are choosing between these two today
- buy the **Precision Pro NX10** only if the discount becomes the story again
