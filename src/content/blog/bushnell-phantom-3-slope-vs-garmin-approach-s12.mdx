This is one of the better golf-tech comparisons on the site because it is not really about features.

It is about **friction**.

The **Bushnell Phantom 3 Slope** says: buy the simpler dedicated golf gadget with the bigger screen, cart magnet, and slope.

The **Garmin Approach S12** says: cool, or buy the watch that quietly lives on your wrist and never needs to be parked anywhere.

That is a real buyer fork.

This comparison is **research-based** and built from the current GPS-device lanes already documented across Birdie Report's Bushnell and Garmin coverage. No pretending I spent a month living out of a cart testing existential forms of simplicity.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Bushnell Phantom 3 Slope** if you mostly ride or push, want the cheaper buy, and care more about screen readability and slope than wearing yardages on your wrist.

Buy the **Garmin Approach S12** if you walk, carry, or just want the cleaner no-extra-device ownership experience.

My short recommendation:

- **Phantom 3 Slope** for most riders and value-first golfers
- **Approach S12** for most walkers and simplicity-first watch buyers

## The Bushnell Is the Better Golf Value

The **Phantom 3 Slope** makes a very clean money argument.

For less money than the **S12**, it usually gives you:

- a larger screen
- **slope-adjusted distances**
- a **BITE magnetic cart mount**
- **GreenView**
- hazard and layup context

That is a lot of practical golf help for a lower entry price.

If you ride or use a push cart, the mounted-device workflow is also genuinely convenient. The screen is just easier to see and easier to live with than a small watch face.

That is why the Phantom keeps making sense for golfers who want simple help without paying premium-watch prices.

## The Garmin Is the Better Ownership Experience

The **S12** wins the moment you care more about **one less device** than **one bigger screen**.

That is the whole argument.

With the S12:

- yardages are always on your wrist
- there is nothing to mount
- nothing needs to sit in a cart cubby
- nothing gets left behind when you walk off the green

It is also why the **S12** keeps holding up against more feature-rich products. The [Shot Scope V5 vs Garmin Approach S12](/blog/shot-scope-v5-vs-garmin-approach-s12/) page proves the same thing from a different angle: the S12 keeps winning when buyers want less drama, not more capability.

## Walking Versus Riding Is the Actual Split

Most of the time, this page can be reduced to one question:

**How do you usually play?**

### If You Mostly Ride or Push

The **Phantom 3 Slope** is the better answer.

The cart magnet, larger display, and slope feature set all become more useful when the device has an obvious place to live and you are glancing at it from a cart or handle.

### If You Mostly Walk and Carry

The **S12** is the cleaner answer.

This is not because it has more features. It does not.

It is because the watch format is better when you are moving constantly and do not want another thing to carry or clip.

That sounds small until you actually picture the round.

## Readability Versus Convenience

This is the tension in one line:

- **Bushnell** is easier to read
- **Garmin** is easier to have

The **Phantom 3 Slope** wins on readability because a dedicated handheld screen simply gives the information more room.

The **S12** wins on convenience because a watch is always with you.

Neither of those truths cancels the other out.

It just means you need to be honest about which kind of convenience matters more to you.

## Battery Life: Garmin Wins, but Bushnell Is Still Fine

Garmin's **up-to-30-hour GPS battery** is one of the S12's real superpowers.

Bushnell's **18+ hour** claim is still good. It is just not absurd in the same way.

So if battery anxiety is one of the main reasons you hate golf tech, the **S12** has the edge.

If battery life is merely important but not the whole story, the **Phantom** is still comfortably good enough.

The **Bushnell Phantom 3 Slope** is the better **value-first golf tool**.

The **Garmin Approach S12** is the better **wear-it-and-forget-it golf tool**.

If you mostly ride, push, or just hate tiny screens, I would start with the **Phantom 3 Slope**.

If you mostly walk, carry, or want the simplest possible wrist-yardage answer, I would start with the **S12**.
