## Why the S44 Still Has a Real Case

The **S44** is the better watch experience.

That matters.

Garmin's current S44 product page lists:

- **43,000+ preloaded courses**
- **up to 15 hours** of battery in GPS mode
- **hazard view**
- **Green View** with manual pin position
- **AutoShot**
- **yardage to front, middle, and back**

The watch also gives you a much cleaner hardware story than the Shot Scope:

- brighter **AMOLED** screen
- smoother Garmin interface
- stronger general polish

That makes the S44 easier to recommend for golfers who want a watch that feels nice every time they use it and do not want the ownership identity to revolve around post-round data review.

## Garmin's Extra Golf Features Come With a Catch

This part matters.

Garmin does have more golf depth available, but some of the best stuff is not fully native to the basic purchase.

Garmin's own product details say the S44 gets:

- **full vector maps** with Garmin Golf membership
- **PlaysLike distance** with Garmin Golf membership
- **touch targeting** with Garmin Golf membership

That is not a disqualifier.

It just means the V5 has the cleaner upfront value pitch if you want tracking and game insight without membership creep.

The S44 is better at being a nice Garmin golf watch.

The V5 is better at packing golf-improvement value into one transaction.
