This is a better comparison than it looks.

The **Garmin Approach S44** and **Shot Scope V5** sit close enough in the golf-watch shopping journey that a lot of golfers end up choosing between them even though the products are selling two different ideas.

The **S44** says: buy the cleaner Garmin experience, nicer screen, and more premium day-to-day watch.

The **Shot Scope V5** says: buy the one that actually tells you where your game goes to hell, and do it without a subscription.

That is a real debate.

This is a research-based comparison built from current pricing patterns, official feature lists, and how both watches fit the wider golf-tech market. No fake "I played 40 rounds with both and achieved launch-monitor nirvana" nonsense.

![Garmin Approach S44 golf watch](https://res.garmin.com/en/products/010-03009-00/v/cf-lg.jpg)
*Image: Garmin*

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Shot Scope V5** if you want the smarter golf purchase. It gives you GPS plus automatic shot tracking, includes the tags, and avoids the subscription nonsense that usually ruins this category.

Buy the **Garmin Approach S44** if you want the nicer watch. If you care about screen quality, Garmin's ecosystem, and a cleaner ownership experience more than detailed shot-tracking analytics, the S44 has a real case.

For most golfers, I would recommend the **Shot Scope V5**.

For golfers who want Garmin polish and do not care much about shot tracking, I would recommend the **S44**.

| **Current price** | $299.99 | about $179.99-$249.99 |
| **Display style** | AMOLED | golf-first color display |
| **Shot tracking** | limited compared with Shot Scope's value story | included tags and automatic tracking |

This is why the matchup works.

The **S44** is not cheap. The **V5** is not trying to be luxurious. So the decision becomes pretty clear: are you paying for **polish** or paying for **insight**?

## On-Course Feel: Garmin Wins

If all you care about is what the watch feels like on your wrist during the round, the **Garmin S44** is the better product.

The AMOLED display matters. Garmin's interface polish matters. The broader Garmin ownership experience matters.

The S44 feels like a cleaner, more modern golf watch. It looks better. It feels better. It has more of that "I bought something nice" energy.

The **Shot Scope V5** is more functional. It is there to do a job. It is not trying to charm you.

That is not a flaw. It is just a different priority.

If you want the more premium watch, **Garmin wins this part comfortably**.

## Shot Tracking and Post-Round Analytics: Shot Scope Wins the Important Part

This is the entire reason the **Shot Scope V5** keeps showing up in smart-buy conversations.

It includes the tags. It automatically tracks shots. It gives you post-round analytics and strokes-gained-style insight without asking for more money later.

That matters because golf-tech math gets annoying fast.

Shot Scope's value story is not just "it is cheaper." It is "it tells you more about your game without making you build a whole separate stack around it."

The **S44** does not really counterpunch here. It is more about clean GPS use and the Garmin ecosystem than about deep game analysis.

If you care about understanding your tendencies, compare Shot Scope here with our [Arccos vs Shot Scope comparison](/blog/arccos-vs-shot-scope-shot-tracking/) and the [Garmin Approach S70 vs Shot Scope V5 matchup](/blog/garmin-approach-s70-vs-shot-scope-v5/). The pattern is the same: Garmin wins the premium-watch feel, but Shot Scope keeps punching above its price on actual insight.

## Everyday Wearability: Garmin Has the Stronger Case

The **S44** is still the better watch if you plan to wear it away from the course.

It is not a full luxury smartwatch, but it is clearly better positioned as an everyday device than the V5. Garmin just does a better job making golf tech feel like modern wearable tech.

The **Shot Scope V5** is much more golf-first. It feels like something you wear because it helps your game, not because it is a nice all-around watch.

That makes the buying shortcut pretty simple:

- want a nicer wearable? **Garmin S44**
- want a golf tool with more improvement upside? **Shot Scope V5**

## Value: Shot Scope Makes the S44 Work Hard

This is where the **Shot Scope V5** gets hard to argue against.

You spend less money and get:

- GPS
- included tags
- automatic tracking
- post-round stats
- no subscription

That is a nasty value combination.

The S44 only really wins if you care enough about the Garmin screen, Garmin ecosystem, and nicer everyday feel to pay more for less golf-analysis value.

That buyer absolutely exists. It is just not most golfers.

- you do not really care about deep shot-tracking analytics
- you want a golf watch that feels less like a dedicated gadget

## Who Should Buy the Shot Scope V5

## Final Verdict

The **Garmin Approach S44** is the nicer watch.

The **Shot Scope V5** is the better golf buy.

That is where this lands.

If you want polish, Garmin software, and a more premium wrist experience, buy the **S44**.

If you want the watch that gives you more useful golf value per dollar, buy the **Shot Scope V5**.

My pick for the average golfer is the **Shot Scope V5** because tags plus analytics plus no subscription is still one of the cleanest deals in golf tech.
