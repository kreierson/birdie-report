## New Golf Gear Alert: March 2026 Is Stacked

If your wallet was just starting to recover from the January equipment launches... I'm sorry. March 2026 is bringing some serious heat to pro shops, and a couple of these are genuinely worth your attention.

## Ping G740 Irons: The Forgiveness King Gets an Upgrade

The big story this month is the **Ping G740**, replacing the already-excellent G730 in Ping's super-game-improvement lineup. And Ping did what Ping does best — made the game even easier for the rest of us.

Here's what's new:
- **22% wider sole** than the G440 (yes, twenty-two percent)
- **3% longer blade** for more forgiveness on off-center hits
- **Lower CG** thanks to a shallower face height
- **¾-inch length progression** in the 5- and 6-irons to bridge that awkward gap between irons and hybrids

Early reviews are glowing. The forgiveness on mishits is reportedly absurd, and the aesthetics are apparently a big step up from the G730 — which, let's be honest, needed it.

If you're a mid-to-high handicapper who wants maximum forgiveness without going to a full hybrid set, the G740 should be on your short list.

## Ping i540 Irons: The Player's Game-Improvement Option

Alongside the G740, Ping is also releasing the **i540 irons** — a slightly more compact option for the golfer who wants some forgiveness but doesn't want to look like they're swinging a shovel. Think of it as the middle ground between the G740 and Ping's i-series blades.

## Wilson Infinite 606 & Lakeview Putters: Zero Torque, Maximum Rolls

Wilson is getting in on the action with two new putters in their Infinite line — the **606** and the **Lakeview**, both featuring zero-torque technology. If you've been fighting a twisty putter face on off-center strikes, these are designed to keep the face square and the ball rolling true.

At Wilson's typical price point, these could be a sneaky-good value play compared to the Scotty Camerons and Odysseys of the world.

## Titleist T-Series Oil Can Finish: Because You Deserve to Look Good

In "things I didn't know I needed" news, Titleist is releasing their **T-Series irons in a limited-edition Oil Can finish** — that gorgeous copper-toned dark finish previously only available on Vokey wedges.

This is purely cosmetic, but you know what? Golf is a game of confidence, and if looking down at a beautiful oil can 7-iron makes you feel like a tour pro, that's worth something. Just don't tell your playing partners you paid extra for the color.

March is shaping up to be a great month for equipment junkies.
