The **Sun Mountain C-130** and **Titleist Cart 14** are both cart bags for people who actually ride.

That is where the similarities mostly end.

The **C-130** is the old-school cart-bag king for golfers who want maximum storage and do not apologize for bringing stuff.

The **Cart 14** is the lighter, cheaper, calmer option for golfers who want a true cart bag without turning the whole purchase into a storage contest.

This comparison is research-based and built from current official specs, current listed pricing, and the way each bag is positioned in the 2026 market as of **May 4, 2026**. No fake trunk-loading laboratory work.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Sun Mountain C-130** if your favorite golf-bag feature is "more room than I will ever realistically fill."

Buy the **Titleist Cart 14** if you want the smarter lightweight cart-bag buy and do not need the golf-bag equivalent of a cargo hold.

For the golfer who rides constantly and values storage above everything, I would lean **C-130**.

For the golfer who wants a cart bag that is easier to justify financially and physically, I would lean **Titleist Cart 14**.

## Price and Core Specs

| | Sun Mountain C-130 | Titleist Cart 14 |
|---|---|---|
| **Current listed price** | $325 | $245 |
| **Weight** | 7.6 lbs. | 5.6 lbs. |
| **Top** | 14-way, 9-inch top | 14 full-length dividers |
| **Pockets** | 11 | 10 |
| **Capacity** | 43.7 liters | not emphasized by Titleist |
| **Main identity** | max-storage cart-bag specialist | lighter value-forward cart bag |

The shortest version of this matchup is simple:

- **C-130** gives you more room and a more committed cart-bag identity.
- **Cart 14** gives you less weight, less spend, and less hassle.

## Storage: C-130 Wins Easily

This is the whole reason the **C-130** exists.

Sun Mountain's current product details call out:

- **11 pockets**
- **43.7 liters** of capacity
- **zip-off ball pockets**
- a **Smart Strap System**
- a **14-way top**

That is a lot of bag.

The C-130 is for golfers who want every category of golf junk to have a proper home. Rain gear, snacks, extra balls, tech, accessories, another layer, and whatever random things seem to multiply inside a golf bag over time.

The **Titleist Cart 14** is more restrained. Ten cart-accessible pockets are still good. They are just not trying to dominate the storage argument.

**Edge: Sun Mountain C-130**

## Weight and Daily Handling: Titleist Wins Cleanly

This is where the **Cart 14** punches back.

At **5.6 pounds**, it is a much easier bag to:

- lift into the trunk
- pull out after the round
- move around the garage
- throw on a push cart when your golf week changes shape

That matters more than golfers like to admit because the inconvenience of a bulky bag shows up every time you touch it, not only while it is sitting perfectly on the cart.

The **C-130** is not absurdly heavy for its class, but **7.6 pounds** is still a real difference.

**Edge: Titleist Cart 14**

## Cart-Use Identity: Slight Edge to C-130

Both bags are built for riders, but the **C-130** feels more committed to that life.

The **Smart Strap System** is a genuinely useful cart-specific detail because it helps keep the bag secure without ruining pocket access. The zip-off ball pockets and the sheer volume also reinforce that this is a bag designed around riding convenience first.

The **Cart 14** is still clearly a cart bag. It just feels more like the rational everyday-cart-bag answer than the category specialist.

If you are a near full-time rider, the C-130's dedicated feel is worth something.

**Slight edge: Sun Mountain C-130**

## Value: Titleist Wins

This is why the **Cart 14** is so easy to recommend.

At **$245**, it undercuts the C-130 by **$80** while still giving you:

- a real **14-way** organization setup
- **10** cart-friendly pockets
- lighter handling
- a cleaner daily-use profile

The C-130 may still be worth the extra money for golfers who truly want the storage. But if you do not already know that you need the extra bag, the Titleist starts looking like the smarter buy fast.

That is why the full [Titleist Cart 14 review](/blog/titleist-cart-14-review/) matters so much here. The value story is not accidental. It is the product's whole point.

**Edge: Titleist Cart 14**

## Which Bag Fits Which Golfer

### Buy the Sun Mountain C-130 if:

- you ride almost all the time
- you want maximum storage and a true cart-bag specialist
- you carry extra layers, gear, snacks, and accessories constantly
- you would rather have too much bag than not enough

### Buy the Titleist Cart 14 if:

- you want the cleaner value play
- you care about easier lifting and daily handling
- you do not need every pocket known to golf
- you mix riding-cart rounds with occasional push-cart use

## The Better Shortcut

Ask yourself a less glamorous question:

**Do you hate running out of space, or do you hate dealing with bulky gear?**

If you hate running out of space, buy the **C-130**.

If you hate dealing with bulky gear, buy the **Cart 14**.

That is a much better filter than reading twenty more spec bullets.

## Final Verdict

The **Sun Mountain C-130** is the better cart bag in a pure storage-first fight.

The **Titleist Cart 14** is the better buy for a lot of golfers because it keeps the price and weight under control without becoming a compromise bag.

My read:

- **C-130** for the gear-heavy rider who wants the classic cart-bag tank
- **Cart 14** for the golfer who wants the smarter, lighter, more rational answer
