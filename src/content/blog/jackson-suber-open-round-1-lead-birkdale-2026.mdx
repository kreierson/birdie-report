The first round of **The 154th Open** did not give us the clean, superstar-led major script people expected.

It gave us **Jackson Suber**.

According to **Associated Press reporting published Thursday, July 16, 2026**, and **The Open's official round-one coverage and leaderboard**, checked on **Friday, July 17, 2026**, Suber opened with a **5-under 65** at **Royal Birkdale** to lead by one shot over **Sungjae Im** and **Daniel Brown**. The official Open recap also confirmed that **Bryson DeChambeau**, **Robert MacIntyre**, **Cameron Young**, and **Francesco Molinari** were part of the group at **3-under**, while **Scottie Scheffler** opened with **68** and **Rory McIlroy** labored to a **72**.

That is a much better first-round leaderboard than another boring coronation forecast.

This piece is based on **AP's July 16, 2026 first-round report**, **The Open's official July 16 leaderboard**, and the championship's **official round-one coverage**, all checked on **Friday, July 17, 2026**. No pretending I was strolling the rope line with a weather meter and a direct line to every caddie's yardage book.

## Suber Did the Hard Part Late

AP reported that **Suber**, a **26-year-old** American playing in his **first Open** and only his **third major**, made the round turn real with a gritty par save on the new **par-3 15th**, then added a birdie on **16** and a **6-foot eagle** on **17**.

The Open's own recap fills in the broader shape: he had drifted back to level par at the turn after a birdie-bogey-birdie-bogey stretch, then steadied himself with birdies at **10**, **11**, **14**, and **16** before the eagle.

That matters because it was not one lucky bounce and a scoreboard glitch.

It was a round that got better as **Royal Birkdale** kept asking harder questions.

## Birkdale Rewarded Calm More Than Reputation

The official Open report on **July 16** made clear the course stayed firm, dry, and awkward all day, especially once the wind picked up in the afternoon. It also noted that just **72 players** found more fairways than they missed.

That is the useful context for the leaderboard.

Suber was not the only debutant who looked comfortable. AP said **four Open first-timers** were part of the group at **67**, and The Open's own story highlighted strong opening efforts from **Pierceson Coody**, **Alex Smalley**, and **Ryan Gerard** too. This course did not spend Thursday separating famous from anonymous. It spent Thursday separating adaptable from stubborn.

That is a much more interesting major filter.

## The Big Names Are Still There, Just Not in Cruise Control

The round did not erase the favorites.

It just made them slightly more annoying to predict.

AP reported that **Scheffler** birdied **four of his first six holes** but did not make another for the rest of the day, finishing at **2-under 68**. The Open's round-one recap backed that up and noted bogeys at **7** and **17** that kept him from getting closer to the lead.

**McIlroy** had the uglier afternoon. AP said he missed **three 4-foot putts in a four-hole span** and made **six bogeys** in a round of **72**. The Open's official recap placed him at **2-over**, alongside **Matt Fitzpatrick**, already needing a Friday response instead of a Thursday victory lap.

That does not take either of them out of the tournament.

It does mean the first page of this major belongs to somebody else.

## Brown and Im Are More Than Placeholder Names

The one-shot chasers are not random filler either.

The Open's report noted that **Brown** also led after the first round at **Royal Troon in 2024**, and he said afterward that he now feels like he can actually compete in a major. **Im** made only **one bogey** in his **66**, with made putts from **12 feet** and **20 feet** among the highlights.

That gives the top of the board more depth than a novelty story by itself.

Suber is the surprise.
**Brown** and **Im** are the reminder that this championship already had more live names than the betting-market script wanted to admit.

## What Thursday Actually Told Us

The clean takeaway is not that the stars are finished.

It is that **Royal Birkdale** immediately turned this into a championship where:

- a newcomer could lead
- afternoon marquee groups could leak shots
- and conservative, patient golf mattered more than pure reputation

## Bottom Line

**Jackson Suber** leads **The 154th Open** after a **5-under 65**, according to **AP's July 16 report** and **The Open's official round-one coverage** checked on **Friday, July 17, 2026**.

He is one shot clear of **Sungjae Im** and **Daniel Brown**, with **Scheffler** still close enough to matter and **McIlroy** already chasing from **2-over**.

That is exactly the kind of first-round mess a major should want.
