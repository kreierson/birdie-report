This comparison gets interesting only because the price stopped being stupid.

The **Bushnell A1-Slope** is currently **$269.99** on Bushnell's site.

The **Voice Caddie TL1** is currently **$349.99 MSRP** on Voice Caddie's site, but that same page is also pushing **20% off in cart**, which drags the effective pre-tax number to roughly **$279.99**.

That turns this from a fake comparison into a very real one.

This is a **research-based comparison** built from the current official **Bushnell Golf A1-Slope** and **Voice Caddie TL1** product pages checked on **June 15, 2026**, plus the surrounding Birdie Report rangefinder cluster. No fake "I walked eighteen with both clipped to my soul" nonsense.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Bushnell A1-Slope** if compact carry, USB-C charging, and smaller-body Bushnell trust are the whole reason you are shopping.

Buy the **Voice Caddie TL1** if the **20% in-cart discount is still live** and the real thing you want is the more premium display-and-feedback experience.

If the discount disappears, this gets much easier and I would lean **A1-Slope** more often.

If the discount stays live, the two products are close enough in price that the answer becomes **smallest Bushnell** versus **nicer gadget**.

## Why the A1-Slope Still Has the Cleaner Practical Case

The **A1-Slope** wins on a kind of practicality that golfers often pretend does not matter.

It absolutely matters.

Bushnell's page gives you:

- the **smallest Bushnell Golf laser rangefinder ever**
- **5.1-ounce** weight
- **rechargeable internal battery**
- **50-plus rounds** on a charge
- **USB-C**
- **BITE magnetic skin**
- **PinSeeker with JOLT**
- **IPX6** weather resistance
- **two-year limited warranty**

That is a very coherent product story.

It is not trying to wow you with premium-display theater. It is trying to be the Bushnell you actually want to carry.

If you:

- walk a lot
- keep your rangefinder in a pocket
- hate chunkier devices
- want to charge with USB-C and move on with your life

the **A1-Slope** makes immediate sense.

## Why the TL1 Becomes Dangerous Once the Discount Is Live

The **TL1** is a much easier recommendation when Voice Caddie keeps the **20% off in cart** offer active.

At full **$349.99**, I would ask harder questions.

At an effective price of roughly **$279.99** before tax, the math changes.

Now you are looking at a product page that offers:

- **Pin Tracer**
- **V-Algorithm**
- **Built-in Magnet**
- **Auto Slope Switch**
- **Auto Brightness Adjustment**
- **Dual-Color OLED Display**

That is a real premium-feature stack.

So if your whole reason for spending the money is:

- better viewfinder feel
- more premium presentation
- richer lock-on / feedback experience

the **TL1** has a stronger case than the tiny Bushnell.

## The Warranty Story Quietly Favors Bushnell

This matters.

The **A1-Slope** carries a **two-year limited warranty**.

The **TL1** page lists a **one-year warranty** and a **30-day return**.

That is not enough by itself to kill the TL1. But if the products are only about **ten dollars apart** after the promo, Bushnell's calmer ownership story becomes a real point in the A1's favor.

So the TL1 needs to win on **experience**, not just on having a longer feature list.
