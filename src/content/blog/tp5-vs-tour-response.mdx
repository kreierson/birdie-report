The **TP5** gets the headlines.

The **Tour Response** gets ignored by golfers who seem personally offended by the idea of spending less money on golf balls.

That is why this comparison matters.

This is not **TP5 vs TP5x**, where the fight is happening inside the expensive end of the same category. This is the premium TaylorMade tour ball against the much cheaper urethane ball that normal golfers can actually buy without feeling slightly robbed.

This comparison is research-based and built from TaylorMade's current product positioning, family comparison language, listed pricing in our existing coverage, and the broader premium-ball context already on the site. No fake "I hit both on my home simulator for three nights and the numbers came to me in a dream" nonsense.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **TP5** if you want the real premium TaylorMade ball and you actually care about the extra short-game polish, the fuller tour-ball fit, and the stronger scoring-club argument.

Buy the **Tour Response** if you want the smarter value play, a real urethane cover, and a softer-feeling TaylorMade ball without paying nearly sixty bucks a dozen.

For most golfers, I would recommend the **Tour Response**.

For the golfer who is serious enough to care about squeezing the better full-premium case out of the lineup, I would recommend the **TP5**.

## Why TP5 Still Has a Real Premium Case

The **TP5** is not expensive just because TaylorMade likes nice margins. It still has a stronger performance story.

TaylorMade frames the current **TP5** as:

- the **softest five-layer tour ball** in the lineup
- **low driver spin**
- **mid iron spin**
- the **highest wedge spin** in the TP5 family

That is a very useful premium profile.

It means TP5 is not just "soft." It is supposed to be soft while still keeping a stronger scoring-club case than the cheaper option below it.

If you are the golfer who notices ball feel on pitches, chips, and putts, and you actually care about the difference between a respectable urethane ball and the flagship one, the **TP5** keeps the better argument.

## Why Tour Response Is More Tempting Than TaylorMade Snobs Want to Admit

The **Tour Response** gets interesting because it is not pretending to be a bargain-bin distance ball.

TaylorMade's current Tour Response story still leans on:

- a **100 percent cast urethane cover**
- a **3-layer construction**
- a softer feel
- a much easier price point than TP5

That is enough to make it a very real option for the golfer who wants:

- more greenside potential than a basic two-piece ball
- a softer feel
- brand-name urethane performance
- less financial stupidity every time a sleeve disappears

That is the whole Tour Response case, and honestly it is a pretty good one.

## Full-Swing Fit: TP5 Is More Premium, Tour Response Is More Practical

The **TP5** is the cleaner answer if you are shopping for the best TaylorMade ball, not just a decent TaylorMade ball.

The five-layer construction and tour-ball positioning mean it is built for the golfer who wants the top-end version of the brand's ball story, not the more reasonable version of it.

The **Tour Response** is more practical.

It is built for the golfer who wants access to urethane-ball benefits without making every dozen feel like a minor act of self-sabotage.

That is why I would split it this way:

- **TP5** if you are shopping for performance ceiling
- **Tour Response** if you are shopping for performance sanity

## Short-Game and Scoring Potential: TP5 Keeps the Better Case

This is where the extra money actually has somewhere to go.

TaylorMade explicitly gives **TP5** the **highest wedge-spin** story inside that family. That matters because it gives the ball a clearer premium identity around the greens and with scoring clubs.

The **Tour Response** still has the cast urethane cover, which is exactly why it is worth discussing at all. That cover is what keeps it out of the cheap-soft-ball bin and inside the serious-value conversation.

But if you are asking which one carries the stronger short-game ceiling and the more complete premium argument, the answer is still **TP5**.

That is the point of paying more.

## Feel: Both Are Soft, but Not in the Same Way

The **TP5** is the softer premium tour ball in TaylorMade's flagship line.

The **Tour Response** is softer in the more accessible, value-friendly way that helps golfers step into urethane without immediately buying the expensive box.

That sounds subtle, but it matters.

If you want the ball that feels like the higher-end product through the bag, the **TP5** is the cleaner fit.

If you mainly want softer feel and better greenside behavior than a basic distance ball, the **Tour Response** is already doing the job at a much friendlier price.

## Which TaylorMade Ball Fits Which Golfer?

### Buy the TaylorMade TP5 if:

- you want the premium TaylorMade ball, period
- you care about wedge-spin ceiling and scoring-club feel
- you already know you are comfortable paying flagship-ball money
- you play enough good golf to actually notice the premium-versus-value difference

### Buy the TaylorMade Tour Response if:

- you want urethane-ball benefits without paying tour-ball prices
- you lose enough balls that flagship pricing gets old fast
- soft feel matters a lot to you
- you want the smarter value buy for normal golf life

## The Recommendation Most Golfers Actually Need

Here is the blunt version.

The **TP5** is the better ball.

The **Tour Response** is the better buy for more people.

That is not me dodging the question. That is the answer.

If you are a better player, a lower-handicap player, or just somebody who genuinely cares about buying into TaylorMade's full premium-ball story, buy the **TP5**.

If you are a mid-handicap golfer who wants the urethane upgrade without the premium-tour-ball bill, buy the **Tour Response** and keep your ego out of it.
