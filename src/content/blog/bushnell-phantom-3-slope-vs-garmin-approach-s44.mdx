The **Bushnell Phantom 3 Slope** and **Garmin Approach S44** are a great comparison because they force you to answer the real golf-tech question first:

Do you want the **best value GPS device** or the **better watch experience**?

The **Phantom 3 Slope** is the cheaper, simpler, more screen-friendly answer.

The **Garmin S44** is the cleaner wearable answer.

Both make sense. They just make sense for different rounds and different golfers.

This comparison is research-based and built from current official Bushnell and Garmin product materials checked on **May 15, 2026**. Bushnell currently lists the **Phantom 3 Slope** at **$169.99**. Garmin currently lists the **Approach S44** at **$299.99**. No pretending I spent a month rotating handhelds and watches like some suburban golf-tech sommelier.

## Quick Verdict

Buy the **Bushnell Phantom 3 Slope** if you mostly ride or use a push cart, want the better value, and care more about readability, slope, and simplicity than wearing your yardage on your wrist.

Buy the **Garmin Approach S44** if you walk a lot, already know you prefer a watch, and want the smoother Garmin ecosystem plus the nicer AMOLED experience.

My short recommendation:

- **Phantom 3 Slope** for most value-first buyers
- **Approach S44** for committed watch golfers

If you want the surrounding cluster first, start with the full [Bushnell Phantom 3 Slope review](/blog/bushnell-phantom-3-slope-review/), the [Garmin Approach S44 review](/blog/garmin-approach-s44-review/), and [Best Golf GPS Watches 2026](/blog/best-golf-gps-watches-2026/).

## The Bushnell Is the Better Value Buy

This is the easiest part of the comparison.

For **$169.99**, Bushnell gives you:

- **slope-compensated distances**
- a **touchscreen**
- **GreenView with movable pin placement**
- up to **6 hazards per hole**
- **14+ hour battery life**
- and the **BITE magnetic cart mount**

That is a very solid list at that price.

And unlike a lot of golf tech, the value case is obvious without needing a long TED Talk. It is a golf-only device that gives you the important yardage features, puts them on a bigger screen, and stays cheaper than the nicer watch.

If you mostly ride or push, the [Phantom 3 Slope vs iON Elite](/blog/bushnell-phantom-3-slope-vs-bushnell-ion-elite/) breakdown already shows why this kind of device keeps making sense. Screen size and mounting convenience are not fake advantages.

## The Garmin Is the Better Watch Experience

This is also pretty straightforward.

Garmin positions the **Approach S44** as the golfer's nicer middle ground:

- **1.2-inch AMOLED display**
- **43,000+ preloaded courses**
- **hazard view**
- **Green View**
- **AutoShot support**
- smart notifications through the Garmin Golf ecosystem

It is the watch for golfers who want the Garmin vibe without paying full **S70** money.

That matters because a good golf watch is less about loading every feature on earth and more about being the thing you will actually wear every round without thinking about it. The [Garmin Approach S44 review](/blog/garmin-approach-s44-review/) lands in the same place: it is not the best pure value in the category, but it is a very sensible premium-midrange watch.

If you already know you want a watch, the **S44** is clearly the more complete answer than forcing yourself into a handheld just because the number is smaller.

## Slope Versus Garmin's Cleaner Core Experience

This is the section that will decide the article for a lot of people.

The **Phantom 3 Slope** gives you **slope-adjusted distances** right out of the box.

The **S44** does **not** give you that on-watch slope-distance experience.

That is a real point for Bushnell.

But Garmin counters with a cleaner premium watch experience:

- brighter AMOLED screen
- more course coverage
- better overall watch form factor
- broader Garmin Golf ecosystem

So the decision becomes:

- **Bushnell** if you want more golf value per dollar
- **Garmin** if you want the better wearable system

That is a normal trade. It is also why I would not call either one universally better.

## Screen and Readability: Bushnell Wins Cleanly

This is one of those obvious truths that golf-tech marketing keeps trying to complicate.

A dedicated handheld is easier to read than a watch.

The **Phantom 3 Slope** has the bigger interface, bigger numbers, and less cramped interaction. That makes it especially appealing for:

- riders
- push-cart golfers
- seniors
- golfers who hate fiddly watch screens

If you want quick numbers with minimal nonsense, Bushnell's bigger-screen setup has a very real advantage.

That is also why some buyers should not force themselves into a watch just because watches are trendier. There is a reason simple readable GPS devices still exist.

## Wearability and Round Flow: Garmin Wins If You Walk

On the other side, a watch is a watch.

If you carry or walk often, the **S44** is simply cleaner.

No clip. No cart bar. No deciding where to park the device. No pulling it from a pocket if you are not on a cart.

That is the whole watch argument, and it is a good one.

It is the same reason the S44 sits naturally alongside the site's other watch comparisons like [Garmin Approach S44 vs Garmin Approach S12](/blog/garmin-approach-s44-vs-garmin-approach-s12/) and [Bushnell iON Elite vs Garmin Approach S44](/blog/bushnell-ion-elite-vs-garmin-approach-s44/). Once you know you want a watch, you should compare watches. The hard part is deciding whether you want a watch at all.

## Battery Life and Ownership Friction

These two are actually pretty close on paper:

- **Phantom 3 Slope**: **14+ hours**
- **Approach S44**: **up to 15 hours in GPS mode**

So battery life is not the separator here.

Ownership friction is.

The Bushnell is simpler because it is just a golf box that gives you numbers.

The Garmin is smoother if you are already living in watch mode and want those numbers on your wrist.

Neither is hard. They are just different kinds of easy.

## Final Verdict

The **Bushnell Phantom 3 Slope** is the smarter recommendation for most golfers shopping this exact comparison, because the value gap is real and the core feature set still does the job very well.

The **Garmin Approach S44** is the smarter recommendation for golfers who already know they will be happier with a watch and do not want to compromise the wrist experience.

So the answer is:

- buy the **Phantom 3 Slope** if you want **better GPS value**
- buy the **Approach S44** if you want the **better watch experience**

That is the honest fork. And unlike a lot of golf-tech decisions, it is actually pretty easy once you stop pretending form factor does not matter.
